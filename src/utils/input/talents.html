Talents

Talents are special abilities that enhance your character's capabilities in specific skills. Each skill has its own set of talents that you can learn as you advance in that skill's ranks.

How Talents Work

  • Learning Talents: When you reach certain ranks in a skill (typically ranks 1, 2, 3, and sometimes 4), you can learn talents associated with that skill.
  • Rank Requirements: Each talent has rank requirements. You must have the appropriate skill rank to learn that talent.
  • Stacking Ranks: Many talents have multiple ranks (Rank 1, Rank 2, Rank 3). You typically must learn the lower ranks before learning higher ranks of the same talent.
  • Specialization: Talents allow you to specialize your character within a skill, creating unique playstyles and strategies.

Combat Skills

Magic Skills

  • Arcana - Arcane spellcasting enhancements, battle magic, spell manipulation
  • Mysticism - Mystic spellcasting enhancements, divine magic, spiritual power

Physical Skills

  • Athletics - Movement mastery, grappling, armor expertise
  • Fortitude - Resilience, endurance, survival under pressure

Mental & Social Skills

  • Education - Knowledge application, teaching, research
  • Influence - Leadership, persuasion, social manipulation
  • Insight - Empathy, deduction, reading people
  • Lore - Esoteric knowledge, rituals, ancient wisdom
  • Perception - Awareness, vigilance, keen senses

Utility Skills

  • Crafting - Item creation, modification, alchemical expertise
  • Nature - Wilderness survival, animal handling, herbalism
  • Stealth - Concealment, infiltration, deception
  • Streetwise - Urban survival, underworld connections, social networks
  • Survival - Wilderness expertise, tracking, resource management

Choosing Talents

When selecting talents, consider:

  1. Your Archetype: Choose talents that support your character's role (striker, tank, support, utility)
  1. Party Composition: Fill gaps in your party's capabilities
  1. Playstyle: Select talents that match how you want to play
  1. Synergies: Look for talents that work well together across different skills
  1. Progression: Plan ahead for higher-rank talents you want to eventually learn
