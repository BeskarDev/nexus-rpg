# Designer Principles — Effect Wording & House Conventions

Global stable numbering, never renumber. Index of all concerns: [../designer-principles.md](../designer-principles.md). If a number referenced here is missing, grep this directory for `^NN.`.

14. **Shared rules must be visible at the point of use — never buried in reference pages** — a global rule hidden in a rules chapter gets forgotten at the table. Default for a shared limiting mechanism: repeat the **same short sentence verbatim** in every affected spell (current example: "A creature can only benefit from one Wound-healing spell effect per day."). A **spell property keyword** (the *illusory* model) is the escalation path only when the family grows large enough that verbatim repetition becomes a maintenance burden — and note a property still hides the rule one lookup away, so the bar is high (owner call).
15. **Effect text order: general application first, exceptions after** — declare the basic effect completely, then follow with its restrictions/exceptions as separate sentences. Never interleave a limit into the middle of the effect declaration.
22. **Don't invent rules terms inside a spell — reference existing rules, or add the missing rules first** — if a spell needs a classification the rules don't define (e.g. "masterwork/complex lock"), that's a signal to add the small rules section (locks got a Quality/cost/Difficulty table in gear.md) and then reference its established terms. When no rules exist and none are warranted, phrase the effect in plain in-world language instead of implying a mechanical category. **This includes creature categories**: a spell that targets or detects by creature nature must use the canonical **creature types** from `docs/08-creatures/02-creature-rules.md` (Aberration, Automaton, Beast, Draconic, Giant, Humanoid, Monstrosity, Ooze, Plant, Spirit, Undead — plus their listed subtypes, e.g. Ethereal Undead), never invented flavor labels like "daimon", "incorporeal creatures", or "otherworldly beings". (Owner caught "daimon/incorporeal" on Sense Spirits — corrected to Spirit + Undead.)
23. **No semicolons or em/en dashes in game content** — the game uses natural, direct language. Split into separate sentences, or use "such as"/"and". (Applies to published effect/flavor text; scratch design notes are exempt.)
30. **Refer to a creature or target as "they/their/them," never "it/its"** — Nexus canon uses the singular *they* for any creature, target, ally, or enemy in rules text, even a single one. Reserve "it/its" for objects, zones, spells, areas, and other non-creatures (a door, the slippery area, the zone, a *Wall of Force*). When a spell effect refers back to "the target," "a creature," or "the attacker," continue with they/their/them ("the target grows one Size larger, their melee attacks deal +2 damage, and they gain +1 boon"). This is a wording rule, not a mechanical one, but the owner enforces it on every published line.
34. **Use the house micro-conventions for common rules phrasings** — match the exact idioms published rules already use rather than paraphrasing. The floor on a reduction is written "(min. 0)" in parentheses, not "to a minimum of 0" (e.g. "They lose 1 AV (min. 0)"). A stat penalty on an attack is written as a signed value, "-2 damage with Strength-based attacks," not "2 less damage with attacks based on Strength." An in-effect roll against a fixed number is written "Roll vs. TN 16," not "vs. legendary difficulty (16)" (the labelled form like "Medium (8)" belongs in the Target column of the mini-table, not in the prose). A bane/boon is written as a signed count that "suffers"/"gains": "Ranged attacks through the area suffer +1 bane," never "are made with a bane" or "with disadvantage." When unsure how to phrase a recurring mechanic (a floor, a duration, a die-step change, a per-scene limit), grep an existing spell or rules page for the pattern and copy it verbatim.
41. **Physical is the default damage type — never write "physical damage," just "damage"** — bludgeoning/slashing/piercing physical damage is the unstated default, reduced by AV normally. Name a type in spell text only when it is *not* physical (fire, frost, lightning, acid, radiant, necrotic, blast, psychic, poison). Write "deal +6 damage," "5 damage (ignore AV)" — not "+6 physical damage." Matches published spell phrasing. (Owner set this reviewing the stub batch.)
47. **Every condition/effect duration must be one of the defined time intervals — never an improvised span like "until the start of their next turn".** Nexus uses a fixed ladder (`docs/06-scenes/02-effect-durations.md`), the shortest being **briefly** (a ~1-turn effect). Ad-hoc phrasings such as "until the start of their next turn", "for 1 round", or "until they leave" are invalid — use **briefly** for the shortest, then short/medium/long/very long. If the effect you want is "roughly one turn," it is **briefly**. (Owner flagged "slowed until the start of their next turn" on Sandstorm.)
49. **[Superseded by the `strike` spell property, 2026-07-11]** Weapon-strike spells now carry the **strike** property (`docs/07-magic/05-spell-properties.md`), which encodes the whole procedure: choose a held weapon, attack with the casting skill, spell power replaces base damage. Spell text holds only flavor, restrictions, and riders — worded in attack language ("On a hit…", "On a strong or critical hit…"), never "On a success", so the player is reminded a weapon attack is required. Target column: Special. Historical idiom this replaced: "use your spell power instead of the attack's base damage."
52. **Reference subsystems by their current terms — sweep for renamed mechanics when touching a spell** — rules terminology evolves ("encumbrance limit" became **carrying capacity**, 1/2 Strength + 8, `docs/04-equipment/01-items.md`). A spell referencing a stale term is a bug: verify every referenced mechanic still exists under that name, and when a rename is found, grep all spell files for the old term and fix every instance in the same pass. (Owner caught "encumbrance limit" on Strong Telekinesis; Weak Telekinesis had the same issue.)
53. **A defined keyword is referenced by name only — never re-explain its rules in spell text** — area properties (Constricted, Difficult Terrain, Heavily Obscured), conditions, and other defined game terms are their own single source of truth; a spell just names them ("the gap is a **Constricted** area"), exactly as principle 46 established for the Obscured properties. Restating the keyword's effects inline creates drift when the definition changes and defeats the point of defining it. Principle 14's verbatim-repetition rule applies only to shared limiting *sentences* that have no keyword of their own (the Wound-healing cap) — once a rule is promoted to a defined keyword/property, reference replaces repetition. (Owner corrected an inline Constricted re-explanation on Stone Pillar.)
54. **A rider that modifies "rolls against X spells" must state whether it covers the spell's own rolls — default is "including this one"** — when a spell imposes a penalty on rolls to resist/end a category of spells and itself grants such a roll (save-ends), the text says explicitly "including this one"; leaving it implicit invites table disputes. Owner also prefers such anchor spells flat (no SL tiers, principle 24) with damage baked into the engine as a conservative flat ignore-AV tick on the *failed* roll (Grave Chill model: 2 at R2, +2 per rank on Heighten) rather than cast-time SL damage — the tick can fire repeatedly across the engine's afflictions, so it stays low. (Owner set this reworking Grave Chill.)
57. **All blunt physical impact from spells ignores 1/2 AV — written "(ignore 1/2 AV)" directly, never "crush damage"** — crush/pierce/slash are weapon damage properties; a spell never names them as a damage type. Any spell dealing blunt kinetic damage (crushing pressure, grinding weight, a hurled boulder, a slam, an implosion) writes "damage (ignore 1/2 AV)" like blast, priced roughly one step below the plain-damage column (the bypass buys the difference; e.g. +3/+6/+9 with ignore 1/2 AV ≈ +4/+8/+12 plain against typical armor). Collision/falling damage from forced movement stays the general rule (principle 43) and is never a stacked second bypass on top of the spell damage. Erratas: Telekinetic Crush "+4/+8/+12 crush damage" → "+3/+6/+9 damage (ignore 1/2 AV)"; Rock Throw likewise (+3/+6/+9, Heighten +5/+10/+15, +7/+14/+21). (Owner set this on the Telekinetics P2 batch, then broadened it from crushing-pressure-only to all blunt impact.)
62. **The duration keyword sits directly next to the mechanical effect it governs — never on a flavor verb earlier in the sentence** — write "granting you +1 AV briefly", not "briefly clings to you, granting +1 AV": when the duration attaches to the narrative clause, it is ambiguous whether it bounds the fiction or the mechanic. Effect and its duration read as one unit. (Owner corrected Bark Shield's rider wording in the P3 batch.)
69. **Capitalize only named mechanics — never properties, conditions, or area properties** — capitalized: attribute and stat names (Strength, Spirit, Resolve, HP, AV), skills (Mysticism, Arcana), Defenses (Parry, Dodge, Resist), action economy terms (Action, Quick Action, Movement), trackers (Wound, Fatigue, Focus), resolution terms (Success Level, Target Number), and named subsystems (Durability, Carrying Capacity). Lowercase: weapon and spell properties (quick, strike, curse, singular, crush), conditions (poisoned, marked, frightened, invisible), and area properties (difficult terrain, lightly obscured, dim light, total darkness, constricted). The conditions table's lowercase entries are the model; the area-property definition list was corrected to match. (Owner ruling in the P4 batch; docs, JSON, and definition headings swept 2026-07-12.)
70. **Activation costs come before the effect in ability sentences** — "You can use your Quick Action and spend 2 additional Focus to cast … as a strike spell", never "You can cast … as a strike spell. This costs your Quick Action and +2 Focus." The reader pays first, then reads what they bought (Battle Mage rank 1 is the house pattern). Applies to talents and any spell text granting an activated ability; the reactive-spell trigger sentence ("Cast this spell when…") is the same rule from the other side — the condition/cost frame opens the sentence. (Owner ruling on the Spellblade rework.)
71. **Non-combat reach and calendar-scale spans use the narrative keyword ladders — never combat bands, modern units, or ad-hoc phrasings** — combat distance bands stop at battlefield scale and the effect-duration ladder stops at very long; beyond them, spells use the defined narrative keywords with ancient flavor. Distances (`docs/05-combat/03-distances-movement.md`): **a thousand paces** (≈1.5 km), **a day's march** (≈30 km), **the same region** (up to ≈150 km across), **the same sphere of existence** (the mortal world); multiples allowed ("seven days' march apart"). Durations (`docs/06-scenes/02-effect-durations.md`): **one moon**, **one season**, **one year**; spans under a moon are plain days ("within the last seven days"), and narrative durations sit outside the Extending Durations ladder. Every keyword's definition carries a modern anchor (kilometers/miles, days/months) so the fiction term never needs a conversion at the table. A tether, horizon, or reach written as a combat band (an "extreme range" leash on a scouting body) or a modern unit ("one mile") is a bug — pick the ladder rung. (Owner set this on Possess Corpse's tether and Prophecy's horizon, P4.5 batch; alignment sweep E2–E5 fixed Animal Messenger, Resurrection, Life Echo, Death's Embrace, Pilgrim's Gate, Teleportation Circle.)
