# Designer Principles — School Identity & Completeness

Global stable numbering, never renumber. Index of all concerns: [../designer-principles.md](../designer-principles.md). If a number referenced here is missing, grep this directory for `^NN.`.

3. **Heighten, don't duplicate** — reserve new spell entries for genuinely different concepts.
4. **Standardized reactive defense** — every school's R1 Quick Action reactive spell has the same base ("+2 to Dodge or Parry against the triggering attack"), no SL scaling, plus one school-specific secondary effect.
7. **Arcane defiles, Mystic honors** — even on shared thematic territory (Necromancy vs. Death), philosophies are opposed. Never share spells between philosophically opposed schools. (Genuinely neutral spells between compatible schools can be shared — see per-school notes, e.g. Acid Splash between Evocation and Nature.)
8. **Binding means containment** — Conjuration's binding trait is lasting constructs (wards, cages, dimensional anchors), not movement debuffs.
9. **Every school needs internal completeness** — R1 reactive Quick Action spell, defensive options, utility/non-combat effects, some damage capability, a repeating condition/gimmick identity, and setup+payoff synergies.
11. **No R0 Quick Action healing** — Quick Action healing starts at R1. At-will, action-free healing would trivialize attrition; healing always costs Focus or an Action.
19. **Proposed spells in `docs/analysis/spells/schools/*.md` are stale drafts** — they predate current design directions and may contain errors and false assumptions. Never treat them as canon or "already designed"; override with fresh designs that follow the current principles.
48. **An in-effect attack/skill roll must name the school's own casting skill — Mysticism for mystic traditions, Arcana for arcane disciplines.** When a spell's text calls for a roll by the caster (a bolt, a hurled object, a repeated attack), it uses the school's skill: mystic (Spirit + Mysticism), arcane (Mind + Arcana). Never write an Arcana roll into a mystic spell or vice versa. Sanity-check every draft against its school before finalizing. (Owner caught a Mysticism-school Lightning Storm rolling Arcana.)
63. **Chassis-family variable slots must not converge on one repeated mechanic — and stay inside the tier's power budget** — when three R1 reactives all read "the attacker takes 2 damage (ignore AV)", the owner ruled it boring: the shared chassis (+2 Parry/Dodge) carries the sameness, so the secondary is where the school speaks. But differentiation has two bounds. (a) Limited repetition is fine when trigger and type genuinely differ: War (melee miss, fury lash) and Evocation (melee attacker, chosen element) both keep flat damage responses. (b) A mechanic can be too strong for the family's tier — an Opportunity Attack response was rejected for the R1 reactive set and graduated into its own spell (Counterstroke R2) instead, where each counter still costs the player's Quick Action: granted reaction attacks never bypass the Quick Action economy, and with that cost the concept priced at R2, not R3. Final family: cover pane (Conjuration), +1 AV (Nature), shadow-slip (Twilight), ally-redirect (Peace), on-miss blind (Light — a reveal secondary was rejected as too situational, an unconditional blind as too strong), distracted double (Illusion), melee-miss damage (War), affliction-back (Death, briefly poisoned), elemental damage-back (Evocation). Wording note: a flat damage rider must not masquerade as a game action — "your instant counterstroke deals 2 damage" implies a weapon strike; write it as pure effect ("your ancestors' fury lashes out and they take 2 damage"). (Owner rulings in the P4 batch.)
