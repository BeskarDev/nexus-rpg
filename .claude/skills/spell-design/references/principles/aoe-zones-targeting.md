# Designer Principles — AoE, Zones, Walls & Targeting

Global stable numbering, never renumber. Index of all concerns: [../designer-principles.md](../designer-principles.md). If a number referenced here is missing, grep this directory for `^NN.`.

35. **A conjured barrier, wall, cage, or construct must have concrete durability stats and never be impregnable** — any spell that creates a physical or force structure (wall, cage, dome, barrier, sealed box) states Parry, Dodge, AV, and HP so it can be attacked and destroyed like an object from either side, giving trapped or blocked creatures real counterplay over a few rounds. Never write "cannot be harmed by nonmagical means" or an unbreakable construct. Use *Wall of Force* as the baseline (R3: 10 Parry, 10 Dodge, 8 AV, 40 HP) and scale up with rank (Force Cage R4: 12/12, 10 AV, 60 HP). A single dedicated counter (a *Disintegrate*-class force-unmaking, or a dispel) may still end it outright, but the plain stat block is the default counterplay.
42. **Placed-hazard zones cast against a fixed TN; the entering creature then rolls its save vs. your Resist.** Distinguish two AoE shapes. (a) An *aimed* AoE the caster delivers this turn (cone, burst, called bolts) uses `vs. Dodge`/`vs. Resist` in the Target column and resolves on the *caster's* roll result — the caster's success level tiers the damage (Pyroclasm, Cone of Cold, Thunder Clap). (b) A *placed* hazard zone that persists and catches whoever enters or lingers (Sandstorm, Distortion Field, Earthquake, Reverse Gravity, Fungal Growth) is **not** targeted at a creature at cast time — it uses a **fixed TN in the Target column** (`6 + 2×rank`: R3 = Very Hard 12, R4 = Extreme 14, matching published Fungal Growth) and resolves "On a success, you create the zone…". Because there is no caster attack roll against a target, there is **no caster success level** — so the recurring hazard is **flat, not SL-tiered** (see principle 44). After the fixed-TN cast, resolve who the zone catches in one of **two** valid ways — pick by feel, don't mix: (b1) **creature-rolls-its-save**, phrased "must succeed on an [Attribute] + [Skill] roll vs. your Resist or [effect]" (Fungal Growth, Distortion Field, Reverse Gravity); pick the attribute+skill from the fiction of enduring the hazard (falling/force: Agility + Athletics). (b2) **caster-rolls-vs-defense-against-all**, the canonical flat-control form, phrased "roll [Spirit + Mysticism / the school's own skill, principle 48] vs. [Parry/Dodge/Resist] against all [creatures] in [area]. On a success, [that creature suffers the effect]. On a failure, [that creature is immune to this ability for a short duration]." — a **fresh roll each turn** (not a reused cast roll), no per-turn creature save. The **On a failure → immune for a short duration** clause is optional: use it for a recurring fear/control aura that would otherwise re-lock the same creature every turn (Avatar of War's fear aura), omit it for a pure repeating hazard that may re-test freely (Earthquake). Models: Earthquake, Avatar of War. **SL-damage variant:** when the recurring effect tiers its per-hit damage by success level (Lightning Storm, Storm Cloud, Cyclone), instead keep "compare your casting result to the [Defense] of each struck creature" with Weak/Strong/Critical lines, because there the cast roll's SL drives the damage (principle 44 exception). Prefer b2 for a recurring per-turn hazard (one roll, less clutter); prefer b1 for a one-shot-on-entry check. Either way the Target column holds only the **fixed cast TN**. **Deprecated:** the older "vs. this spell's target Difficulty" phrasing (Web, Grease); putting `vs. Resist` in the Target column of a placed zone; and the old flat-control b2 wording "compare your casting result to the X of each creature… each creature you meet or beat [flat effect]" (Earthquake reworded to the fresh-roll form 2026-07-13). (Owner confirmed fixed-TN-cast on Sandstorm, Distortion Field, Reverse Gravity; set the fresh-roll "roll vs. Defense against all / On a success / On a failure" idiom on Avatar of War and reworded Earthquake to match.)
44. **Recurring zone/aura damage is flat, scaled by rank — never Weak/Strong/Critical.** A persistent zone that deals damage each turn (Wind Ward, Sandstorm, Earthquake, Distortion Field) resolves its cast as pass/fail ("On a success…") and deals a **fixed** per-tick number, scaled only by rank or Heighten — never split into SL tiers. Two reasons: placed zones have no caster success level to tier from (principle 42), and a recurring effect multiplied across many turns and targets makes SL swings explosive. Benchmark ladder from published Wind Ward (R2 +2 / R3 +4): roughly **+2 per rank** for a **utility/ward** zone whose value is spread across several effects. A **damage-primary** zone (Sandstorm R4 = 8) runs a step hotter, since its soft control doesn't carry the spell; a **control-primary** zone (Earthquake R5 = 8, a step under a same-rank damage-primary's ~10) sits lower, since repeating hard control (prone, fissures, collapse) carries it. So weight the tick by how much the zone's non-damage layer contributes, don't apply +2/rank blindly. Non-attack scouring/debris damage ignores AV. Aimed per-turn attacks are the exception — a spell that compares a casting result to each creature's Dodge each turn (Storm Cloud, Cyclone, Lightning Storm) legitimately produces an SL that tiers its per-hit damage. (Owner confirmed on Sandstorm, Earthquake, Distortion Field.) **Generalized (owner, P4.5 batch): every passive damage tick — zone, aura, or lasting single-target affliction (a plague, a rot, a persistent burn of magic) — deals its damage with "(ignore AV)" written inline. No attack roll is involved, so armor never mitigates it; this is the default pattern, not a case-by-case call.** (Owner caught bare ticks on Eclipse and Necrotic Plague.)
45. **The range distance in "in <distance> range of the target location" IS the zone radius — never also state a separate area size.** "a medium area in short range of the target location" double-specifies the size and is contradictory. Pick one: the canonical radius form "in <short/medium/…> range of the target location" (the distance = the radius, per rank-scaling.md), or the other AoE idiom "choose a <close/…> area within range". Not both. (Owner flagged on Sandstorm; also fixed Earthquake, Lightning Storm.)
46. **Sight-blocking zones use the defined area properties Lightly Obscured / Heavily Obscured — never an ad-hoc gloss.** These are canonical area properties on `docs/05-combat/03-distances-movement.md` (Lightly Obscured = +1 bane on sight rolls in/through the area; Heavily Obscured = blinded for seeing into/through/within). Fog/smoke/sand/dust spells reference them by name, exactly as Difficult Terrain is referenced — do not re-explain the mechanical effect inline. Dim Light / Total Darkness remain the light-based parallels. (Owner added these properties when Sandstorm needed a mechanical "heavily obscured".)
55. **One roll per cast — secondary, spread, and rider targets resolve against the caster's existing roll result, never fresh per-creature saves at cast time** — when a spell's effect reaches beyond its primary target (a spread, an arc, a splash, a rider on bystanders), reuse the caster's single roll with the published idiom: "Target each [creature] in [area], using your roll's result vs. their [Resist/Dodge]. On a success/hit, [effect]." (Mass Hold, Corpse Explosion, Insect Swarm, Cyclone, Nightmare Realm — grep the exact wording per principle 34, don't paraphrase into "compare/meet or beat"). A room full of per-creature save rolls at cast time is table overhead the system deliberately avoids. Distinct and still correct: per-turn save-ends rolls to shake a lasting condition, and entry saves on standing zones (principle 42 b1). (Owner corrected per-creature saves on Spreading Contagion, then the paraphrased comparison wording.) **Refinement (owner, P4.5 batch): reusing the cast result is only valid for spreads that resolve in the same action as the casting. A spread that triggers later (a death-burst, a delayed detonation) makes one fresh spell roll per trigger event (the school's own skill, principle 48), compared against each affected creature's defense — still never per-creature saves. A stale roll must not drive an effect many turns removed from it.** (Owner set this on Necrotic Plague's eruption.)
74. **An aimed AoE whose Target column already reads "vs. Dodge/Resist" never restates the comparison in its text** — the effect text only defines the affected targets ("Target each creature in the cone." / "Target each creature in medium range of the target location."); the fuller idiom "using your roll's result vs. their X" appears only where the comparison is NOT in the Target column: a static-TN column (Corpse Explosion targets the corpse at Hard (10), then compares to bystanders' Dodge), or a delayed/fresh roll resolving later (Smoldering Star's detonation, principle 55 refinement). Published model: Negative Energy Flood ("Target all living creatures in the spell's area", column vs. Resist). (Owner caught the redundancy on Psychic Maelstrom, P5 arcane batch; Entropy and Annihilating Wave fixed in the same pass.)
82. **A spell with both an ally/self mode and an enemy mode casts at a fixed TN and reuses the roll's result against enemy defenses** — the Target column carries the static TN (so the friendly mode is never an auto-success), and the enemy mode says "using your roll's result vs. their Resist". Never put "vs. Resist" in the Target column of a dual-mode spell — it would make the ally half unrollable-against. (Owner ruling on Final Triumph and Fate's Decree, P5 mystic batch; the single-roll-carried idiom of principle 74.)
84. **A non-zone utility or self-cast spell puts a Medium (8) difficulty in the Target column at R0–R1 — never the placed-hazard `6 + 2×rank` formula.** The `6 + 2×rank` fixed TN (principle 42) is for *placed hazard zones only* (it scales because the zone's rank is its whole threat). A spell that just needs the caster to succeed at a working — a utility trick, a self-buff, a reshaping — uses the standard table difficulty, which floors at **Medium (8)** and is the same at R0 and R1 (matching published Bestial Adaptation R0 = Medium 8, Flaming Weapon R1 = Medium 8). Do not read R0 down to Easy (6) from the rank-scaling cast-TN column — that column is the contested-cast baseline, not the fixed difficulty a self/utility spell writes in its Target column. Higher-rank self/utility casts step up with the difficulty ladder as their working grows more demanding, judged per spell. (Owner ruling, P7.1 Batch 1: set Thermal Control R0 and Elemental Shaping R1 both to Medium 8, rejecting the zone formula for non-zone casts.)
