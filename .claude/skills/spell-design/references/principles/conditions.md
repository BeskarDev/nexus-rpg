# Designer Principles — Conditions & Their Intent

Global stable numbering, never renumber. Index of all concerns: [../designer-principles.md](../designer-principles.md). If a number referenced here is missing, grep this directory for `^NN.`.

64. **The distracted condition's non-creature sources are deliberate — an unattackable source (an object, a sound, an illusion) makes the condition strictly stronger, and that is the point** — with a phantom source the target's exemptions (rolls against the source, Quick Actions in response to it) never apply, so they are baned on everything and locked out of reactive abilities entirely. Owner confirmed this is the intended strength of rogue-style misdirection (sneaking past a guard distracted by a thrown stone) and of certain Illusion abilities. Design with it consciously: an Illusion spell emitting distracted with an illusory source is delivering the condition at full power, and its rank/trigger should price that in (Illusory Dodge gates it behind a missed attack). Wording: the condition's Quick Action clause reads "in response to anyone or anything except the source" — reactive abilities, not action targeting. (Owner ruling in the P4 batch, alongside the condition-text clarification.)
68. **Conditional payoffs: one rider per key set, keys never overlap the rider's own effect, and no explicit boost against a weakness the type already exploits** — Divine Judgement stacked "+damage and blinded" onto keys including blinded (blinding the already-blind is a dead rider) plus an Undead damage boost (redundant: Undead weakness to radiant already deals 1.5×). Corrected shape: flat damage one step below column paying for one strong conditional rider (briefly stunned) on keys the rider doesn't duplicate (blinded, burning, invisible). Check every keyed rider against its own keys and against the damage type's existing weakness interactions. (Owner ruling on Divine Judgement in the P4 batch; also extracted the invisible condition there.)
73. **Check every condition against its definition before writing it — parameterized conditions carry their (X) value, never a bare adverb** — several conditions are parameterized (burning (X), bleeding (X), marked (X)): a spell writes "suffers burning (4) for a short duration", never "briefly burning" or "is burning". Others are plain states (dazed, slowed, staggered) where "briefly slowed" is the correct idiom. Before a draft leaves review, look up each referenced condition in `docs/05-combat/04-conditions.md` and copy a published spell's exact phrasing for it (principle 34) — a condition written from memory is how rules drift into drafts. (Owner caught "briefly burning" on Smoldering Star, P5 arcane batch.)
78. **Keyed bonus damage replaces the SL column, never adds to it** — when a damage spell deals more against a keyed target (a condition, a guilt state), write a full replacement column ("deal +12/+24/+36 fire damage instead"), never an additive rider ("+4/+8/+12 additional damage"). One number to read at the table beats two numbers to sum mid-turn. (Owner ruling on Divine Retribution, P5 mystic batch.)
80. **Never key an effect on a condition the triggering attack itself consumes, and write kill-riders as "slain", never "reduced to 0 HP"** — marked ends when the next attack against the target resolves, so a spell attack cannot both consume the mark and be gated by it (the key is gone before the rider checks). And a rider that destroys or transforms on death must say "slain by this spell": Elite and Lord creatures refill HP when they suffer a Wound, so "reduced to 0 HP" would erase a boss mid-fight on its first pool. (Owner ruling on Final Judgement, P5 mystic batch.)
88. **High-impact conditions never ride automatic, no-roll triggers** — frightened (and peers of similar impact) must not be emitted by a trigger the enemy walks into without any roll or cost, such as "when a melee attack misses you". An on-miss or on-hit emitter uses a low-impact condition from the school's list (distracted, briefly slowed); frightened and stronger conditions need a cast roll, a save, or an SL gate behind them. A defensive aura that frightens every attacker who whiffs turns a ±1 defense package into repeated hard control for free. (Owner ruling on Phantasmal Armor, P7.1 Illusion batch: on-miss frightened downgraded to distracted.)
