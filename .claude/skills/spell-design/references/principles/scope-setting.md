# Designer Principles — Scope, Setting & Exploits

Global stable numbering, never renumber. Index of all concerns: [../designer-principles.md](../designer-principles.md). If a number referenced here is missing, grep this directory for `^NN.`.

1. **Spells assist, never bypass** — travel/social/exploration spells grant boons and reduce difficulty; they never auto-succeed skill challenges or trivialize game modes.
6. **Traits are thematic seeds, not literal requirements** — an R0 "teleportation" cantrip means spatial manipulation (interact at range, sense space), not actual teleportation. Interpret traits creatively at each power level.
12. **Limits must live in the fiction, never in game structure** — never scope an effect to meta-constructs ("only during encounter turns", "only in combat"). Constrain power through diegetic costs the world can see: concentration, Focus, Actions, duration, positioning, material costs, or daily rhythms. If an effect is too strong outside combat, change its magnitude or duration — don't bolt on a combat-only gate.
16. **HP is encounter-scale only** — HP refills at any short break; it never interacts with rest-scale or travel-scale effects. Night/travel effects trade in the long-term trackers: Fatigue, Wounds, Resolve, Focus. "Regain HP at dawn" is a nonsense effect.
21. **A classic name obligates the classic's core action** — if a spell borrows an iconic name (Knock, Identify, Sending), its effect must actually deliver that spell's defining function (Knock *opens*, not merely "grants boons to open"). Assist-only reskins keep an original name; classic names carry expectations.
33. **Weigh a spell's worldbuilding implications, not just its combat math** — Nexus is Bronze Age sword & sorcery: magic is rare, feared, priest- or sorcerer-gated, never institutionalized. Before finalizing any spell with social, economic, legal, informational, or logistical reach (truth-compulsion, scrying, sending, resurrection, food/water creation, healing, teleportation), ask what happens if it becomes a routine tool of courts, markets, armies, or rulers. A spell must not modernize or industrialize the setting (no CSI courtrooms, no magical telegraph networks, no death made trivial). Three levers keep a spell genre-safe: keep it **rare and costly** (rank, Focus, concentrate, mystic/arcane gating, material cost), make it **constrain rather than extract or auto-solve** (Zone of Truth forbids lies but cannot force answers, so intrigue survives), and leave **in-world counterplay** (wards, resistance, evasion, refusal, social workarounds). When a spell's reach is setting-shaping, add a short GM/worldbuilding framing note (a temple truth-rite, not a courtroom gadget) rather than pretending the implication away. Evaluate this as an explicit balance axis alongside power and theme.
36. **Synergies are diegetic, never rotational — this is a TTRPG, not an MMORPG** — the synergy framework (synergy-framework.md: setup/payoff/extender, cold-cast deficit, shared sockets) is design vocabulary only, never in-game keywords or stack counters. Every combo state must be something the table can see and describe in the fiction (a creature on fire, prone, terrified; slick ground; a hanging ward) and must make sense in the campaign world outside combat too (extends principle 33). If a combo only reads as an ability-rotation optimization, redesign it. (Owner set this boundary when approving the framework.)
37. **No cheap magical safety nets — prefer disturbance-triggered saves over auto-protection** — when guarding a control spell against instant-kill exploits (the Polymorph kill-box: transform, carry the helpless beast to a pit, release), do not bolt on protective magic that saves the victim from the environment on reversion ("reverts to the nearest safe space" feels cheap and unearned). Instead make the exploit *hard and risky*: any physical disturbance of the controlled creature (grabbing, carrying, shoving, damage) grants an immediate re-roll of their save, mirroring Sleep's wake-on-disturbance. The kill-box then remains as a legitimate high-risk strategy with payoff, not a cheap trick — every step of the setup invites the spell to break. (Owner set this resolving Polymorph Others; Sleep is the model.)
38. **If a spell's exploits trace to a reverting physical state, remove the reversion rather than patching edge cases** — when a spell creates a temporary change to the physical world that later undoes itself (a hole that reseals, a shape that reverts, a wall that reforms), the reversion is an infinite edge-case generator: every object, creature, or position caught in the change at the moment it reverts is a new exploit (entombment, kinetic harvest, hold-open, trapped loot, bisection). You cannot patch your way out — each fix spawns the next case, because the exploitable core IS the reverting state. The elegant fix is to eliminate the reversion from first principles: make the change permanent (Part Stone bores a permanent passage — no reseal, so no reseal cases can even be stated), or make it never alter matter at all (a permeability field creatures pass through, so no object can be caught in it). Balance the now-permanent effect through its preconditions (material, thickness, ritual casting, warding immunity) instead of through the reversion. When neither elegant form works and the spell stays exploitable, cut it rather than shipping a patched-but-leaky version. (Owner established this resolving Passwall after the reseal spawned a chain of exploits — kinetic push-out, hold-open, sub-thickness entombment — each fix exposing the next.)
40. **All spellcasting requires a verbal component — speaking words of power aloud** — this is the assumed default across the system (the *Silent Spell* metamagic confirms it: it costs +1 Focus to swap the spoken words for subtle gestures and is explicitly what "allows you to cast a spell while being silenced"). Consequences a designer can rely on: a silenced, gagged, or mute caster cannot cast normally; any effect that suppresses sound (Silent Night, a silence zone) disables spellcasting inside it. **But the block is not absolute** — a caster using silent casting (the *Silent Spell* metamagic, or any future gestural equivalent) bypasses it. Word anti-cast silence effects as "no spell can be cast within the zone unless cast by silent means (such as the *Silent Spell* metamagic)," never as an unconditional lockout. (Owner ruled this designing Silent Night; *Silent Spell* is the canonical interaction.)
61. **The name is a promise — a spell's effects must match what its name leads a player to expect, and vice versa** — companion to principle 21 (a classic name obligates the classic's action), applied to original names: a secondary effect the name doesn't evoke gets cut or redesigned (*Bark Shield* promises protection, so its rider is +1 AV, not a thorn retaliation nobody expects from that name), and a name narrower than the mechanics gets renamed (*Fire Shield* with a flame/frost mode choice became *Frostfire Shield*). Check both directions at design time: does every effect fit the name, and does the name cover every mode? (Owner corrected both in the P3 reactive batch.)
72. **Crossing into another sphere of existence requires a physical attuned object of Quality 6 or higher — planar reach is anchored in a tangible relic, never in pure knowledge or study** — any spell that moves creatures into another sphere (a gateway, a planar step, a summoning that drags the caster across) gates its cross-sphere mode behind holding an attuned object of that sphere: a relic carried out of it, an idol consecrated to its powers, a shard of its substance. Quality 6+ puts the anchor at legendary-item tier, so reaching a new sphere is a story event (the party must first *obtain* something of that world), never a knowledge unlock. The object is a reusable *material* focus, not a consumed cost — the mandatory R5 coin cost stays separate (principle 5). (Owner set this on Planar Gateway, P5.1 batch.)
79. **A standing effect only yields to a dispel of equal rank — the clause closes the effect block as its own sentence** — when an R5 working (a truce field, a consecration) lists "an effect that ends spells" as its counterplay, that effect must be cast at rank 5 ("...but only if that effect is cast at rank 5"); a lesser working does not unmake a greater one. Extract the dispel clause out of the effect bullets and place it as the final sentence after the list. (Owner ruling on Sanctuary Sphere and Sacred Covenant, P5 mystic batch.)
83. **Talent interactions are gated in the talent, never by per-spell disclaimers — the Communal Practices model** — the Mysticism talent Communal Practices multiplies targets only for "a singular mystic spell that targets only a single willing creature" (the willing gate keeps hostile singulars — duel sigils, execution curses — out of its reach), so a singular spell that already targets multiple creatures (Wellspring of Life) is excluded by the talent's own wording; never write "this spell ignores talent X" into spell text (arbitrary-feeling, missable, smears talent knowledge across the spell corpus). When designing a multi-target singular spell, verify the gating talent's wording still excludes it; when designing a talent that modifies spells, put every restriction in the talent. (Owner ruling on Wellspring of Life, P5 mystic batch; companion to principle 27.)
91. **Names and flavor speak in a pre-scientific register — no modern physics vocabulary** — the setting is Bronze Age sword & sorcery; in-world casters do not know "gravity", "kinetic energy", "momentum", or "vacuum" as concepts. A spell's *effects* may model such physics exactly, but its name and prose describe what people of the age would perceive: the earth's pull, weight, burden, the scales of judgment, unseen force. "Gravity Shift" is a modern name; "Alter Weight" says the same function in honest pre-scientific words. The counterweight: player-facing names must stay *clear and functional* first — a purely poetic name ("Burden and Feather") fails the table even when the register is right. Function in the name, poetry in the flavor line and mode names. Meta-terms (trait names like "gravity" in analysis docs) are exempt — the rule governs published player-facing text. Balance against recognizability: classic genre vocabulary (Fireball, Invisibility) stays even when slightly anachronistic — rename only where a name implies a modern scientific framework, and prefer renames that stay one step from the original so veteran players still recognize the spell. Published legacy names that violate this (Gravity Orb, Reverse Gravity) are tracked in SPELL_SYSTEM_ANALYSIS.md Priority 9 for an owner-approved rename sweep. (Owner rulings on Gravity Shift → Alter Weight, P7.1 batch 04 — struck first for modern register, then re-struck as too thematic.)
97. **Water and substance spells never read as a supply source — move, shape, and purify what exists; never imply created substance sustains** — the Bending Spell Effects rule already states spell-produced substance has no nutritious value and can't replace food or drink unless the spell says otherwise. Designs must never imply the opposite: a water spell moves, shapes, or cleanses water that already exists (say so outright when a mode could read as filling from nothing — "This spell moves water that already exists and never creates it"), volumes carry a concrete Bronze Age bound (a waterskin, a large storage jar, an amphora), and flavor text avoids conjuring imagery ("gathering out of air" reads as creation; "water already at hand answers" does not). Desert survival, siege stores, and drought are real pressures in the setting — a cantrip must never quietly dissolve them. (Owner ruling on Shape Water, P7.1 batch 12, 2026-07-16.)
