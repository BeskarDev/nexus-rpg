# Designer Principles — Info-Gathering & GM-Facing

Global stable numbering, never renumber. Index of all concerns: [../designer-principles.md](../designer-principles.md). If a number referenced here is missing, grep this directory for `^NN.`.

27. **Validate a new spell against talents and other subsystems, not just other spells** — before finalizing, check whether a skill talent, folk trait, or item already covers the same job (e.g. Identify vs the *Identify Artifact* Lore talent, detect-magic vs *Magical Sense*). A spell must not strictly outclass a dedicated talent that a player spent a slot on. Match its power and copy its constraints (Quality-scaled difficulty, once-per-target, fail consequences) so the two sit in parity, with each keeping a distinct edge (the talent is repeatable and upgrades; the spell needs no slot but costs Focus). Grep the talent files, not only the spell files.
28. **Ground info-gathering tiers in the real anatomy of what is examined** — a spell (or talent) that reveals information must scale along the axis the target actually has, per the established rules — never a borrowed D&D assumption. A Nexus magic item carries exactly one enchantment (rare artifacts excepted) with Quality-scaled values, so a "one property → all properties" ladder is wrong (both collapse to the same reveal); the honest ladder is general category → the enchantment in full → cursed status/hidden property + any extra enchantments. Curses are a defined subsystem (curses.mdx), so a curse reveal is grounded, split it as Strong "a curse exists" / Critical "exactly how it works." Read the target subsystem's rules before writing the tiers; when a spell exposes a mismatch, fix the spell and the old ability together.
29. **A GM-facing effect must give the GM parameters to move within, never open-ended "the GM decides"** — Nexus deliberately does not leave the GM to invent a resolution on the spot for something they may not have prepared. Whenever a spell's effect hands a decision to the GM (what a divination reveals, what an omen says, what an insight contains), spell out a concrete menu of answer types, categories, or bounded options the GM chooses among. Include a safety-valve option for when nothing concrete applies (e.g. a symbolic omen). This keeps play flowing and never forces on-the-spot creativity. Models: *Divination* (four defined answer types, GM picks), the *Magical Sense* talent (pick a category, GM fills it), Psychometry's Discovery. Contrast the D&D idiom ("the DM offers a truthful reply") which this house style rejects.
