# Designer Principles — Power & Rank Budget

Global stable numbering, never renumber. Index of all concerns: [../designer-principles.md](../designer-principles.md). If a number referenced here is missing, grep this directory for `^NN.`.

5. **R5 material costs match the item economy** — 5,000+ coins (Q6–Q7 tier), never trivial amounts.
10. **AoE trades damage for targets** — half single-target spell bonus at R2+ (R0–R1 keep legacy values). AoE pays off at 3+ clustered targets (150% throughput), never strictly beats focused fire.
13. **Wound healing gates at R4+ for new spells** — Wounds are serious injuries; recovering them magically is a major effect. Existing lower-rank exceptions (Healing Touch R1 ritual-only, Abundance of Life R3) are grandfathered because their restrictions are intense (extended casting / once per day per creature + Action cost). Don't add new wound healing below R4.
17. **Fatigue removal must respect travel attrition** — nightly recovery baseline is 1 Fatigue; travel inflicts ~0–2/day. A spell granting +1 removal already doubles recovery (acceptable ceiling); never scale removal higher via Heighten or rank, or fatigue accumulation stops threatening entirely.
20. **Material cost only when coins actually burn** — the *material cost (X)* property (coin value) appears only when a cast literally consumes coins/coin-valued goods. A reusable focus (mirror, bone set) is *material (X)*, not a cost, and carries no coin value. Coin amounts follow the rank table in rank-scaling.md (anchored to spell scroll costs: consumed ≈ one tier below same-rank scroll; R5 = 5,000+). Most spells consume nothing.
31. **Never scale a spell's tier cap on Heighten** — many control and removal spells cap their targets by tier (affect creatures of a tier up to your Mysticism/Arcana) to respect the mortal ceiling and stop the spell trivializing bosses. That cap is a fixed guardrail, not a scaling axis. Heightening it ("tier up to Mysticism + 1, + 2") reads as an escalating auto-win against ever stronger foes, which the owner rejects. Keep the tier cap constant at every rank and scale the honest axes instead, duration, area, or number of targets. (Sleep, Banishment, Serene Exile all had tier-scaling heightens cut for exactly this.)
39. **Teleportation and long-form travel rank one tier higher in Nexus than their D&D equivalent** — magical transit (teleport, tree stride, planar travel, gate) is deliberately rarer and more special in Nexus's grounded Bronze-Age setting than in high-magic D&D, where such magic is commonplace. When a travel/teleport concept straddles a rank boundary, resolve upward: Tree Stride (D&D lvl 5 → would-be R3) sits at R4, and Teleportation Circle likewise landed at R4. Judge these by in-world rarity, not raw D&D-level math. (Owner set this confirming Tree Stride at R4.)
43. **Falling damage (and other general-rule damage) is never a spell stat — defer to the rule** — falling damage is a fixed distance-based table (`docs/05-combat/03-distances-movement.md`: Close 5 / Short 10 / Medium 20 / Long 40 / Very Long 60 / Extreme 80, ignores AV, causes prone). A spell that makes a creature fall (Reverse Gravity, a knockback off a height, a collapsing surface) causes the fall and lets the general rule set the damage — it does **not** invent its own SL falling damage. Write "takes falling damage for the distance fallen." Same for any effect the core rules already price (drowning, environmental hazards): reference the rule, don't restate or re-scale it. Such a spell often has no SL damage line at all — it is a flat control effect on any success. (Owner set this on Reverse Gravity.)
50. **A spell that grants or rides a full weapon attack adds bonus damage at the half/multi-target column for its rank, not the single-target column.** The weapon attack itself already supplies roughly single-target-spell-equivalent damage (base ½-attribute + weapon damage ×SL-multiplier 1/2/3), so a full single-target rider double-dips — at R3 a full +6/12/18 rider beat a pure R3 nuke at every tier *and* still kept the weapon damage. Use the half column (R3 = +4/8/12). This is distinct from the two other weapon-spell budgets: **enchant-weapon** spells run a small sustained flat rider (+2 → +4/+6 per hit over a duration), and **instant-strike** spells (Mighty/Oaken Strike) add no bonus at all (pure spell-power-for-base-damage swap). Alpha-strike stacking of multiple flat per-hit buffs onto one crit is real but Focus/action/HP-economy-gated and inherent to buff-stacking — not a per-spell fault. (Owner flagged on Glorious Strike.)
51. **At-will (R0) spells never trigger Durability checks or any other attrition subsystem** — an unlimited-use cantrip with an equipment-degradation rider grinds gear to dust for free (spam every turn, no Focus cost). R0 riders stay weak and static (Acid Splash's "ignore 1/2 AV" is the model — intentional, keep it). Durability pressure, supply drain, and similar attrition riders start at Focus-costed ranks (R1+), where each cast has a real price. This is the attrition sibling of principle 11 (no R0 Quick Action healing). (Owner rejected the proposed Acid Splash durability rework at R0 for exactly this.)
56. **Psychic damage always ignores all AV and therefore prices at the half column** — published ladder: Mind Blast R0 +0/+2/+4, Attack Thoughts R1 +2/+4/+6, Psychic Wave R2 cone +2/+4/+6, Death's Door R3 +4/+8/+12. Ignoring all AV is worth roughly the other half of the damage against armored targets, so psychic single-target spells use the multi-target column values (R0 subtracts 2 flat), and psychic AoE at low ranks discounts a step further (Psychic Wave R2 +2/+4/+6). Refinement (owner, P4 batch): the extra AoE step-down is not automatic at higher ranks — the R4 flagship Phantasmal Catastrophe runs the multi column directly (+5/+10/+15, ignore AV) with a conditional escalation rider; judge by the spell's whole package. Condition riders on top follow the normal deviation trades. Never write psychic at the full single-target column. (Owner caught Psychic Lance at one-step-below instead of half.)
58. **Multi-hit spells price each separately-rolled hit at the half column** — a spell firing several rolled hits that can stack on one target (Scorching Ray, Lightning Volley) uses multi-target column values per hit: focused fire then peaks at ~150% of a single-target spell across the rolls, the same premium AoE earns at 3+ clustered targets, paid in per-hit variance. Adjacency-capped hits that cannot stack (Lightning Arc, Hale of Blades) may run ~66–75% per hit. Benchmark: Lightning Volley R3 at +4/+8/+12 per ray. (Owner-approved in the 2026-07 multi-target scaling audit; Scorching Ray's Heighten was corrected to this.)
59. **A standing effect that attacks again on later turns states its action cost — an Action by default** — repeat-attack engines (Living Plants, Insect Swarm, Storm Cloud, Cyclone) explicitly cost the caster's Action to re-fire; free re-fires are an R5-tier privilege (Lightning Storm). Movement of the zone/swarm may stay a Quick Action or free rider. Never leave the re-fire cost unstated. (Owner-approved in the 2026-07 audit.)
60. **Blast damage always ignores 1/2 AV and every blast effect writes the annotation "(ignore 1/2 AV)" inline** — the annotation is the rule at the table (no rules-chapter definition exists); an unannotated "blast damage" is an oversight, not a variant. Sweep new text for bare "blast damage". (Owner confirmed on Gust/Lightning Step in the 2026-07 audit.)
65. **R4+ self-transformation spells are ability suites that run the school's engine themselves — never a stacked flat damage rider** — the benchmark is Avatar of Storms (fly, temp HP, resistances, retaliation, damage affinity, the recast-at-duration-end clause): a transformation earns its rank with several distinct abilities, at least one of which both *emits* and *spends* the school's synergy state on its own (Blood Frenzy inflicts bleeding on strong/critical hits, and its hits on bleeding targets increase the bleeding value by 2 — an escalating payoff, valid because bleeding already triggers on every source of damage (a miss-trigger clause was cut for that reason), with the condition's own save-ends and bandage rolls as counterplay). Passive flat damage riders on sustained buffs are nova-combo fuel and were rejected even at R4, and so was HP regen that refunds the transformation's own per-turn blood price (a self-cost must stay a real cost, principle 12 spirit). Kill/threshold riders that spread a condition are tier-capped to the creature that triggered them (fear from a slain minion tests minions, never the boss). (Owner rulings reworking Blood Frenzy in the P4 batch.)
66. **A ±1 bane/boon package is R1–R2 tier — an R4 single-target control spell needs several interacting teeth** — Champion's Challenge drafted as "+1 bane vs others, +1 boon for you, conditional fear, save-ends" was ruled an R2 package at R4 cost; benchmark R4 spells reshape fights (Wall of Fire owns territory, Blood Frenzy transforms, Finger of Death nearly kills). The upgrade pattern for a high-rank duel/mark: keep the soft modifiers, add **punishment** (breaking the constraint costs flat ignore-AV damage, not just a bane) and **isolation** (cut the target off from ally-granted boons within the constraint's scope). Calibrate rank by what the spell does to the shape of the fight, not by its modifier count. (Owner ruling on Champion's Challenge in the P4 batch.)
67. **Life is the healing ceiling — no school's healing may be more focused or more powerful than Life's at the same rank** — other schools get healing effects per their role spread, but every non-Life heal must be strictly worse than Life's same-rank option in at least one axis: a real cost (Redemptive Sacrifice pays half the healing in the caster's own HP), a narrow trigger (Final Triumph's kill gate), or a lower rate (multi-target/Quick chassis). Two obligations when designing one: (a) never exceed the healing chassis of §16/rank-scaling, and (b) verify Life's own ladder is actually published at that rank first — if Life's chassis heal hasn't cascaded there yet, extend Life's Heighten in the same batch (Healing Touch's R4/R5 extension shipped alongside Redemptive Sacrifice for exactly this). (Owner ruling in the P4 batch.)
75. **R5 coin cost is set by the spell's mechanical tempo, not its fictional grandeur** — a spell whose effect ends with the scene or with concentration is combat-class and carries no material cost (Focus 10 plus the Legendary casting bar is its price), however world-shaking it looks in the fiction. Dawnbreak calls a false dawn seen for miles yet pays no coin, because the light ends when concentration does. Only a spell that performs a lasting rite whose result outlives the casting (a consecration, a resurrection, a land-healing, a journey blessing, a rest-rite) is rite-class and pays the mandatory R5 material cost (principle 5). Ignore the scattered "material cost 5000+" notes in stale analysis seeds (principle 19) and apply this test instead. (Owner ruling in the P5 mystic batch, resolving Dawnbreak and Ancestral Convergence to no-cost against their seeds.)
76. **Rest-acceleration effects shorten a rest, they never grant an extra one** — a spell that speeds recovery (a dream-rest taken in an hour, a restorative trance) lets a creature take their normal rest faster, but must be capped so it cannot bank a second full rest inside the same day. Word it explicitly: "a creature can gain the benefit of a night's rest only once in a day, whether taken in full or through this effect." Otherwise it breaks the Fatigue and Wound attrition economy (principles 16, 17) by refilling the long-term trackers on demand. (Owner ruling on Dream Realm, P5 mystic batch.)
77. **A granted immunity must have a circumvention — the house form is that acting aggressively drops it to resistance** — a spell or form that grants immunity to a damage type (Shadowform: immune to physical) must leave enemies a window, or it becomes an invincible shell. The house pattern: making an attack forces the creature to partially materialize, dropping the immunity to mere resistance until its next turn, so committing to offense opens it to reprisal. Prefer this diegetic tradeoff over a flat duration cap alone. (Owner ruling on Shadowform, P5 mystic batch.)
81. **Rest-granting magic tops out at a night's rest — a full rest belongs to safe havens only** — the full rest (all Fatigue removed, every Wound rolled) is the escalation tier bound to a day and night at a comfortable safe site such as an inn; that safe-site requirement is the point of the tier, and no spell may replace the inn with a rite in the wilderness. A rest-granting spell says explicitly that it never counts as a full rest. Extras beyond the night's rest are fine as riders (Dream Realm grants +1 additional Resolve on top). (Owner ruling on Dream Realm, P5 mystic batch; companion to principle 76.)
85. **A flat "X damage" tick (no "+") still receives flat spell-damage modifiers such as the Spell Catalyst bonus — it only forgoes Spell Power.** Per `docs/07-magic/01-magic-spells/index.md`: "+X damage" adds Spell Power as a base, while "X damage" without the "+" uses "only the listed value and any other applicable modifiers." The Spell Catalyst's flat +1/+2/+3/+4 spell-damage bonus (`docs/04-equipment/07-magic-items/effects.md`) is such a modifier, so it **does** apply to a flat zone/aura/affliction tick (Acid Rain's "2 acid damage", Grave Chill's failed-save tick, principles 44/54); only the Spell-Power base is excluded by dropping the "+". For any bonus that would otherwise scale by Success Level, treat the no-SL tick as a permanent **weak** success. This is already RAW — do not restate it in spell text (principle 53); it is documented here so flat-tick spells are costed knowing the catalyst rides them. (Owner clarification, P7.1 Batch 1, confirming Acid Rain.)
