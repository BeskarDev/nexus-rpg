# Designer Principles — Success Levels & Heighten

Global stable numbering, never renumber. Index of all concerns: [../designer-principles.md](../designer-principles.md). If a number referenced here is missing, grep this directory for `^NN.`.

2. **SL scales magnitude, not effect type** — the primary utility/control effect (condition, duration, target count) is identical on any success. Only damage, healing, and *secondary* effects scale with Success Level. A control spell applying "dazed" on Weak still applies "dazed" on Critical — the Critical adds bonus damage or a secondary debuff.
18. **Heighten upscales, never transforms** — Heighten entries may only increase existing magnitudes (damage, healing, target count, range, duration, reach/timeframe). A genuinely new capability (new mode of use, new sense, new interaction) is a new spell, not a Heighten entry.
24. **A flat single-success effect is often the correct choice, especially for utility** — not every spell needs Weak/Strong/Critical tiers. When a spell's value is binary (a door is locked or it isn't, you understand a language or you don't), give it one effect that happens on any success. Predictability is a feature: the player knows a successful cast works as intended. Only add SL tiers when there is a genuine *potency* axis to scale (damage, healing, duration of a condition, amount of information divined). Reserve this for effects where "more" is meaningful.
25. **Optional modifiers belong on Heighten, never on Success Level** — a binary feature that is unrelated to how well you cast (a hidden sigil, a damage backlash, an added sense) must never be gated behind Strong/Critical, because SL is not the player's choice and such features are not "potency." If a spell should offer such a modifier, make it a Heighten option the player actively pays for and chooses. This is the corollary to principle 2: SL scales potency, Heighten grants options.
26. **Heighten cascades to R5 by default, unless the scaling axis is genuinely exhausted** — a Heighten entry normally provides a benefit at every rank above the base up to rank 5. **Use the published house idiom: explicit per-rank lines, one per rank, each phrased "…instead"** — `(Rank 3) … instead. (Rank 4) … instead. (Rank 5) … instead.` Do NOT use the "For each additional rank of Focus spent, …" formula phrasing; it reads clunky and does not match any published spell (write out each rank's line). For a menu-style Heighten, escalate the count of choices per rank: `(Rank 3) choose one of the benefits below. (Rank 4) choose two instead. (Rank 5) choose three instead.` Spells that merely stop at R3/R4 out of old habit are legacy and should be extended when touched. **Exception**: some effects have a natural ceiling the scaling reaches before R5 (e.g. a timeframe that reaches "the object's entire history" — there is nowhere further to scale). Then the Heighten legitimately caps at that rank. Do NOT pad it to R5 by bolting on an unrelated effect just to fill ranks — that violates principle 25. A limited, honest cascade beats a padded one. (Psychometry's Legend Lore heighten caps at R4 for exactly this reason.)
87. **Rank 0 spells can never be heightened** — a rank 0 spell is always cast at rank 0 and never carries a Heightened block. Cantrips are at-will and their power is fixed at the cantrip chassis; Heightening exists for Focus-costed spells. If a rank 0 concept has a meaningful scaling axis, that scaled version is a separate higher-rank spell (or the concept belongs at R1+ in the first place), never a Heighten entry on the cantrip. Owner ruling 2026-07-15 (P7.1 Illusion batch review: Phantom Feint's marked-value cascade and Gravity Shift's duration heighten both struck for this reason).
32. **Every Heighten rank must be self-contained, restating all inherited effects, not just the delta** — in a cumulative cascade a reader who casts at rank N reads only that rank's line, so it must list the full state at that rank, not the one thing that changed since rank N-1. Wrong: "(R3) lasts a medium duration. (R4) also covers a short area" (a reader could miss that R4 still includes the medium duration). Right: "(R3) lasts a medium duration instead. (R4) lasts a medium duration and covers a short area instead." This is why the "…instead" idiom works, each line replaces the base wholesale. Only genuinely independent single-rank heightens (one line, nothing to inherit) are exempt.
