# ✨ Resolve

![banner-img](/img/banner/resolve-banner.png)

> "What keeps a person moving when the blade breaks and the odds are gone? Not gods. Not luck. The thing in their chest that refuses to stop: the drive that first sent them out the door, and the flaw that makes them too stupid or too proud to turn back."

Resolve represents the sheer force of an adventurer's will, the tension between their sharpest drives and their worst instincts. You can have a maximum of 3 Resolve at any time.

## Using Resolve

You can spend 1 Resolve to re-roll the result of one test and take the new result.

> Example: Arzhag spends 1 Resolve to re-roll a failed Strength test when attempting to move a fallen pillar blocking the party's escape route.

## Regaining Resolve

**You regain Resolve through certain actions and decisions:**

- Regain 1 or more Resolve at the start of an adventure after at least one week of downtime
- Regain 1 Resolve after a comfortable rest without interruptions
- Gain +1 Resolve when you invoke your Motivation or Vice during a skill test (see below; once per session per trait)
- The GM can grant every adventurer +1 Resolve for an especially creative solution (once per gaming session)

You only regain Resolve passively if you had less remaining. For example, when regaining 1 Resolve after a comfortable rest, you only do so if you had 0 Resolve left. The exception is Resolve gained through your Motivation, Vice, or the GM, which is always added to your total (max. 3).

> Example: After infiltrating the forbidden archives, where Sansar used 1 Resolve to re-roll a Stealth test, the party rests for the night. At the end of the rest, Sansar regains 1 Resolve because they had 0 remaining. If they hadn't spent their Resolve, they wouldn't have regained any.

## Invoking Your Motivation

Once per session, you can invoke your Motivation before making a roll. Have your character say something, make a decision, or act in a way that makes the Motivation visible to everyone at the table, then tell the GM how it connects to this moment. If they agree it fits, you gain **+1 Resolve immediately** before the roll. You can spend it on that roll right away or keep it for later. Your Motivation can change after a major story event; discuss it with the GM and replace it.

> Example: Sephi (Motivation: **Knowledge**) spots a sealed chamber covered in unknown script. Her player has her push past the others, press her hands against the door, and say: "I have spent six years looking for texts from this period. We are not leaving until I know what is written here." The player then tells the GM: "That is Knowledge — I want to invoke it." The GM agrees. Sephi gains +1 Resolve before she rolls Lore, and can spend it on that roll or save it.

## Invoking Your Vice

Your Vice can enter play in two ways. **You** can invoke it once per session: when your flaw is visibly driving what you are doing, describe how to the GM. If they agree, gain **+1 Resolve immediately** before making your roll, which you can spend on that roll or save. **The GM** can also invoke it: when they frame a scene where your Vice would credibly compel action, they may offer +1 Resolve in exchange for acting on it. You can accept and indulge, or decline and simply forgo the Resolve.

> Example: The party has just secured a deal with a merchant and is about to leave. The GM turns to Nagkhan's player: *"Before you go, you notice the merchant casually mention he is having the tablets in the back room destroyed tomorrow — they are taking up space. You know those are exactly what you have been looking for. Acting on your Obsession and making a scene to stop him would get you +1 Resolve, but it might cost the group goodwill they just earned."* Nagkhan's player has them step back into the room and demand the merchant reconsider, voice rising. The group's hard-won rapport frays. Nagkhan gains +1 Resolve. If the player had declined, they simply forgo the Resolve and the moment passes.
