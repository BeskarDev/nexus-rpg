# Streetwise

Name | Description
--- | ---
**City's Pulse**  | <strong>(Rank 1)</strong> When you roll Streetwise to navigate a settlement's streets, read the mood of its people, or deal with its authorities and gatekeepers, you can re-roll the test once per scene.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 2)</strong> Before you interact with an NPC in a settlement you have visited before, you can spend a delving turn (some minutes) asking around about them. Roll Spirit + Streetwise vs. a TN based on the NPC's prominence (TN 8 for common folk, TN 10 for notable figures, TN 12 for rulers and other powerful figures). On a success, the GM reveals one of the NPC's Motivations (a desire, fear, bond, or value they hold) or Pitfalls (something that offends or alarms them), GM's choice. You can use this ability once per NPC.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 3)</strong> Once per scene, when you succeed on a Streetwise roll to get something done in a settlement by leaning on someone you know locally, you gain +1 boon on your next roll toward the same goal. If you are resolving a Challenge whose pace allows for reaching out to a contact, such as a Research or an Investigation, you can instead reduce that challenge's die by an additional 1. This doesn't apply during a Social Intrigue or a fast-moving Challenge such as a Chase.
**Forger**  | <strong>(Rank 1)</strong> You can create false documents. Choose one of the following categories, matching the complexity of what you want to forge, and pay its cost in writing materials, wax, and other supplies:<br/>- <strong>Common document</strong> (a letter of introduction, a travel pass, a simple permit). 10 coins. One exploration turn (some hours) of work.<br/>- <strong>Official document</strong> (a trade permit, guild papers, a contract). 50 coins. One exploration turn of work. Suffer +1 bane on your roll.<br/>- <strong>Noble or royal document</strong> (a lord's seal, a royal decree, letters patent). 250 coins. Two exploration turns of work. Suffer +1 bane on your roll.<br/>Forging a document requires a genuine example of the document type to work from. Roll Mind + Streetwise. The result of your roll becomes the TN for any creature to identify the document as false with a Perception, Education, or Insight roll. An official who checks the document against records or with its supposed issuer uncovers the forgery automatically.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 2)</strong> You gain the following abilities:<br/>- You can reproduce a specific person's handwriting or seal if you have a sample to work from. Creatures who know that person's hand or seal well gain +1 boon on rolls to identify such a document as false.<br/>- Once per scene, when you present one of your forged documents and it supports your case, you gain +1 boon on your next roll toward the same goal. If this happens during a Social Intrigue, the NPC's Interest increases by 1 instead.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 3)</strong> You gain the following abilities:<br/>- You gain +1 boon on rolls to create forged documents. You can also spend one delving turn (some minutes) instead of your category's normal time to forge a document, but suffer an additional +1 bane on the roll when you do.<br/>- Once per adventure, one forged document of your choice automatically holds up the first time anyone attempts to detect it as false, whether by a roll or by checking it against records or its supposed issuer. Any further attempt resolves as normal.
**I Know A Guy**  | <strong>(Rank 1)</strong> During downtime in a settlement you have previously used the Carouse activity in, you can forgo using Buy Item, Sell Item, or Find Contact. Instead, roll Spirit + Streetwise. On a success, you know somebody related to what you are looking for but the results are more unpredictable.<br/><strong>Weak.</strong> Your contact holds a grudge against you from your last interaction. They can get you what you are looking for but have a major request in return.<br/><strong>Strong.</strong> Your contact can get you what you are looking for but with a small request in return.<br/><strong>Critical.</strong> Your contact can get you what you are looking for without any additional requests in return.<br/>Can be used once between adventures.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 2)</strong> When you use your I Know A Guy ability, you can also ask your contact about one specific person, faction, or topic. The GM gives you information based on the SL of your roll.<br/><strong>Weak.</strong> A rumor or partial information.<br/><strong>Strong.</strong> Reliable information.<br/><strong>Critical.</strong> Detailed insider knowledge.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 3)</strong> Once per adventure, you can use your I Know A Guy ability in a settlement you have never visited before. Roll as normal, but treat the SL of your roll as one step lower (minimum weak success).
**Jack of All Trades**  | <strong>(Rank 1)</strong> You can choose to add your Streetwise to any roll instead of another skill (except for magic skills) once per scene.<br/><strong><br/>(Rank 2)</strong> After you have used your Jack of All Trades ability to replace a skill and succeeded on the roll, you can continue to add your Streetwise to rolls instead of that same skill for the rest of the scene.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 3)</strong> After you have used your Jack of All Trades ability to replace a skill and succeeded on the roll, you can use it again during the same scene for another skill.
**Road Warden**  | <strong>(Rank 1)</strong> You gain the following abilities:<br/>- During travel, when the party stops at a safe waypoint, such as an inn, farmstead, or friendly settlement, you can roll Spirit + Streetwise vs. the travel difficulty. On a success, choose one: anything the party buys here costs 10% less, or you learn where the next such stop along your route lies and one useful fact about it, such as how far off it is, whether it is safe or troubled, or what supplies or services it offers.<br/>- You gain +1 boon on Influence and Streetwise rolls toward travelers, patrols, and gatekeepers the party meets on the road or at a border.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 2)</strong> You gain the following abilities:<br/>- When enemies try to surprise the party while traveling or camping, you can roll Spirit + Streetwise vs. the travel difficulty. On a success, you sense the ambush in time and the party isn't surprised.<br/>- When the party runs into a hostile encounter while traveling, you can roll Spirit + Streetwise vs. the travel difficulty. On a success, you notice the danger early and the party can choose to avoid it entirely at the cost of 1 progress.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 3)</strong> You gain the following abilities:<br/>- While you are the Navigator and the party travels along a road, you gain +1 boon on your Navigation rolls.<br/>- Once per adventure, when the road ahead is blocked, such as by a toll gate, patrol, or barricade, you can roll Spirit + Streetwise vs. the travel difficulty. On a success, you talk, bluff, or bribe your way through, and the party passes without a detour or a fight.
**Size Up**  | <strong>(Rank 1)</strong> Once per scene, you can use your Quick Action to quickly size up a room and everyone present in it. Roll Mind + Streetwise.<br/><strong>Weak.</strong> Choose one of the following to learn.<br/><strong>Strong.</strong> Choose two.<br/><strong>Critical.</strong> Learn all three.<br/>- Which creature present is the most dangerous.<br/>- Which creatures carry concealed weapons or hidden items, if any.<br/>- Where the nearest way out of the location is, such as a back door, window, or gap in a crowd.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 2)</strong> You gain the following abilities:<br/>- After you use your Size Up ability, you gain +1 boon on the first attack roll, Influence roll, or Streetwise roll you make against a creature you sized up during the same scene.<br/>- You can use your Size Up ability just before rolling Initiative, unless you are surprised.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 3)</strong> When you use your Size Up ability, you also learn one tactically useful feature of the location, such as a strong position, an unstable structure, or a usable improvised weapon (the GM chooses what is present). When combat breaks out in that location during the same scene, you and all allies who heard your assessment gain +1 boon on their Initiative rolls.
**Swashbuckler**  | <strong>(Rank 1)</strong> Gain +2 HP. When you hit an enemy with a light melee weapon, this turn that enemy can’t use Quick Actions or other abilities outside of their own turn against you.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 2) </strong>Gain +2 HP. If you hit a creature with a light melee weapon, you gain +1 boon on the next attack with a light melee weapon against them during the same turn.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 3)</strong> Gain +2 HP. After you attack a creature with a light melee weapon, you can choose to immediately spend 1 unprovoked Movement. You can use this ability once between your turns.
**Thug Tactics**  | <strong>(Rank 1) </strong>When you are in melee range with at least one ally and you outnumber the enemies you are in melee range with, gain +1 boon on attacks against them.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 2)</strong> Your allies that you are in melee range with also gain +1 boon against outnumbered enemies you are in melee range with.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 3) </strong>Outnumbered enemies suffer +1 bane on attacks against you and allies you are in melee range with.
