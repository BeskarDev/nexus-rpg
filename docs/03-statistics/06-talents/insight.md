# Insight

Name | Description
--- | ---
**Deflecting Presence**  | <strong>(Rank 1)</strong> When you use the Protect Ally Quick Action, you gain +2 to the targeted Defense against the redirected attack (situational bonus).<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 2)</strong> When an attack you redirected to yourself with Protect Ally misses you, the attacker briefly suffers +1 bane on their attack rolls.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 3)</strong> When you take a hit for an ally with the Protect Ally Quick Action, that ally can immediately make an Opportunity Attack against the attacker if the attacker is within their melee reach, using their Quick Action as normal.
**Empath**  | <strong>(Rank 1)</strong> When you roll Insight about the emotional state, personality, or life circumstances of another intelligent creature, you can re-roll the test once per scene.<br/><strong><br/>(Rank 2)</strong> You can use your Quick Action during your turn to roll Spirit + Insight vs. Resist against a creature you can see.<br/>On a success, you use your insights into the creature’s emotional state, personality, or life circumstances to predict their behaviour. You briefly gain +1 boon on any rolls targeting them and they suffer +1 bane on any roll targeting you. On a success, you can also roll for this ability again on your following turns without using your Quick Action. On a failure, the target is immune against this ability for the rest of the scene.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 3)</strong> When you roll Insight about the emotional state, personality, or life circumstances of another intelligent creature, you gain +1 boon on the roll.
**Foresight**  | <em>Requires Insight at rank 4 and the Empath talent at Rank 3.</em><br/><br/><strong>(Rank 4)</strong> You gain the following abilities:<br/>- You can roll Spirit + Insight for your Initiative rolls and gain +1 boon on them.<br/>- Once per scene, when a creature you can see attacks you or an ally within short range, you can use your Quick Action to call out the attack before it lands. The defender gains +2 to the targeted Defense against that attack (situational bonus).<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 5)</strong> Once per day, immediately after any creature within short range rolls a test and the result is known, you can declare that you already saw this moment unfold. The test is re-rolled and the new result stands.
**Intuitive Appraisal**  | <strong>(Rank 1) </strong>After spending a delving turn (some minutes) with an object, you can estimate its Quality and material worth in coin (not including magical properties). <br/><strong></strong><br/><strong>(Rank 2) </strong>You can use your Quick Action to appraise the state of a humanoid’s equipment or a non-humanoid’s natural body structure that you can see and gain information about how to defeat them efficiently.<br/>Roll Spirit/Mind + Insight vs. a Difficulty determined by the target’s Tier (Tier 0: TN 4, Tier 1: TN 6, Tier 2: TN 8, Tier 3: TN 10, Tier 4: TN 12, Tier 5: TN 14, Tier 6: TN 16). <br/>On a success, you gain +1 boon on your next attack against them and ignore 1/2 AV on a hit. You can succeed on this ability only once per creature.<br/><strong><br/>(Rank 3)</strong> You gain the following abilities:<br/>- When you estimate an item’s Quality and worth, you also determine if the item is magical, it’s general magical nature (offensive, protective, enhancing, summoning, etc.) and can deduce the total worth in coin including magical properties.<br/>- When you successfully use your Quick Action for Intuitive Appraisal against a creature during combat, their equipment or sellable body parts aren’t as damaged after defeating them, as they normally would be.<br/>In the case of equipment, it isn’t damaged after the fight. In the case of valuable body parts, you or your allies gain +1 boon on the roll to harvest them.
**Piercing Look**  | <strong>(Rank 1)</strong> When you roll Insight to know if a creature is lying, how they will act, or why they act how they do, you can re-roll the test once per scene.<br/><strong><br/>(Rank 2)</strong> As a Quick Action on your turn, you can focus your attention on one creature you can see in long range. Roll Spirit + Insight vs. Resist. On a success, your rolls against the creature gain +1 boon and any rolls the creature makes against you suffer +1 bane. This effect lasts for a short duration. You can succeed on this ability once per scene.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 3)</strong> When you use your Piercing Look ability to focus your attention on one creature you can see and the target dies or falls unconscious, you can use your ability again on your next turn against another target.
**Read the Room**  | <strong>(Rank 1)</strong> Before you interact with an NPC in a way that matters to them, you can quietly read them first. Roll Spirit + Insight vs. their Resist. On a success, the GM reveals one of the NPC's Motivations (a desire, fear, bond, or value they hold), GM's choice. You can use this ability once per NPC.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 2)</strong> You gain the following abilities:<br/>- When your Read the Room roll achieves a strong or critical success, the GM also reveals one of the NPC's Pitfalls.<br/>- Once per scene, when an ally makes an appeal to a Motivation you uncovered, you can support them with a subtle cue. They gain +1 boon on their roll.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 3)</strong> Once per scene, when you or an ally triggers one of the NPC's Pitfalls, you can immediately interject to smooth over the misstep. Roll Spirit + Insight vs. the NPC's Resist. On a success, you gain +1 boon on your next roll with this NPC. If this happens during a Social Intrigue, the NPC's Interest doesn't drop instead.
**Sense of Deduction**  | <strong>(Rank 1) </strong>When you roll Insight to investigate a chain of events, the responsible entity of an event, the time an event took place, or who might know more about an event, you can re-roll the test once per scene.<br/><strong></strong><br/><strong>(Rank 2) </strong>While investigating a chain of events, the responsible entity of an event, the time an event took place, or who might know more about an event, you can choose to make an assessment about that topic. The GM will tell you if your assessment is generally true or false without any more nuance. You can use this ability once per scene.<br/><strong><br/>(Rank 3)</strong> When you use your Sense of Deduction ability to make an assessment, you can re-use this ability multiple times per scene until you make a false assessment.
**Take Their Measure**  | <strong>(Rank 1)</strong> Once per scene, you can spend a delving turn (some minutes) discreetly studying a creature to take their measure. Roll Spirit + Insight vs. their Resist. On a success, the GM truthfully answers one of the following about them, your choice: what they are likely to do next, what they most fear or want to avoid, or where they are most vulnerable.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 2)</strong> You gain the following abilities:<br/>- You can roll Spirit/Mind + Insight in place of Education or Lore to reason out a problem from clues and observation, such as how a device works, what caused something, or how to solve a puzzle.<br/>- After you take a creature's measure, you gain +1 boon on the first roll you make against them during this scene or the next scene they appear in.<br/><br/><strong>(Rank 3)</strong> You gain the following abilities:<br/>- When you take a creature's measure, the GM answers two of the options instead of one.<br/>- When you share what you learned from taking a creature's measure, each ally who heard you gains +1 boon on their first roll against that creature during this scene or the next scene it appears in.
