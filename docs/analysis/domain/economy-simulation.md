# Domain Economy Simulation: Founding to Fabled Seat

> **Companion to:** [Build Your Domain — GDD](DOMAIN_SYSTEM_GDD.md). Simulates a domain's full progression against the group's assumed wealth accumulation, derived from the [Random Treasure tables](../../10-gm-tools/01-random-tables/05-random-treasure.mdx). Answers: when can a party realistically found a domain, what must they invest beyond what the domain handles internally, and at what character levels does each rank become reachable.
>
> **Reusable output:** §2's "Assumed Group Liquid Wealth by Level" table is written to stand alone as future GM guidance, independent of the domain system.

---

## 1. Method & Assumptions

All numbers derive from published tables. Every assumption is listed so the owner can re-tune one knob and re-derive the rest.

**Treasure value by Quality** (Random Treasure, cost formula averages):

| Q1 | Q2 | Q3 | Q4 | Q5 | Q6 | Q7 | Q8 |
|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|
| 25 | 75 | 250 | 750 | 2,500 | 7,500 | 25,000 | 75,000 |

**Treasure frequency per level (Assumption A).** The group earns per character level: **4 × Simple Loot, 2 × Minor Treasure, 1 × Major Treasure**, using the Random Treasure by Level row for that level (1d3 counted as 2). This matches the XP pacing (levels take roughly 4–9 sessions, about 1–2 adventures) and the treasure distribution guidance in the magic item design skill (minor finds per session, one major treasure per arc).

**Liquidity factor (Assumption B).** Not all treasure becomes spendable coin. Roughly half the treasure type table is valuables (gems and trade goods sell at full value), the rest is gear (sold at half value or kept and used). Living costs, supplies, healing, and downtime expenses eat a further share. Net: **50% of nominal treasure value accumulates as liquid group savings.** The other half is worn, drunk, burned, and carried.

**Party size (Assumption C).** 4 adventurers. Per-PC figures divide by 4.

**Downtime volume (Assumption D).** One downtime phase of 4–5 weeks per character level, ~45 downtime weeks across a full 1–10 campaign.

## 2. Assumed Group Liquid Wealth by Level

Nominal treasure per level (4 Simple + 2 Minor + 1 Major, averages):

| Level | Simple (each) | Minor (each) | Major | Nominal This Level | **Cumulative Group Liquid (50%)** | Per PC |
|-------|---------------|--------------|-------|--------------------|-----------------------------------|--------|
| 1 | 25 | 125 | 450 | 800 | **400** | 100 |
| 2 | 50 | 200 | 700 | 1,300 | **1,000** | 250 |
| 3 | 75 | 325 | 1,400 | 2,350 | **2,250** | 550 |
| 4 | 150 | 650 | 2,150 | 4,050 | **4,250** | 1,050 |
| 5 | 250 | 1,000 | 4,500 | 7,500 | **8,000** | 2,000 |
| 6 | 500 | 2,000 | 7,000 | 13,000 | **14,500** | 3,600 |
| 7 | 750 | 3,250 | 14,000 | 23,500 | **26,000** | 6,500 |
| 8 | 1,500 | 6,500 | 21,500 | 40,500 | **46,500** | 11,600 |

**Sanity check against the item economy:** a Level 7 PC (6,500 liquid) affords a Q5 item (2,500–15,000), a Level 9 PC (21,000) reaches into Q6 (7,500–45,000). That matches the treasure tables' own Quality-by-level progression and the magic item skill's "one permanent item at character-level quality per arc." The model is internally consistent.

> **Reuse note:** this table is the general "assumed wealth per level" baseline. It belongs in GM guidance eventually (pricing rewards, ransoms, bribes, patron gifts) and nothing in it is domain-specific.

## 3. The Domain Cost Stack

What a domain actually costs, cumulative, per rank. "Comfortable" means the rank plus a typical facility spread for it (the GDD §16 stages), including the half-credit on chain upgrades.

| Milestone | Sticker Costs | Cumulative Domain Investment |
|-----------|---------------|------------------------------|
| Found (Rank 1, 3–4 facilities) | 500 seed + 2–3 × T1 (250) | **~1,500–2,500** |
| Rank 2, comfortable (6–8 facilities, T2s) | 2,500 upgrade + ~3 × T2 (1,000) | **~7,000–9,000** |
| Rank 3, comfortable (incl. one T3 chain upgrade) | 10,000 upgrade + T3s (5,000, half-credits apply) | **~22,000–28,000** |
| Rank 4, comfortable (incl. Grand Atelier T4) | 40,000 upgrade + ~2 × T4 (20,000) | **~95,000–110,000** |
| Rank 5, full | 150,000 upgrade + T5s (75,000) | **~300,000+** |

Running costs (upkeep + wages, GDD §16) are handled internally by an engaged domain (break-even) and are excluded here. This stack is pure growth capital.

## 4. What the Domain Contributes to Its Own Growth

Internal income is capped (GDD §16 ceilings) and mostly consumed by running costs:

- **Engaged posture** (one PC-week on domain activities per week, income facilities staffed): income ≈ running costs. Growth contribution ≈ **0**.
- **Commerce-maxed posture** (income facilities built out for ~5,000–10,000 of their own capital, Trade Venture run every single week, saturation cap respected): surplus ≈ 50–150 coins/week. Across the ~25 downtime weeks of a mid-to-late campaign: **~2,000–4,000 total**, under 5% of the Rank 3 → 4 step alone.

**Conclusion: 95%+ of all scaling capital must come from adventure treasure or patrons.** The domain sustains itself. It never grows itself at any pace that matters. This is the intended answer to "self-sustaining and growing without adventuring": self-sustaining yes, growing no.

## 5. Simulation Run: Unassisted Party

Rule of thumb applied: a party will realistically commit **one third to one half of group liquid wealth** to the domain, because gear, consumables, and downtime life compete for the same coins (the intended tension, GDD §16).

| Level | Group Liquid | 1/3–1/2 Available for Domain | Domain State Reachable | Verdict |
|-------|--------------|------------------------------|------------------------|---------|
| 1–2 | 400–1,000 | 150–500 | Nothing, or bare founding at full stretch | Too early. Founding eats every coin and upkeep bites |
| 3–4 | 2,250–4,250 | 750–2,100 | **Found at Rank 1** (~1,500–2,500) | **Natural founding window.** Affordable at the 1/2 mark by Level 4 |
| 5–6 | 8,000–14,500 | 2,700–7,200 | **Rank 2 comfortable** (~7,000–9,000) by Level 6 | On curve |
| 7–8 | 26,000–46,500 | 8,700–23,000 | **Rank 3 comfortable** (~22,000–28,000) by Level 8 | On curve at the 1/2 mark. The Atelier (Q5 crafting) lands here, exactly when PCs want Q5 gear |
| 9–10 | 84,000–150,000 | 28,000–75,000 | **Rank 4** (~95,000–110,000) only at ~2/3+ commitment or with windfalls | **Deliberate stretch.** Unassisted Rank 4 means choosing the domain over personal Q6 gear |
| 10+ | 150,000 ceiling | — | **Rank 5 (~300,000+) exceeds total campaign group wealth** | **Impossible unassisted, by design.** Requires patrons, a kingdom's gratitude, or a legendary hoard, matching the GDD §5 story requirement |

**Level thresholds, condensed:**

| Domain Rank | Unassisted Threshold | With a Generous+ Patron |
|-------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| 1 (found) | Level 3–4 | Level 1 (patron provides claim + seed, as in the playtest campaign) |
| 2 | Level 5–6 | Level 3–4 |
| 3 | Level 7–8 | Level 5–6 |
| 4 | Level 9–10, heavy commitment | Level 7–8 |
| 5 | Not reachable | Level 9–10, plus the story achievement |

The patron column shifts everything about two levels earlier and is exactly the Shrouded Court configuration: patrons carry upkeep and gift capital, and the price is obligations (GDD §14).

## 6. The Greed Test: Can a Domain Out-Earn Adventuring?

Three postures compared over one mid-campaign level (Level 6, ~5 downtime weeks, ~6 adventure sessions):

| Posture | Coins In | What It Cost |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Adventuring the level normally | ~13,000 nominal, ~6,500 liquid | Wounds, Fatigue, risk of death |
| Domain, engaged (1 PC-week/week) | ~0 net (running costs covered) | 5 PC-weeks of downtime |
| Domain, commerce-maxed (all PCs grinding) | ~250–750 net surplus, treasury-bound | 20 PC-weeks, every other downtime benefit forgone, and the surplus cannot become personal gear beyond the capped extraction channels |

Adventuring out-earns maximal domain commerce by a factor of **~10–25×** while the domain surplus is additionally fenced inside the treasury (GDD §6). And the moment the party extracts in kind (having the Artificer craft group gear with treasury funds), the surplus is consumed immediately: one Q4 commission (~2,100 with fees) erases years of commerce margin. **A growing domain and personal extraction are mutually exclusive at every scale, which is the design goal.** The only fast path through the domain is the liquidation exploit family, and §16's closed-loop table blocks each member.

---

## 7. Findings & Adjustments

1. **The cost curve holds.** No rank price needs changing. Rank thresholds land where the GDD intends: founding mid-early, Rank 3 as the mid-late sweet spot delivering Q5 crafting on time, Rank 4 as a real sacrifice, Rank 5 as patron-or-legend territory.
2. **GDD §16 stage table adjusted** (this simulation supersedes it): Rank 3 belongs to Levels 7–8, not 4–6, for unassisted parties. The patron column covers faster campaigns.
3. **The playtest campaign's roadmap is patron-math, and should say so.** The Shrouded Court reaching Rank 4 around sessions 21–30 (Levels 7–8) requires its two patrons to contribute roughly 30–40% of all capital across the campaign, on top of upkeep. That is the intended shape of that campaign, not the baseline.
4. **Founding has no cheap-entry problem.** At Levels 1–2 even the 500-coin seed plus upkeep is a real burden, so patron-financed early domains (the intended low-level path) stay a meaningful narrative debt.
5. **Watch in playtest:** Assumption A (treasure frequency) is the load-bearing knob. GMs who roll treasure more generously than 4/2/1 per level shift every threshold earlier roughly one level per doubling. The §2 table should eventually ship as GM guidance with exactly that warning attached.
