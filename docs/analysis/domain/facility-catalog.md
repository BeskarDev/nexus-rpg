# Domain Facility Catalog

> **Companion to:** [Build Your Domain — GDD](DOMAIN_SYSTEM_GDD.md). Read §9 there for shared facility rules.
>
> **Shared rules recap:** A facility's tier requires domain rank ≥ tier. Tier costs are 250 / 1,000 / 5,000 / 20,000 / 75,000 coins with construction timers d4 / d4 / d6 / d6 / d8. Facilities of Tier 2+ require a specialist of grade ≥ tier (see [Domain NPCs & Staff](domain-npcs.md)). Unstaffed facilities keep passive benefits but provide no activities or Provisions. Upgrading and replacing facilities follows the rules in the next section.

---

## How to Read This Catalog

Each entry is a facility or an upgrade chain. Chains list one row per tier. "On site" means the party can take that downtime activity at the domain, regardless of any settlement, using the listed terms.

**Footprint.** Facilities occupy space in the game's abstract terms (see GDD §5, Physical Scale), defaulting by tier: Tier 1 is **compact** (a room, cellar, or corner within a shared area), Tier 2 and 3 are **standard** (an own building, hall, or yard of about 1 area), Tier 4 and 5 are **sprawling** (2 to 3 areas with wings and outbuildings). Exceptions are called out in the entry: walls and gates are the domain's perimeter rather than an area of their own, a Safehouse stands off-site in a nearby settlement, and gardens and grounds facilities (Sacred Garden, Herb Garden, Hunting Lodge) sit at or beyond the domain's edge. When a facility upgrades along its chain, its footprint grows with its tier, which the fiction should show: the cellar shrine becomes a temple with a real doorway, and hiding it stops being free. Boons from facilities apply to downtime and domain rolls only, never to adventure rolls. Where a facility produces a **Provision**, the Provision's rules are in the master list at the end of this file. Built facilities can be customized further with **Add-Ons** (see the section before the Provisions list), the facility-side parallel to a magic item's special material.

## Upgrading & Replacing Facilities

Rising domain ranks unlock higher facility tiers, and the hub-camp loop expects the party to grow what they already own. These rules cover every change to a built facility:

1. **Chain upgrades.** Upgrading a facility along its chain costs the new tier's price **minus half the old facility's cost**, uses the new tier's construction timer, and keeps the same slot. The domain recovers half of what it built, the same principle as liquidation: half back, never more.
2. **Works through the dust.** The old facility keeps functioning at its current tier while the upgrade builds. It switches to the new tier when the timer completes.
3. **Staffing rises with tier.** The upgraded facility needs a specialist of grade ≥ its new tier. Plan the person before the stones: a Grand Atelier with an Expert artificer is an unstaffed hall with a very good forge. The Dedicated Quarters add-on (grade +1 for facility function) can bridge one tier, except for the Workshop chain's Quality ceilings.
4. **Add-ons carry over** to the upgraded facility at no cost.
5. **Same-category replacement.** Rebuilding a facility into a different facility of the **same category** (a Shrine into a Hidden Altar) works like a chain upgrade: new facility's cost minus half the old one's, same slot, new timer.
6. **Demolition or cross-category rebuild** refunds nothing. Tear it down, free the slot, build new at full price.

> **Design Note:** Rank-ups never force facility upgrades. A Rank 4 domain full of Tier 1 facilities is legitimate (broad and humble), just as a Rank 3 domain with one Atelier and little else is (narrow and deep). The half-credit makes upgrading the default-attractive path without punishing either extreme, and it keeps the "recover half" arithmetic uniform across the whole module.

## Hearth

The living heart of any domain. Most parties build here first.

### Quarters (Tier 1)

Beds, hearths, and a roof for the party and their followers. The party can take **Recover** and **Leisure** on site at their normal costs, with no settlement required. Followers housed here are fed and rested: the domain ignores the first Wear and Tear event result of "Weary Hands" each downtime phase.

### Kitchens → Brewery & Larder

| Tier | Name | Cost | Timer |
|------|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Kitchens | 250 | d4 |
| 2 | Brewery & Larder | 1,000 | d4 |

**Kitchens (T1).** Hot meals for all. The "Hungry Mouths" expense event costs half. Once per downtime phase the party can hold a small feast for 25 coins per domain rank: the next Recruit Followers or Extend Influence roll gains +1 boon.

**Brewery & Larder (T2).** Produces the **Traveling Fare** Provision. Feasts also remove 1 Lingering Fatigue from every attending adventurer.

### Infirmary → House of Healing

| Tier | Name | Cost | Timer |
|------|------|------|-------|
| 2 | Infirmary | 1,000 | d4 |
| 4 | House of Healing | 20,000 | d6 |

**Infirmary (T2).** Staffed sickbeds. **Recover** on site costs 25 coins instead of 50 and also removes all Lingering Fatigue. Produces the **Salves and Bandages** Provision.

**House of Healing (T4).** Renowned healers and rare remedies. As Infirmary, and once per downtime phase one adventurer or staff NPC can be treated for a lasting affliction (a curse, disease, or permanent injury the GM has established). Treatment takes 1 week and costs 500 coins, and the GM states plainly whether the affliction is within mortal healing or needs a quest.

### Vault (Tier 2)

Stone, iron, and a guarded door. The treasury and stored items are safe: the domain ignores "Spoiled Stores" events, and thieves need a staged story, not a die roll, to reach the vault. Stored magic items do not count against any carrying limits while home.

### Palisade → Stone Walls → Bastion Unbroken

| Tier | Name | Cost | Timer |
|------|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Palisade | 250 | d4 |
| 3 | Stone Walls | 5,000 | d6 |
| 5 | Bastion Unbroken | 75,000 | d8 |

**Palisade (T1).** Ditch, stakes, and a watch. +1 flat bonus on Force checks made to defend the domain.

**Stone Walls (T3).** True fortification with gatehouse and towers. +2 flat bonus on Force checks made to defend the domain, and raiders never inflict Disrepair on a successful defense.

**Bastion Unbroken (T5).** Walls that enter legend, laid with master rites and cyclopean stone. As Stone Walls, and the domain cannot be taken by any force short of an army, which is always a staged story arc, never an event roll.

### Well & Cistern (Tier 1)

Clean water in any season. The domain and its followers endure sieges, droughts, and long isolation without event penalties tied to supply of water or fresh stores, and the "Sick Livestock" event is ignored on a d6 roll of 4+.

## War

For drillmasters, champions, and anyone who wants followers who can hold a line.

### Training Yard → Drill Grounds → War College

| Tier | Name | Cost | Timer |
|------|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Training Yard | 250 | d4 |
| 3 | Drill Grounds | 5,000 | d6 |
| 5 | War College | 75,000 | d8 |

**Training Yard (T1).** Posts, pells, and sparring rings. Adventurers can take **Train with a Master** on site if a qualified teacher (skill rank 3+) is among staff or party.

**Drill Grounds (T3).** Professional drill for the followers. +1 flat bonus on mission rolls resolved with Force. The Master-at-Arms counts as a qualified teacher for Fighting, Archery, and Athletics.

**War College (T5).** Veterans of famous wars teach here. **Train with a Master** on site gains +1 boon, and once per downtime phase the college's doctrine grants one mission an automatic weak success instead of a roll (declare before rolling).

### Armory (Tier 2)

Racks, oil, and a whetstone wheel always turning. Weapon and armor **Durability** damage is repaired on site for 10 coins per item. Produces the **Whetstones and Oils** Provision.

### Barracks → Garrison

| Tier | Name | Cost | Timer |
|------|------|------|-------|
| 2 | Barracks | 1,000 | d4 |
| 4 | Garrison | 20,000 | d6 |

**Barracks (T2).** Housed, paid guards. +1 boon on **Recruit Followers**, and the domain can always mount a watch: surprise never favors attackers of the domain itself.

**Garrison (T4).** A standing armed company. The domain's **agent pool** increases by 1 (see [Missions & Agents](missions-and-agents.md)), and defense missions gain +1 boon.

### War Room (Tier 3)

Maps, markers, and reports. Once per downtime phase the party may redirect one active mission: change its objective or recall the agents safely, ending the mission without a roll. Mission briefings are sharper: the GM always states a mission's danger tier and likely complications before agents are committed.

### Fighting Pit (Tier 3)

A sanded ring with benches. Sparring bouts entertain and harden the followers: once per downtime phase, gain 1 progress toward **Force** or hold a public spectacle (Open domains only) that earns 25 × domain rank coins and grants +1 boon on the next Extend Influence roll.

## Arcana

For casters who want a home worthy of their craft. Arcane facilities serve any tradition of magic unless noted.

### Study → Sanctum → Hall of Mysteries

| Tier | Name | Cost | Timer |
|------|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Study | 250 | d4 |
| 2 | Sanctum | 1,000 | d4 |
| 4 | Hall of Mysteries | 20,000 | d6 |

**Study (T1).** A quiet room of slates and lamps. Adventurers can take **Learn a Skill** on site for Arcana, Lore, or Mysticism.

**Sanctum (T2).** Warded practice chambers. Spells can be practiced safely: rolls made during downtime to learn or swap spells (per whatever learning rules the group uses) gain +1 boon, and arcane mishaps during downtime practice affect no one outside the sanctum.

**Hall of Mysteries (T4).** A seat of magical learning that rivals a city academy. As Sanctum, and **Research** on site into magical topics has no settlement rank cap. Once per downtime phase the resident Loremaster answers one question about magic, creatures, or relics with concrete, reliable information (or names exactly what quest would uncover it).

### Library → Great Library

| Tier | Name | Cost | Timer |
|------|------|------|-------|
| 2 | Library | 1,000 | d4 |
| 4 | Great Library | 20,000 | d6 |

**Library (T2).** Scrolls, tablets, and maps. Adventurers can take **Research** on site as if in a Town (rank 3).

**Great Library (T4).** A collection scholars would travel months to see. **Research** on site as if in a City (rank 4) with +1 boon, and visiting scholars pay their respects: gain 1 progress toward **Influence** each downtime phase the library is staffed.

### Alchemy Lab (Tier 2)

Alembics, burners, and locked reagent chests. Adventurers and staff can craft alchemical items on site per the crafting rules, up to Q4. Produces the **Alchemical Draught** Provision. Material costs for alchemical items are reduced by one quarter if the domain has an Herb Garden or Physic Garden.

### Scriptorium (Tier 3)

Copy desks and inks both common and rare. Spell scrolls and written works up to Q5 can be crafted or commissioned on site per the crafting rules. Copying commissions earn the treasury 25 × domain rank coins each downtime phase when staffed.

### Ritual Circle → Circle of the Veiled Stars

| Tier | Name | Cost | Timer |
|------|------|------|-------|
| 3 | Ritual Circle | 5,000 | d6 |
| 5 | Circle of the Veiled Stars | 75,000 | d8 |

**Ritual Circle (T3).** Inlaid stone, precise sightlines, stored power. Rituals and long castings performed here gain +1 boon, and Provide Offering performed by casters on site gains +1 boon.

**Circle of the Veiled Stars (T5).** One of the great workings of the age. As Ritual Circle, and once per campaign arc the circle enables one **great working**: a single Rank 5 scale magical undertaking (sealing a gate, breaking a citywide curse, calling a season of rain) staged by the GM as an adventure culminating at the circle. The circle enables the attempt, never the automatic success.

## Faith

For the oath-bound and the god-touched. Hidden domains often build their faith facilities in cellars and caves.

### Shrine → Temple → High Temple

| Tier | Name | Cost | Timer |
|------|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Shrine | 250 | d4 |
| 3 | Temple | 5,000 | d6 |
| 5 | High Temple | 75,000 | d8 |

**Shrine (T1).** A consecrated niche with offerings bowl and icon. Adventurers can take **Provide Offering** on site. Produces the **Blessed Token** Provision.

**Temple (T3).** A proper house of the divine with resident clergy. **Provide Offering** on site gains +1 boon and its benefit extends to all adventurers present, not only the one performing it. Pilgrims begin to arrive: gain 10 × domain rank coins in offerings each downtime phase when staffed.

**High Temple (T5).** A seat of faith that draws pilgrims from other city-states. As Temple, and the domain's Hierophant may once per downtime phase perform a **grand rite** granting every adventurer +1 Resolve (max. 3). The temple's sanction carries political weight: +1 flat bonus on Influence checks involving religious powers.

### Hidden Altar (Tier 2)

A concealed sanctum for rites the world must not see. Functions as a Shrine, and the domain's faith activities never generate Heat. **Research** into occult, forbidden, or forgotten religious topics can be taken on site as if in a Town (rank 3). Hidden domains only, and it never appears to visitors as anything but storage.

### Sacred Garden (Tier 2)

Green shade, water, and quiet. **Leisure** on site also grants +1 Resolve (max. 3) on a Spirit + Fortitude roll vs. TN 8, and the garden yields herbs: counts as an Herb Garden for other facilities' benefits.

### Reliquary (Tier 3)

Warded housings for holy and unholy things. Relics, cursed items, and dangerous artifacts stored here are inert and undetectable from outside. Each relic housed (GM confirms what counts) grants +1 boon on Provide Offering on site, up to +2.

### Oracle Chamber (Tier 4)

Smoke, still water, or thrown bones, and a seer who reads them. Once per downtime phase the party may put one question about the coming adventure or a named power to the oracle. The GM answers with one of: a true fact, a named danger, a named opportunity, or a warning that the matter is veiled (at most one veiled answer per downtime phase, and the GM then names what would lift the veil).

## Shadow

For infiltrators, spies, and organizations that live in the dark. The natural home category of a covert campaign.

### Safehouse → Safehouse Network

| Tier | Name | Cost | Timer |
|------|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Safehouse | 250 | d4 |
| 3 | Safehouse Network | 5,000 | d6 |

**Safehouse (T1).** A bolt-hole nobody connects to the domain. +1 boon on **Lay Low**, and once per downtime phase one person (adventurer, agent, or guest) can vanish from sight for a week, beyond finding by mundane search.

**Safehouse Network (T3).** Boltholes, couriers, and cutouts across the region. As Safehouse, and once per downtime phase reduce Heat by 1 automatically. When a mission blunders, roll the domain's Secrecy vs. TN 8: on a success, captured agents escape to a safehouse instead (the mission still fails).

### Listening Post → Web of Whispers

| Tier | Name | Cost | Timer |
|------|------|------|-------|
| 2 | Listening Post | 1,000 | d4 |
| 4 | Web of Whispers | 20,000 | d6 |

**Listening Post (T2).** Paid ears in taverns and markets. Each downtime phase the party receives one free rumor (use the Quest Hooks random tables), and the GM reveals the current stage of any faction agenda that has reached Maneuvering.

**Web of Whispers (T4).** Informants in courts, temples, and guilds. As Listening Post, and +1 flat bonus on mission rolls resolved with Influence or Secrecy. Produces the **Scout's Dossier** Provision.

### Disguise Workshop (Tier 2)

Wigs, dyes, seals, and stagecraft. Covert missions gain +1 boon when infiltration is the method. Produces the **False Papers** Provision.

### Smugglers' Cache (Tier 2)

Hollow walls, false floors, quiet buyers. The party can fence goods on site: sell items for 60% of value instead of 50%. **Haggle (Buying)** on site can seek illegal and restricted goods, using the domain's rank as the settlement rank.

### Shadow Cells (Tier 3)

Quiet rooms below, for guests who must not leave. Captives can be held indefinitely without event risk. Once per week per captive, one adventurer or the Spymaster may question them: roll Spirit or Mind + Insight or Influence vs. TN 8 + the captive's tier. On a success the captive yields one true, useful piece of information chosen by the GM from: a name, a place, a plan, or a weakness. Coercion beyond words is a story choice with story consequences, never a bonus.

### Poisoner's Still (Tier 3)

Glass, gloves, and labeled dread. Toxins, antidotes, and sleep agents up to Q5 can be crafted on site per the crafting and alchemy rules. The domain's antidote stores grant +1 boon on rolls against poison during downtime.

## Trade & Craft

The economic engine and the magic item pipeline.

### Forge (Tier 1)

Anvil, bellows, and honest work. Mundane items up to Q3 can be crafted or repaired on site per the crafting rules. Durability repairs cost 10 coins per item.

### Workshop → Atelier → Grand Atelier → Wonderworks

| Tier | Name | Cost | Timer | Max Quality | Required Artificer Grade |
|------|------|------|-------|-------------|--------------------------|
| 2 | Workshop | 1,000 | d4 | Q4 | 2 |
| 3 | Atelier | 5,000 | d6 | Q5 | 3 |
| 4 | Grand Atelier | 20,000 | d6 | Q6 | 4 |
| 5 | Wonderworks | 75,000 | d8 | Q7 | 5 |

The heart of the domain's long game. At each tier, magic items up to the listed Quality can be **crafted** (by an adventurer with the required profession and rank) or **commissioned** (from the resident artificer at the standard commission fee) on site, following the existing crafting rules without change: required successes, weekly costs, and material costs all apply.

**Workshop (T2).** Lesser magic arms, armor, and wearables (Q4), matching a Town's ceiling.

**Atelier (T3).** Potent works (Q5), matching a City's ceiling.

**Grand Atelier (T4).** Greater works (Q6). No settlement sells these. This is where the domain surpasses every market in the world.

**Wonderworks (T5).** Superior works (Q7), the summit of mortal craft. Each Q7 undertaking is a story in itself: sourcing its materials is always at least one adventure.

### Counting House (Tier 2)

Ledgers, scales, and a sharp-eyed keeper. +1 boon on the weekly **upkeep check** and on **Trade Venture**. The domain ignores "Windfall Tax" events.

### Trade Post → Caravanserai

| Tier | Name | Cost | Timer |
|------|------|------|-------|
| 2 | Trade Post | 1,000 | d4 |
| 4 | Caravanserai | 20,000 | d6 |

**Trade Post (T2).** Regular dealings with passing merchants. The party can take **Haggle** (buying and selling) on site, treating the domain's rank as the settlement rank.

**Caravanserai (T4).** Caravans route themselves to the domain's gates. As Trade Post, and the domain hosts its own market with 1 magic item slot, generated and refreshed per the settlement market procedure. Gain 10 × domain rank coins to the treasury each downtime phase from tolls and lodging.

### Warehouse (Tier 1)

Dry, guarded space in quantity. Bulk goods, supplies, and trade stock are safe and organized: the domain ignores "Supply Shortage" events, and the party's stored materials never spoil.

## Hall

Where the domain meets the world: hosting, remembering, negotiating.

### Common Hall → Feast Hall → Great Hall

| Tier | Name | Cost | Timer |
|------|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Common Hall | 250 | d4 |
| 2 | Feast Hall | 1,000 | d4 |
| 4 | Great Hall | 20,000 | d6 |

**Common Hall (T1).** One long room for meals, councils, and song. Adventurers can take **Carouse** on site among their own followers and neighbors.

**Feast Hall (T2).** Long tables and a reputation for hospitality. **Carouse** on site gains +1 boon. Once per downtime phase the party may host a feast for 50 coins per domain rank: every attending NPC relationship improves as if one Tend to Relationships success had been spent on the group (each named NPC improves at most 1 step per downtime phase this way).

**Great Hall (T4).** A hall built to awe. As Feast Hall, and the party can receive dignitaries: **Petition/Negotiate** conducted at the domain gains +1 boon, and visiting powers treat the party as peers rather than petitioners (the GM reflects this in starting Interest).

### Archive of Deeds (Tier 2)

Chronicles, trophies, and a keeper who writes it all down. +1 boon on **Extend Influence**. When the party completes a notable deed (GM confirms), the archive records it: the next Carouse or Petition/Negotiate that invokes the deed gains +1 boon, once per deed.

### Envoy Quarters (Tier 3)

Fine rooms for guests who matter. Emissaries, patrons' agents, and notables can be hosted in dignity: once per downtime phase the party may delay one patron obligation timer by 1 tick with hospitality instead of service. Produces the **Letters of Introduction** Provision.

## Designing New Facilities (GM Kit)

The catalog will never predict every campaign. When players ask for a place it does not contain (an apiary, an embalming chamber, a smoke-house for questionable meats), do not improvise costs. Price it with this kit, the same grammar the catalog itself was built from.

**This kit is GM-facing.** Players interact with it through fiction: they describe the place they want, the GM assembles and prices it in a couple of minutes, and what returns to the table is a named facility with a cost, a timer, and a staffing need, indistinguishable from a catalog entry. Players never pick components, and every facility they commission must have a name and a face (who works there). Encourage players to rename catalog facilities too. "The Feast Hall" is a menu entry, "Senbi's Long Table" is a place.

### Benefit Components

Each component is one effect pattern used across the catalog, rated at the tier it is worth.

| Component | Tier Worth | Catalog Examples |
|-----------|-----------|------------------|
| Basic activity access (take one downtime activity on site, normal terms) | 1 | Shrine, Common Hall, Quarters |
| Improved activity (on site with +1 boon or reduced cost) | 2 | Infirmary, Feast Hall |
| Beyond-settlement access (activity as settlement rank 3) | 3 | Library, Hidden Altar |
| Beyond-settlement access (as rank 4, uncapped, or an own market slot) | 4 | Great Library, Caravanserai |
| Crafting Quality ceiling (fixed ladder: Q3/Q4/Q5/Q6/Q7) | 1/2/3/4/5 | Forge, Workshop chain |
| Provision production (minor single-boon token / full Provision) | 1 / 2 | Shrine, Armory |
| Event immunity (ignore one named event result: minor / costly) | 1 / 2 | Warehouse, Vault |
| Once-per-downtime-phase service (minor / major) | 2 / 3 | Feast Hall feast / War Room recall |
| Flat mission bonus (+1 on missions resolved with one stat) | 3 | Drill Grounds, Web of Whispers |
| Defense bonus (+1 flat / +2 flat and no raid Disrepair / story-proof) | 1 / 3 / 5 | Palisade / Stone Walls / Bastion Unbroken |
| Income (10 × domain rank coins per phase, requires staffing) | 2 | Temple, Caravanserai |
| Bounded truthful answers (with a parameter menu, never open-ended) | 4 | Oracle Chamber, Hall of Mysteries |
| Once-per-arc capability (from a bounded menu of 3) | 4 | Beastgarden |
| Story-scale great working (enables an attempt, staged by the GM) | 5 | Circle of the Veiled Stars, War College |

### Assembly Rules

1. **Name and face first.** The facility needs a name, a one-line description, a category, and a staffing plan before any component is picked.
2. **Pick 1 or 2 components.** The facility's tier equals the highest component's tier. Cost, construction timer, and staffing follow the standard tier table.
3. **A third component raises the tier by 1.** Never more than 3 components.
4. **Guardrails.** Adventure-facing effects go through Provisions only. Flat bonuses respect the +2 cap on domain checks. Truthful-answer effects always ship with a parameter menu and a named quest-out for what they cannot answer. Income components always require staffing. Crafting ceilings always require the artificer's true grade.
5. **Chains.** To give a custom facility an upgrade path, redesign it at a higher tier with stronger components and keep the name lineage.

### Worked Examples

**Shrine, rederived (Tier 1, 250 coins).** Basic activity access (Provide Offering on site, tier 1) + minor Provision production (Blessed Token, tier 1). Highest tier 1, two components. Matches the catalog entry exactly.

**Feast Hall, rederived (Tier 2, 1,000 coins).** Improved activity (Carouse on site with +1 boon, tier 2) + minor once-per-phase service (the feast, tier 2). Matches the catalog entry exactly.

**The Reed Hives, new (Tier 2, 1,000 coins, d4 timer, grade 2 beekeeper).** A playtest-campaign request: Prince Nehsi officially keeps bees, so the Shrouded Court makes it true. Income (honey and mead trade, 10 × domain rank coins per phase when staffed, tier 2) + Provision production (Honeyed Draughts, treat as Traveling Fare, tier 2). Two components, highest tier 2. The cover story now pays for the conspiracy, which is exactly the kind of facility no catalog would have predicted.

> **Design Note:** The kit is the catalog's grammar made explicit, mirroring how the creature and talent design skills encode budgets: a designer tool that keeps a hundred custom facilities on one power curve. If a proposed facility does not decompose into these components, it is probably an adventure reward or a story permission, not a purchase.

## Facility Add-Ons

Add-ons customize a built facility, the way a special material customizes a magic item. An add-on costs **half the facility's tier cost**, takes a **d4 construction timer**, and does not use a facility slot. Each facility holds at most **2 add-ons**, and each add-on can be taken once per facility. When a facility upgrades along its chain, its add-ons carry over at no cost (the crews rebuild what they know).

| Add-On | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Concealed** | A false front, hidden entrance, or cellar depth. Visitors and searches do not find this facility. It never causes and never suffers Exposure results, and discovering it is always a staged story, not a roll. |
| **Warded** | Layered charms, seals, and honest locks. This facility ignores "Sabotage" events, and supernatural intrusion into it is a staged story, not a roll. Requires a Shrine, Hidden Altar, Sanctum, or Ritual Circle in the domain. |
| **Expanded** | More room, more benches, more hands. A benefit of this facility that is limited to once per downtime phase can be used twice per phase instead. |
| **Fine Appointments** | Inlay, incense, and comfort worth remarking on. **Tend to Relationships** and **Petition/Negotiate** conducted in this facility gain +1 boon. |
| **Fortified** | Thick doors, arrow slits, a fighting roof. While defenders hold this facility, +1 flat bonus on Force checks defending the domain (the +2 flat bonus cap applies). |
| **Dedicated Quarters** | The specialist lives beside their work. The specialist staffing this facility counts as one grade higher for the facility's function (maximum grade 5). This never raises the Quality ceiling of the Workshop chain, which always requires the artificer's true grade. |
| **Reinforced Stores** | Stone shelving, sealed jars, dry air. This facility ignores "Wear and Tear" events, and materials stored in it never spoil. |

> **Design Note:** Add-ons are the modular layer that customizes without abstracting: every add-on modifies a named place the party already owns, so "our Feast Hall, Expanded, with Fine Appointments" stays a place, not a stat line. Costs scale with the host facility so a 125-coin Concealed cellar shrine and a 10,000-coin Concealed Grand Atelier both stay proportionate purchases.

## Provisions Master List

Shared rules: see GDD §12. Ready up to domain rank per departure, maximum 2 per adventurer, one use each, no stacking, expire on return.

| Provision | Source Facility | Effect (one use) |
|-----------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Traveling Fare** | Brewery & Larder (T2) | When you finish a night's rest, remove 1 additional Fatigue. |
| **Salves and Bandages** | Infirmary (T2) | When you treat Wounds or HP after a scene, the treated character regains +2 HP. |
| **Whetstones and Oils** | Armory (T2) | Ignore one failed Durability check, or gain +1 boon on your first attack roll of one combat scene (declare before rolling). |
| **Blessed Token** | Shrine (T1) | Gain +1 boon on any one roll. Declare before rolling. |
| **Alchemical Draught** | Alchemy Lab (T2) | Counts as one Q3 alchemical consumable of your choice, chosen when readied. |
| **Scout's Dossier** | Web of Whispers (T4) | Gain +1 boon on the first travel, navigation, or investigation roll of the adventure. |
| **False Papers** | Disguise Workshop (T2) | Gain +1 boon on one roll to pass as someone you are not, or to get past a checkpoint or guard post. |
| **Letters of Introduction** | Envoy Quarters (T3) | Gain +1 boon on your first social interaction with an authority or official in one settlement. |
| **Beast Feed & Tack** | Stables (T1) | Your mount or companion gains +1 boon on one roll, or recovers 2 HP when rested and tended. |

> **Design Note:** Nine Provisions, all early-to-mid tier on purpose. High-tier facilities buy capability (Q6 crafting, oracles, great rites), not bigger adventure buffs. The consumable power ceiling stays flat from Rank 2 onward, only the menu widens.
