# Worked Example: The Shrouded Court

> **Companion to:** [Build Your Domain — GDD](DOMAIN_SYSTEM_GDD.md). A complete, ready-to-run setup for the playtest campaign: an occult underground organization built in the remote estate of a disgraced royal, financed from the shadows, growing from a handful of initiates into a power that city-states must reckon with.
>
> All proper names are placeholders. Rename freely to fit the campaign's city-state and folk choices.

---

## 1. The Premise

**Prince Nehsi**, a disgraced member of the royal line, was stripped of his offices after a succession intrigue and exiled to **the House of Reeds**, a crumbling ancestral estate three days upriver from the capital, amid marsh, ruin, and forgetting. Officially he keeps bees and grieves. Unofficially he is building something: a court in shadow, gathering the discarded, the gifted, and the dangerous, and the party is its founding circle.

The money comes from two places. Nehsi's own hidden reserves, and **Meresankh**, a high official of the Great Temple in **Kesh**, the more powerful neighboring city-state. Meresankh is corrupt, ambitious, and a true believer in something the temple would burn her for. Her coin is steady. Her demands are not optional. Neither patron fully knows what the other wants.

The party's brief: build the organization, keep it hidden, and grow its reach until the Shrouded Court can change what happens in palaces it will never officially enter.

---

## 2. The Domain at Campaign Start

Nehsi provides the claim (the estate), pays the seed cost, and fast-tracks the founding. The party starts play with the domain already at **Rank 1**, founded, with its free Tier 1 facility built and one more gifted by Nehsi.

```
DOMAIN: The Shrouded Court        Profile: Hidden
Rank: 1 (Camp)                    Location: House of Reeds, upriver marshes
Wealth d4 (0/2)   Influence d4 (0/2)   Force d4 (0/2)   Secrecy d4 (0/2)
Heat: 0
Treasury: 100 coins
Facilities: Quarters (T1), Safehouse (T1, in the river town of Perit)
Staff: see roster
Patrons: Prince Nehsi (Generous), Meresankh of Kesh (Generous)
Missions: none yet          Disrepair: 0
```

**Why these facilities:** Quarters make the estate a working home (Recover and Leisure on site, no settlement needed, which matters three days from anywhere). The Safehouse in Perit, the nearest river town, gives the fledgling Court a bolt-hole and +1 boon on Lay Low from week one.

**First purchases to steer players toward** (all Tier 1, 250 coins each): a **Shrine** or **Hidden Altar** path for the occult identity (Hidden Altar requires Rank 2, so a Shrine in the cellar now, rebuilt later as a same-category replacement with half the Shrine's cost credited), a **Common Hall** to gather the growing circle, an **Herb Garden** for the poisoner-minded. Rank 2 at 2,500 coins should land after the first adventure arc pays out.

## 3. The Patrons

### Prince Nehsi (the Disgraced Royal)

- **Stipend:** Generous. Covers upkeep through Rank 3 growth and adds 50 coins/week. His reserves are deep but not bottomless, and the GM should let the players glimpse the bottom eventually.
- **Disposition:** Friendly (+1). The party is his project and his hope.
- **Motivations:** Restoration (his name cleared or his line's future secured), Vindication (those who ruined him shown for what they are), and a quieter one he hides: genuine belief in the occult teaching at the Court's heart.
- **Pitfalls:** Publicly embarrassing the royal line (even now, he flinches), moving against his family directly without his blessing.
- **Obligation timer:** d6. His demands favor **Discretion**, **Audience**, and **Errand** results: quiet favors, late-night councils, small missions against the men who unmade him.

### Meresankh of Kesh (the Corrupt High Official)

- **Stipend:** Generous. 50 coins/week and temple-laundered cover for the Court's purchases in Kesh.
- **Disposition:** Indifferent (0). The party is an investment, not yet a loyalty.
- **Motivations:** Power within the Great Temple, suppression of a rival doctrine, and access to what the Shrouded Court digs up. She wants the occult knowledge itself.
- **Pitfalls:** Anything that threatens her standing in Kesh, contact in daylight, being asked direct questions about her faith.
- **Obligation timer:** d6, and the GM should keep her timer and Nehsi's out of phase so demands alternate. Her demands favor **Tribute** (relics, texts, secrets), **Leverage**, and **Errand** results aimed at her temple rivals.

**The designed collision:** Nehsi wants the Court aimed at the capital. Meresankh wants it aimed at Kesh's temple politics, and wants its discoveries in her hands. Around Rank 3 the party will hold something both patrons claim. That session is the campaign's hinge, and the refusal rules (GDD §14) give it teeth without scripting it.

| **Ipuki** | Spymaster (acting) | Scoundrel | 2 | Indifferent | Polite feud with Senbi / wants a debt in Perit paid quietly |
| **Tarhunza** | Master-at-Arms | Adventurer | 2 | Friendly | Old scars, unfinished stories / wants a rival reconciled |
| **Weret** | Hierophant (acting) | Scholar | 1 | Friendly | Devout beyond the Court's norm / wants a masterwork completed: the Doctrine, written whole |
| *(vacant)* | Artificer | — | — | — | The Court cannot craft above Q3 until this is solved. Early recruiting goal |

Senbi and Tarhunza came with Nehsi from his old household. Ipuki is Meresankh's suggestion, which the players should eventually think hard about. Weret is grade 1: her faith outruns her learning, and leveling her up (through story, or replacing her gently) is a live question the group will feel.

## 5. Running Heat in This Campaign

The Shrouded Court starts at Heat 0 and Secrecy d4. Early expectations:

- Recruit Followers and Extend Influence blunders, covert mission complications, and "Word Spreads" events will push Heat up by 1 about every second downtime phase if the party plays boldly.
- With Secrecy at d4, the first Exposure check (at Heat 3) fails often. **That is intended.** The first Exposure result should land in the first third of the campaign and teach the table that the pressure is real. The Exposure table's results (Informant, Investigator, Blackmail) are all better stories than punishments.
- Steer the party toward Secrecy progress early (the Deeper Shadows bonus on Lay Low, Watch missions, buying progress) and the Safehouse Network at Rank 3. A covert campaign wants Secrecy one die ahead of Influence.
- **The standing hazard:** Ipuki reports to Meresankh. This is not a betrayal yet, it is a patron keeping books on her investment. If the party's relationship with Meresankh sours, the GM has the Informant result pre-cast.

## 6. Three Missions Ready to Launch

**The Ledger of Perit** (Acquire, danger 1, timer d4, resolving Wealth or Force). A grain-house ledger in Perit records barge cargoes that would let the Court smuggle without notice. The clerk sells copies to anyone with 25 coins and discretion. A gentle first mission: outfitting 25 coins, near-certain success, and it introduces mission mechanics plus the smuggling theme.

**Voices in the Reed Villages** (Spread Word, danger 2, timer d4, resolving Influence). Seed the marsh villages with the Court's gentler public face: a mutual-aid brotherhood, dues of a copper, protection from river toughs. Success brings 2 Influence progress or a planted rumor. A weak success's complication is the fun part: Noticed, and someone in the villages starts asking who pays for all this kindness.

**The Man Who Watched the House** (Watch, danger 2, timer d4, resolving Secrecy). A stranger has twice been seen sketching the estate from a fishing skiff. Agents shadow him. Strong success: cancel the next Trouble event, and the GM reveals him (Meresankh's second set of eyes? a creditor's man? the capital's?). Blunder: the watcher vanishes, Heat +2, and the party knows someone knows.

## 7. Growth Roadmap

A suggested arc mapping domain ranks to campaign beats. **This roadmap runs about two levels ahead of what an unassisted party affords** (see the [Economy Simulation](economy-simulation.md), §5): it assumes the patrons carry all upkeep and contribute roughly a third of growth capital across the campaign through founding gifts, stipend surplus, and gifted facilities. That dependency is the campaign's spine. If the party breaks with both patrons, the Court's growth stalls at their own treasure's pace, and they will feel exactly what the patronage was worth.

| Stage | Domain | Campaign Beat |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| Sessions 1–5 | Rank 1, 3–4 facilities, first missions | The Court finds its feet. First Exposure scare. First patron demands land |
| Sessions 6–12 | **Rank 2** (2,500). Hidden Altar, Feast Hall, Listening Post, Infirmary. Secrecy and Influence to d6 | The Court has a doctrine, a network in Perit, and a name whispered in two city-states. Recruit an Artificer (grade 2–3) as a mission-then-adventure |
| Sessions 13–20 | **Rank 3** (10,000). Safehouse Network, Atelier (Q5 crafting), Temple below ground, War Room | The Court is an organization. The patron collision arc fires. Danger 3 missions open. A faction in Kesh starts an agenda against "the marsh cult" |
| Sessions 21–30 | **Rank 4** (40,000). Grand Atelier (Q6), Web of Whispers, Garrison | The Shrouded Court rivals the powers that made it. Meresankh or Nehsi must be chosen, managed, or replaced. The Court's own agents now run danger 4 work the party once did themselves |
| Beyond | Rank 5 (150,000) if the campaign runs long | The Fabled Seat: the Court steps out of shadow, or becomes the shadow. Requires the story achievement (GDD §5): perhaps the Doctrine completed, a god's quiet sanction, or the capital's throne changing hands |

## 8. Adventure Hooks From the Machine

The domain generates these without GM invention, from its own moving parts:

1. **Rescue our people.** Any mission blunder (GDD-guaranteed rescue hook). The Court does not abandon its own, or it learns what abandoning its own costs.
2. **The patron's price.** Every obligation timer completion at a bad moment. Meresankh wants the relic the party just decided to keep.
3. **The investigator.** Exposure result 2 puts a named, competent enemy on a d6 timer. Mislead, bribe, ruin, or befriend them.
4. **Recruit the Master.** Every grade 4+ specialist is an adventure by rule. The artificer the Grand Atelier needs is indentured to a rival, or retired into the desert, or dead and the question is what knows her craft now.
5. **The rival agenda.** A faction agenda aimed at the domain (Trouble events, or the Kesh temple arc) runs on the standard dice timer, fully visible machinery the party can fight at every stage.
6. **What the workers found.** Domain event Traces 1: the House of Reeds is old, the marshes are older, and Nehsi's ancestors buried more than beehives. The occult heart of the campaign can literally be under the floor.

## 9. GM Notes for the Playtest

- **Run the Domain Turn out loud** the first three times, then hand the upkeep roll and timer ticks to a player permanently. The system is designed to be player-operated with the GM only adjudicating events.
- **Keep downtime phases to 3–5 weeks** between adventures early on. Long enough for one construction, one stat step, and one mission resolution. The machine should visibly move every time the party comes home.
- **Let one player own the sheet.** In hub-camp games, one player is always the base-builder at heart. Feed that player. The opt-in design means the others can just enjoy the Provisions and the feasts.
- **Track the playtest questions** from GDD §19 as they surface, especially pacing (question 1), Heat pressure (question 4), and whether missions get forgotten between sessions (question 5). A physical mission board with index cards is the recommended fix to test from session one.
