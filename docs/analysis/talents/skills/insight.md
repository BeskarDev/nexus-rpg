# Insight — Talent Design Workbench

> **Source:** [docs/03-statistics/06-talents/insight.md](../../../03-statistics/06-talents/insight.md) | **Main Analysis:** [TALENT_SYSTEM_ANALYSIS.md](../TALENT_SYSTEM_ANALYSIS.md)

## 1. Skill Identity

**Role Spread:** Excels: Control | Decent: Utility, Support | Weak: Offense, Defense, Healing

**Primary Archetypes:** Oracle (support), Ranger (support), Rogue (support), Shaman (support), Sorcerer (support), Summoner (support), Warlock (support)

**Identity Tags:** motive reading, social anticipation, behavioral pattern, deductive analysis, combat intuition

**Design Lens:** Insight is the mind's eye — not brute intellect but the ability to read what others reveal unconsciously through posture, micro-expression, and behavioral pattern. Its talents translate this into mechanical control: Empath, Piercing Look, and Sense of Deduction all create specific "I know something about you that gives me an edge" advantages. The skill is critically undersized at five talents for seven dependent archetypes — Oracle in particular has its entire identity (prophecy, prediction) hanging on the undesigned Foresight stub. The gap between the "Control/excellent" role rating and the actual talent pool is the largest discrepancy in the system: Insight has excellent thematic design space but almost no developed content.

## 3. Gaps & Priorities

**Signature gap:** Oracle's entire archetype identity — prophecy, omens, fate guidance — depends on Insight as a key support skill, yet Foresight (the one stub) is the only talent with any prophetic flavour and it has no rank content at all. Ranger uses Insight for tracking and quarry-reading (Monster Hunter synergy) but has no Insight-native talent supporting the "reading an animal's movement" angle. Rogue and Warlock use Insight for reading social situations and enemies but have adequate coverage through Empath and Piercing Look. The Oracle gap is the most critical.

**Role gaps:** Combat applications of Insight are almost entirely limited to Piercing Look (a once-per-scene focus talent) and the R2 combat-prediction of Empath. No talent adds Insight to Initiative rolls, despite "combat intuition" being a core fantasy of the skill. No Insight talent supports downtime activities. The deductive/analytical angle is well-covered by Sense of Deduction but the predictive/anticipatory angle (acting before something happens, not just understanding after) is almost entirely absent in the complete pool.

**System integration gaps:** Social Intrigue should heavily use Insight for motive-reading — Empath and Sense of Deduction both touch this but without explicit Social Intrigue challenge-language mechanics. No travel hook whatsoever. No Challenge interaction. Needs at minimum one talent explicitly for Social Intrigue scene play and one for the travel/exploration domain.

**Rank gaps:** No talent reaches R4. Foresight was described as a "high level talent" — it should target R4 or even Signature designation, since Oracle would invest up to Rank 5 Insight for prophetic progression. With only 5 talents total, a Rank 3 Insight character already has all content; Ranks 4 and 5 are entirely dead zones. Needs 3–6 new talents to reach the 8-talent baseline, plus R4/R5 design.

**Priority ranking for design work:**
1. Complete Foresight — the most critical stub in the system given Oracle's dependency; design as R4/Signature with concrete prediction mechanics (not open-ended "GM describes your vision")
2. Add 3 new talents to reach 8-talent baseline
3. Add a combat-initiative or reaction-speed talent (the "combat intuition" angle — Insight bonus to Initiative or first-round rolls)
4. Add a Social Intrigue-specific talent with explicit challenge-scene mechanics

## 4. Design Space

Insight's deepest unexplored territory is **prediction as resource**: the ability to spend time reading a situation or creature to bank an advantage that pays off later in the same scene. Empath and Piercing Look both grant advantages, but they activate in combat and require ongoing focus. A preparation-phase talent — spending an exploration turn studying an enemy, a social target, or a location to pre-bank a specific mechanical benefit — would be unique. "I spent twenty minutes watching how they guard the door; when we go in, I have a window" is a fantasy that no current talent captures.

Foresight as a design problem: the stub description says "supernatural insight leaning into instinctively predicting the future." The trap to avoid is open-ended "ask the GM a question" mechanics that create pressure on the GM to improvise. The right design gives the player concrete choices from bounded options — "choose one: know the highest-value target in the room, know the first enemy action next round, or know whether an NPC is planning betrayal." This is the pattern from CLAUDE.md's "give GM parameters, never open-ended GM decides" rule. Foresight should give the Oracle player specific, actionable information from a constrained menu.

### Unexplored Thematic Angles
- **Banked prediction:** A preparation-phase talent spending exploration time to study a target, banking a specific mechanical benefit (negating their next advantage, automatically succeeding on the first Insight roll against them) — the "I watched you for an hour" fantasy
- **Anticipatory defense:** An Insight-based defense boost — not Parry or Dodge but a "you can't surprise me" aura — where high Insight rank grants Initiative advantages or first-attack benefits against studied opponents
- **Social read-ahead:** A talent allowing a pre-conversation assessment that grants information about the target's negotiating position, price, or breaking point before the social roll — Insight as social preparation
- **Pattern disruption:** Sense of Deduction inverted — using knowledge of an enemy's pattern to break their rhythm, imposing a bane when they use a technique the character has already seen

### System Integration Opportunities
- **Challenges:** Investigation challenges (piecing together crime scenes, mapping a conspiracy, identifying an impersonator) are natural Insight domains; Sense of Deduction already interacts here, but a talent explicitly granting Insight as a primary challenge skill for these types would formalize the connection
- **Social Intrigue:** Motive-reading is Insight's most powerful social application; a "Read the Room" or "Beneath the Mask" talent explicitly granting advantage in Social Intrigue challenge scenes by revealing hidden motivations or stances would be the highest-impact addition
- **Travel:** No current application — but "reading terrain for danger signs" (unusual animal behaviour, tracks that suggest ambush, signs of magical disturbance) is a natural Insight travel role that could be expressed as a Scouting-adjacent talent

## 5. Talent Suggestions

### Phase 1 — Diverge: Seed Ideas

1. **Foresight** — Prescient awareness of danger at scene or encounter start, scaling to predict specific enemy actions.
2. **Read the Room** — Detect an NPC's Motivations and Pitfalls before or during a Social Intrigue challenge.
3. **Deflecting Presence** — Intercept an attack aimed at an ally by reading its trajectory and stepping into its path.
4. **Uncanny Pattern** — After observing a creature fight, exploit their behavioral predictability to disrupt repeated tactics.
5. **Studied Observer** — Spend time before a scene studying a target to bank actionable intelligence; extend Insight to Research challenges.
6. **Sixth Sense** — Add Insight to Initiative rolls; ignore the **Surprised** condition on a successful Insight check at scene start.
7. **Tell Reading** — Detect micro-signals mid-conversation to warn allies when a Pitfall is about to be triggered.
8. **Prophetic Omen** — Share a premonition with one ally, banking a reroll for them to use this scene.
9. **Cold Reading** — Quick on-the-fly Insight check when first meeting a stranger to learn one social fact (motivation type, hidden state, active lie).
10. **Pattern Lock** — After observing an enemy repeat a tactic, impose a bane on them the next time they attempt that same action.
11. **Crisis Reader** — Under threat, a fast Insight assessment that identifies the most dangerous combatant in a group and shares it with all allies.
12. **Eye of the Seer** — Capstone: enter a precognitive state to negate surprise and protect allies from predicted attacks; R5 unlocks prophetic scene preparation.
13. **Living Pattern** — The longer you observe a creature without attacking, the more powerful your next Insight-backed action becomes.
14. **Moment of Clarity** — Once per day, make three bounded statements about an imminent scene and bank them as in-scene free-action advantages.

---

### Phase 2 — Converge: Selected Talents

**Selected for full design:** Foresight, Read the Room, Deflecting Presence, Studied Observer, Eye of the Seer.

**Eliminated and why:**
- **Sixth Sense** — Initiative bonuses are squarely Perception territory ("vigilance, scan"). Negating surprise is better placed as R4 content in Eye of the Seer, which earns it via investment.
- **Tell Reading** — Absorbed into Read the Room R2 (Pitfall detection). A standalone talent with only one narrow mechanic is too thin.
- **Prophetic Omen** — Reroll-banking for allies risks colliding with Bard and Lore support talents. The ally-aid prediction angle is covered more elegantly by Foresight R3 and Eye of the Seer R5.
- **Cold Reading** — Too close to Empath (emotional reading) and Piercing Look (motive reveal on a creature). No clean separation.
- **Pattern Lock** — The core "exploit what I've seen" fantasy is captured better by Studied Observer R2–R3. Standalone version is too narrow for a full three-rank Basic talent.
- **Crisis Reader** — Partially covered by Foresight R1's danger-sense options. Insufficient depth for a dedicated talent.
- **Living Pattern** — Stacking mechanics require tracking and risk the "+skill rank" anti-pattern. The escalating-observation fantasy is handled by Studied Observer.
- **Moment of Clarity** — Absorbed into Eye of the Seer R5 as the prophecy mechanic with proper bounded-query structure.
- **Uncanny Pattern** — Redundant with Empath R2 (behavioral prediction) and Piercing Look R2 (boon/bane aura) when implemented as boon/bane. The distinctive angle — exploiting specific witnessed patterns — is captured by Studied Observer R2–R3 without a separate talent.

**Coverage check against design brief:**

| Gap | Covered By |
|-----|-----------|
| Complete Foresight (Oracle identity — most urgent) | Foresight R1–R3 |
| Social Intrigue hooks (Motivation/Pitfall/Interest) | Read the Room R1–R3 |
| Defensive redirection (§3.5 Deflecting Presence proposal) | Deflecting Presence R1–R3 |
| Challenge integration (Research + pre-scene prep) | Studied Observer R2 (always-on Research unlock) |
| High-Level talent (R4–R5) | Eye of the Seer |
| No overlap with Empath / Piercing Look / Sense of Deduction / Perception | Verified per talent below |

**Pool after additions:** 4 complete existing + 1 stub completed + 4 new = **9 talents total** — hits the 8–9 target range.

### Phase 3 — Develop: Full Talent Designs

### Foresight (Basic)

*Prescient awareness of what is about to happen — not emotional state, not past events, but the shape of the unfolding moment before it arrives.*

**Rank 1.** Once per scene, at the start of any combat encounter or when first entering a new location, you may reach out with your instincts before any other rolls are made. Roll Spirit + Insight vs. TN 8 as a free action. On a success, choose one of the following glimpses:

- **Danger Sense:** The GM confirms whether any creature in the immediate area is hidden, concealed, or in disguise. You learn only the fact — not their position or identity.
- **Strike First:** You gain +2 to your Initiative roll for this encounter.

On a failure, nothing is revealed and the ability is expended for the scene.

**Rank 2.** When you succeed on a Foresight roll (Rank 1), you may also declare one specific prediction about a creature or NPC visible to you: "They will target [ally] on their next action," or "They will attempt to deceive or misdirect before this scene ends." If your prediction proves accurate, you gain +2 Defense (situational bonus) against the predicted attack, or +1 boon on your next Insight roll in this scene — whichever applies. If inaccurate, no effect. Once per scene.

**Rank 3.** Once per scene, after observing a creature or NPC for at least one full round (they have acted at least once), roll Spirit + Insight vs. TN 10. On a success, you predict their most likely next action precisely. The GM confirms whether your prediction is correct. If it is, you immediately share your premonition — you and all allies within short range who can hear you gain +1 boon on their first roll made in direct response to that action this round.

**Archetype enablement:** Oracle (primary — this is the prophecy and foresight identity), Ranger (tactical combat preview before engaging a quarry), Shaman (spiritual premonition), Warlock (eldritch foreknowledge), Rogue (anticipating guard patterns and timing).

**Design notes:** Foresight is prescience, not analysis — it asks "is something wrong?" rather than explaining what it is. The R1 choice between Danger Sense and +2 Initiative allows both oracle-flavored and tactically-minded characters to enter effectively. R2 adds named-target prediction — still pre-emptive, not reactive. R3 is a group force-multiplier with a capped payoff: +1 boon for allies is strong but conditional on the roll and correct prediction. No overlap with Sense of Deduction (retrospective forensic analysis) or Empath (in-scene emotional reading). All bonuses are fixed values — no "+skill rank" scaling.

### Read the Room (Basic)

*The practiced art of reading a space before speaking — detecting unspoken needs, festering fears, and the raw nerves that define how a conversation will move.*

**Rank 1.** Once per Social Intrigue scene, when a Social Intrigue challenge begins or when you first approach a potentially sensitive conversation with an NPC, you may roll Spirit + Insight vs. the target's Resist. On a success, the GM reveals one of the target's Motivations. This roll does not advance the challenge die and does not count as one of your challenge actions.

**Rank 2.** When you succeed on a Read the Room roll (Rank 1), you may also ask whether the target has any Pitfalls currently relevant to the topic of discussion. The GM reveals one active Pitfall if any exist. Additionally, once per Social Intrigue scene, when any party member makes an appeal that directly targets a Motivation you identified, you may use your Quick Action to lend social weight to that appeal — they gain +1 boon on their roll.

**Rank 3.** Once per Social Intrigue scene, when a Pitfall is accidentally triggered by a party member (causing Interest to drop), you may use your reaction to attempt damage control. Roll Spirit + Insight vs. TN 8. On a success, the Interest drop is negated: your quick interjection reframes the stumble before it can fracture the relationship.

**Archetype enablement:** Rogue (infiltration and social manipulation), Oracle (reading court dynamics and hidden power structures), Warlock (detecting leverage and concealed agendas), Shaman (spirit-reading of social currents), Sorcerer (intellectual social analysis), Summoner (negotiating with powerful entities).

**Design notes:** Read the Room hooks directly into Social Intrigue's Motivation, Pitfall, and Interest mechanics (see docs/06-scenes/07-challenges/01-social-intrigue.md). It is an intelligence-gathering talent, not a re-roll or a general Influence assist — it gives the party better options for what everyone else rolls. R1 Motivation reveal means future appeals have better targets. R2 Pitfall reveal prevents Interest collapse. R3 is the emergency catch when someone stumbles anyway. None of this overlaps with Empath (emotional reading in combat and general interaction) or Piercing Look (focus-boon on one creature). TALENT_SYSTEM_ANALYSIS §5.3 identified Pitfall recovery as a critical missing mechanic — R3 directly fills that gap.

### Deflecting Presence (Basic)

*Reading the geometry of an incoming attack precisely enough to place yourself between blade and ally.*

**Rank 1.** Once per scene, when an ally within reach is targeted by a melee or ranged attack and before the attack resolves, you may use your Quick Action to interpose yourself. You become the new target of that attack. The attacker's roll is resolved against your Defense, and any damage is dealt to you.

**Rank 2.** When you use Deflecting Presence, you may also roll Spirit + Insight vs. TN 8 as part of the same Quick Action. On a success, you gain +2 Defense (situational bonus) against the redirected attack. You may now use Deflecting Presence twice per scene.

**Rank 3.** When you use Deflecting Presence and the redirected attack hits you, the attacker suffers +1 bane on all their attacks until the end of their next turn — your calculated interception disrupts their rhythm. Additionally, once per scene, if a redirected attack would reduce your ally to 0 HP, you may use Deflecting Presence as a free reaction without spending your Quick Action.

**Archetype enablement:** Oracle (calculated self-sacrifice to protect key allies), Shaman (the protective elder role), Warlord (battlefield leadership — keeping high-value allies fighting), Ranger (covering the group's retreat), Rogue (covering a partner during extraction).

**Design notes:** Implements the Deflecting Presence concept from TALENT_SYSTEM_ANALYSIS §3.5. The "redirect to yourself only" constraint is maintained throughout — you never redirect to a third party, only draw it to yourself. Activation is explicitly Quick Action at R1–R2, and a free reaction only in the specific R3 emergency condition (which has its own once-per-scene cap). The Insight flavor is not brute blocking but precise reading of the attack trajectory — you see the angle before it arrives. R3's bane is a flat, bounded debuff: +1 bane, one attacker, duration until end of their next turn. No sustained mode, no stance mechanics.

### Studied Observer (Basic)

*The intelligence gathered in careful watching before the work begins — habits, patterns, and predictable choices that become weapons in the right hands.*

**Rank 1.** Once per scene, during any scene outside of combat (exploration, travel, downtime, or the approach phase before a challenge), you may spend a delving turn observing a creature or studying a situation closely. Roll Spirit + Insight vs. TN 8. On a success, ask the GM one of the following:

- "What does this creature or person most want to avoid in this context?"
- "What habit or pattern are they unlikely to break under pressure?"
- "What is the weakest or most vulnerable point in this situation?"

The GM answers honestly and within reason.

**Rank 2.** Insight is a primary skill for Research challenges — you may use Spirit + Insight as a primary roll in any Research challenge, even if the GM did not list it as an applicable skill. Additionally, when you enter a Social Intrigue challenge having succeeded on a Studied Observer roll about the target this scene, all your rolls in that challenge gain a +1 situational bonus.

**Rank 3.** Once per scene, after succeeding on a Studied Observer roll, you may make a single broader prediction about the coming encounter or scene — a declarative statement of what will occur before it happens. At the relevant moment, the GM confirms whether your prediction was accurate. If it was, one ally of your choice gains +1 boon on their next roll in that scene.

**Archetype enablement:** Oracle (scene preparation via pre-observation before court appearances or ritual confrontations), Ranger (reading quarry behavior before a hunt), Rogue (intelligence before infiltration or a heist), Sorcerer (analytical pre-engagement), Summoner (studying entities before bargaining with them), Apothecary (behavioral study for diagnosis or poisoning assessment).

**Design notes:** Studied Observer is Insight's explicit bridge to the Challenge system. R2's Research unlock is an always-on passive — it formalizes what was thematically true (pattern recognition is research) and has zero ongoing cost. The +1 situational bonus for Social Intrigue is bounded: it only applies when prep work was done this scene, uses a bonus type that does not stack with other situational bonuses, and affects your rolls only — not party-wide. The R1 question menu is a bounded list of three options — "GM answers within reason" allows contextual honesty without open-ended narrative pressure. Distinct from Sense of Deduction: Deduction is retrospective forensic analysis ("what happened, who did it"); Studied Observer is prospective behavioral analysis ("what will they do, where will they crack").

### Eye of the Seer (High-Level)

*Prerequisite: Insight R3*

*The boundary between reading the present and glimpsing the future grows thin — perception stretches into the moments before they happen.*

**Rank 4.** Once per scene, as a Quick Action, you enter a state of heightened precognitive awareness that lasts until the end of the scene or until you become **unconscious** or **stunned**. While active:

- You cannot be caught off-guard: effects that grant enemies advantages from ambush, hidden positioning, or surprise attacks against you are negated.
- Once per round, when any creature within medium range declares an attack, you may grant one ally within short range a +2 Defense (situational bonus) against that specific attack as a free reaction — you read the trajectory before it fully resolves.

**Rank 5.** Once per day, over the course of a short rest or meditation, you access a fragment of prophetic clarity. State three specific assessments about the next scene or imminent encounter — each must be a concrete, verifiable statement (such as "There is a hidden creature in the area," "The NPC is not who they claim to be," or "The first attack will target [ally name]"). The GM answers each with "true" or "false" without further detail. For each true assessment, you may use a free action at any point during the relevant scene to share the insight aloud, granting one ally of your choice +1 boon on their next roll. You may spend each true assessment as a free action once (maximum three total). After the scene ends, you are briefly dazed as the visions recede.

**Archetype enablement:** Oracle (signature capstone — prophecy and foresight identity crystallized at mortal pinnacle), Shaman (elder's role as prophetic voice for the group), Warlock (eldritch patron-clarity and foreseen obligations).

**Design notes:** Eye of the Seer is the Oracle's capstone and the payoff for deep Insight investment. R4 solves the "Insight character in combat" problem by making their battlefield role protective and reactive — scanning threats for allies — rather than offensive. The once-per-round free reaction is bounded to specific declared attacks within medium range. R5 uses the same "GM answers true or false without nuance" mechanic established by Sense of Deduction R2, scaling it to three pre-scene assessments that can be spent as in-scene free-action boons. The prophecy is bounded: three concrete verifiable statements, not open-ended narrative prediction. The **Dazed** drawback at R5 keeps it from being purely upside — there is always a cost for looking ahead. Requires only Insight R3, keeping it accessible to all high-Insight archetypes without a second skill gate.

### Open Book (Insight, High-Level)

*At the human pinnacle of Insight, deliberate deception becomes as visible as a spoken word — micro-expressions, physiological tells, and behavioral inconsistency form an unambiguous picture.*

**Rank 4.** Once per scene, as a Quick Action, you enter a state of heightened deception-reading that lasts until the end of the scene or until you become **unconscious** or **stunned**. While active:

- When any creature within short range of you makes a deliberate lie or intentional misdirection, you automatically sense the deception — no roll required. The GM privately confirms that active deception is present, though not the specific content of the lie. Self-deception, genuine mistakes, and deeply held false beliefs do not trigger this sense.
- You gain +2 boon on all Insight rolls made to detect lies, read unstated motives, or assess the sincerity of any NPC's stated position.

**Rank 5.** Permanent. Your deception-reading is always active. Mundane attempts to conceal emotional state, hidden agenda, or active hostility toward you do not succeed — you sense them automatically during normal interaction without a roll. Magical concealment of emotion or intent is not affected. Additionally, once per day, after sensing that a creature is deceiving you, you may press for the specific lie: roll Spirit + Insight vs. their Resist. On a success, the GM reveals one concrete thing they are concealing (their actual intent, the specific false claim, or the emotion beneath their displayed state). On a failure, you sense that something deeper is hidden but cannot identify it.

**Archetype enablement:** Rogue (infiltration counter-intelligence and identifying traps in negotiations), Oracle (reading false prophecies and liars at court), Warlock (detecting concealed agendas from patrons and clients), Sorcerer (analytical truth-seeking), Shaman (discerning spirits who wear human faces), Ranger (determining whether a wounded creature is feigning).

**Capstone feeling:** "Everyone is readable. The only question is how long they pretend otherwise."

**Design notes:** R4's passive deception-sensing is bounded carefully — it detects deliberate, conscious lies only, not genuine errors or self-deception. The GM confirms "deception is present," not what the lie contains; extracting specific content requires the R5 once-per-day press. The +2 boon on Insight lie-detection rolls is strong but condition-gated (only while the state is active, once per scene). R5's always-on mundane deception-sensing is the mortal pinnacle: the character has trained their behavioral-reading to the human ceiling. The magical carveout correctly maintains the magic/mundane boundary. The once-per-day "press for the specific lie" roll vs. Resist gives a direct line to concrete content at cost (action-gated, daily limit, Resist-opposed). Distinct from Sense of Deduction (retrospective chain-of-events analysis), Empath (emotional state reading for combat prediction), and Piercing Look (personal focus boon/bane on one creature). No overlap with Perception — Open Book reads behavioral and physiological micro-signals, not environmental sensory data.

### Calculated Pressure (Insight, High-Level)

*Not emotion, not force — the clinical recognition of exactly which fear, guilt, or pride to invoke, and the precise moment to invoke it.*

**Rank 4.** Once per scene, use your Action to deliver a precisely targeted psychological challenge to one creature within short range who can hear and understand you. Roll Spirit + Insight vs. the target's Resist. On a success, choose one of the following effects:

- **Hesitation:** The creature is **dazed** until the end of their next turn.
- **Redirected Hostility:** Before the creature's next attack, they must target a creature of your designation within their normal attack range (you cannot designate yourself as the new target). The attack otherwise resolves normally.
- **Planted Doubt:** The creature suffers +1 bane on all social rolls (Influence, Insight, and Deception) for a short duration.

On a failure, the creature recognizes the attempt and is immune to Calculated Pressure for the rest of the scene.

**Rank 5.** Permanent. Your Calculated Pressure effects deepen on a Strong Success or better:

- **Hesitation** extends through the end of the following round.
- **Planted Doubt** applies to all rolls (not only social rolls) for the same short duration.

Additionally, once per scene you may use Calculated Pressure as a Quick Action instead of an Action. When used as a Quick Action, only **Hesitation** is available.

**Archetype enablement:** Rogue (social manipulation and mid-combat disruption), Warlock (eldritch psychic destabilization and patron-inspired pressure), Oracle (redirecting fate by redirecting choices in critical moments), Shaman (invoking ancestral fear and obligation), Sorcerer (calculated intellectual domination).

**Capstone feeling:** "You push the right nerve. You watch the architecture collapse."

**Design notes:** The three-effect menu ensures the talent functions across combat, social, and mixed scenes. Hesitation (Dazed) is a combat-control tool; Redirected Hostility is tactical manipulation without nullifying the attack; Planted Doubt is a social debuff. The "failure = immune" rule prevents repetitive fishing and adds meaningful risk. None of the effects overlap with Empath (boon/bane sustain via emotional prediction), Piercing Look (personal focus boon/bane), or Eye of the Seer (defense-protection and prophecy). R5's two upgrades are gated behind Strong Success, preventing them from triggering on marginal wins. The Quick Action downgrade at R5 (Hesitation only) represents mastery of a single swift psychological jab without inflating the ability beyond its once-per-scene cap. The "excluding yourself" clause in Redirected Hostility prevents the talent from functioning as personal immunity.
