# Education — Talent Design Workbench

> **Source:** [docs/03-statistics/06-talents/education.md](../../../03-statistics/06-talents/education.md) | **Main Analysis:** [TALENT_SYSTEM_ANALYSIS.md](../TALENT_SYSTEM_ANALYSIS.md)

## 1. Skill Identity

**Role Spread:** Excels: Utility | Decent: Support, Control | Weak: Offense, Defense, Healing

**Primary Archetypes:** Apothecary (support), Bard (support), Engineer (support), Magus (support), Oracle (support), Priest (support), Sorcerer (support), Summoner (support), Warlord (key support)

**Identity Tags:** applied knowledge, cultural fluency, tactical planning, research shortcut, instructive authority

**Design Lens:** Education is the conversion of knowledge into advantage — not intuition or perception, but studied understanding applied under pressure. Its talent pool correctly splits between social intelligence (Diplomat, Eloquent Talker, Linguist — knowing cultures and languages opens doors) and battlefield anticipation (Commander, Tactician — knowing theory lets you direct others and plan before contact). The unique contribution is "anticipatory power": talents that pay off when the character has invested preparation time, researching, planning, and briefing allies. The skill's design gap is in its core noun — the actual act of *education* (teaching others, transferring knowledge as a mechanical benefit) is completely absent from the pool.

## 3. Gaps & Priorities

**Signature gap:** Warlord relies on Education as a key support but needs a dedicated zone-control or formation-tactics talent — Commander targets individual allies and Tactician sets up the opening, but neither creates the sustained area-formation effects that "zone control" and "tactical commands" describe as Warlord/Hoplite identity tags. Oracle uses Education for divine scholarship and ritual knowledge but has no Education-specific signature supporting divination or ritual preparation. Engineer uses Education for theoretical knowledge but has no Engineering-Theory talent for contraption design.

**Role gaps:** Teaching and knowledge transfer — the act of *educating* others — has zero representation. No talent lets a character temporarily elevate an ally's skill capability through instruction. Research as a tactical action (spending time before an encounter to learn specific enemy weaknesses or dungeon layout) is absent. Records and documentation (ancient texts, institutional archives, historical precedent as social leverage) have zero talent expression. Needs 2–4 more talents to reach the 8-talent minimum.

**System integration gaps:** Tactician is an excellent pre-battle Challenge-adjacent talent. Diplomat and Eloquent Talker interact with Social Intrigue scenes. But no talent uses Challenge system language explicitly, and Travel has zero Education hooks despite military logistics, route planning, and cultural map knowledge all being Education-adjacent. A "Historian's Route" or "Strategic Survey" talent for Travel would be the natural fit.

**Rank gaps:** No talent reaches R4. Tactician and Commander are the strongest R4 candidates — a Master-level strategist should direct larger formations (all allies rather than one) or give multiple orders per turn. Needs at minimum 1–2 High-Level (R4–R5) talents to serve 9 archetypes at Grandmaster. A Warlord with Education 5 currently has only 6 talents to invest in.

**Priority ranking for design work:**
1. Add a zone control/formation tactics talent for Warlord/Hoplite signature — the "tactical commands" identity tag has no current home in Education or Fighting
2. Add a teaching/knowledge transfer talent — the actual education fantasy that the skill's name promises
3. Add a research or preparation talent for use in Challenge preparation phases
4. Extend Commander or Tactician to R4 per the natural progression of their mechanics

## 4. Design Space

Education's deepest unexplored territory is **knowledge as preparation resource**: a mechanic where spending time studying translates into a specific bounded advantage that cashes out in the next relevant scene. This is the "research phase" fantasy — a scholar who knows their enemy's customs, a strategist who has studied the dungeon layout, a historian who recognizes the cult's ritual timing. None of the existing talents do this at the scene-preparation level; Tactician provides pre-battle planning but requires the enemies to be known in advance. A "Research" or "Field Intelligence" talent that functions as a downtime or exploration-turn activity would be unique.

The formation-tactics gap is equally clear and equally important: Hoplite and Warlord both need a talent that creates benefits based on allies' relative positioning rather than just buffing individual actions. A "Formation Drill" or "Tactical Overlay" talent — where the character's Education rank creates an area within which their allies gain specific bonuses (resistance to being pushed, +1 on attacks while adjacent to a formation-mate) — would be mechanically distinct from everything in the current pool and directly address the zero-coverage zone control gap.

### Unexplored Thematic Angles
- **Formation tactics:** Position-based bonus for allies arranged in proximity — a talent creating an area effect based on ally arrangement rather than individual buffs; serves Hoplite and Warlord directly and fills the zone control gap
- **Research and intelligence gathering:** A downtime or exploration-turn activity that banks a specific advantage for the next relevant scene (knowledge of enemy type grants +boon on first attack against them; knowledge of location layout grants an advantage on one exploration roll)
- **Teaching transfer:** Temporarily elevating an ally's skill capability through instruction — a downtime or short-break talent that lets the character grant an ally a temporary basic skill rank or remove their untrained penalty for a specific skill during the next scene
- **Historical authority:** Using documented precedent, legal codes, or cultural records as social leverage — invoking institutional records or historical examples to gain advantage in formal social scenes; the "actually the law says..." mechanic

### System Integration Opportunities
- **Challenges:** Education is the natural preparation-phase skill for nearly every challenge type — researching a dungeon before entering, studying an enemy cult's rituals, consulting historical records before a social encounter; a talent explicitly granting a Challenge preparation bonus would be the most direct win
- **Social Intrigue:** Institutional knowledge — knowing which department handles what, which official has jurisdiction, which procedure grants access — is a form of education-based social leverage that no current talent covers; a "Procedural Mastery" or "Institutional Authority" talent would serve Warlord, Priest, and Oracle in formal social scenes
- **Travel:** Route planning based on historical maps, cultural geography knowledge, and military logistics is a natural Education travel role; a "Strategic Route" or "Historian's Map" talent granting a Navigation bonus or allowing avoidance of known-hostile territories based on historical records

## 5. Talent Suggestions

### Phase 1 — Diverge (Seed Ideas)

*12 candidate concepts across Education's design space:*

1. **Archivist** — Before a Research challenge, consult records to reduce the challenge die's starting value.
2. **Expedition Leader** — Improve the Quartermaster travel role through logistics doctrine: fewer ration checks, less Fatigue from poor camp conditions, bonus progress on strong rolls.
3. **Field Instructor** — Brief one ally during a delving turn; they gain a skill boon for the following scene.
4. **Grand Strategist** — High-level capstone: R4 tactical assessment reveals enemy state and grants party boon; R5 pre-battle briefing grants Initiative advantage and first-round re-rolls.
5. **Historical Precedent** — Invoke institutional records or legal codes as social leverage in formal Social Intrigue situations.
6. **Cartographic Memory** — Recall historical routes and maps to grant the Navigator +1 boon on travel rolls.
7. **War College** — Extended downtime training: grant one ally a temporary talent-adjacent combat benefit for the next session.
8. **Theoretical Approach** — Apply academic theory in unexpected contexts; substitute Education for a secondary skill once per challenge.
9. **Counterintelligence** — Recognize enemy information warfare patterns; predict a trap or ambush and grant the party a prepared reaction.
10. **Formal Procedure** — Navigate bureaucratic situations without error; automatically satisfy etiquette requirements in formal institutional scenes once per session.
11. **Intelligence Report** — After recalling information about an enemy type, grant allies +1 boon on their first roll against that type in the following scene.
12. **Mentor** — During a Long Rest, train one ally; for the next day they treat one skill as one rank higher for a specific declared task.

### Phase 2 — Converge (Selected Designs)

**Selection rationale:**

| # | Candidate | Gap Filled | Selection Reason |
|---|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| 1 | Archivist | Challenge integration — Research primary | Unique trigger (pre-challenge prep roll); R3 expands to all Education-adjacent challenges |
| 2 | Expedition Leader | Travel — Quartermaster role | Builds directly on the existing Mind + Education Quartermaster roll; clean 3-rank logistics arc |
| 3 | Field Instructor | Teaching / knowledge transfer | Education's name promises this; mechanically distinct from Commander (action grants) and morale talents (emotional boost) |
| 4 | Grand Strategist | Warlord High-Level capstone | Addresses analysis §8.5 proposal; R4 = encounter-shaping intelligence; R5 = session-defining pre-battle plan |

**Eliminated candidates and reasons:**

- *Historical Precedent*, *Formal Procedure*: Would encroach on Diplomat's established social-leverage design space without addressing a higher-priority gap.
- *Cartographic Memory*: Narrow (Navigator only); Expedition Leader covers travel comprehensively as a 3-rank talent.
- *War College*: Core teaching fantasy is better served by the tighter Field Instructor design. Multi-day duration would need downtime rules infrastructure not yet in the system.
- *Theoretical Approach*, *Intelligence Report*: Risk making Education a skill-substitute catch-all; the skill's unique identity is anticipatory preparation, not generic flexibility.
- *Counterintelligence*, *Mentor*: Strong concepts for a future design pass once priority gaps are filled.

### Phase 3 — Full Designs

---

#### Archivist (Basic)

*Transforms preparation time into challenge scope reduction and party briefings for Education-adjacent challenges.*

**Rank 1.** During an Exploration Turn before a Research challenge begins, you may spend the turn consulting available records, archives, or your own knowledge. Roll Mind + Education vs. the challenge's TN. On a success, the challenge die starts 1 lower than its set value (minimum value of 1).

**Rank 2.** When you use your Archivist ability and succeed, you may also designate one willing ally who will participate in the challenge. That ally may re-roll one failed roll during the challenge and take the better result.

**Rank 3.** When you use your Archivist ability and succeed, all allies participating in the challenge gain +1 boon on their first roll in that challenge. At this rank, you may use this ability before any challenge for which Education is listed as a Primary or Secondary skill, not only Research challenges.

**Archetype enablement:** Oracle (pre-ritual divination research), Sorcerer (arcane library preparation), Magus (historical records before a dungeon approach), Summoner (researching a bound entity's nature), Engineer (technical documentation before a construction challenge), Priest (sacred archive consultation before a formal negotiation).

**Design notes:** The preparation roll is gated at the same TN as the challenge itself — the tool is never trivially reliable, and the opportunity cost of an Exploration Turn is real. The die reduction at R1 is scoped to Research challenges, where Education is the primary skill and where the preparation fantasy is most fiction-consistent. R2 introduces the "help others" theme that Field Instructor expands on at a different scale. R3's expansion to all Education-applicable challenges (Social Intrigue has Education as Primary, Tracking has it as Secondary) rewards full investment without duplicating any existing talent.

---

#### Expedition Leader (Basic)

*Applies military logistics and supply doctrine to the Quartermaster travel role.*

**Rank 1.** When you fill the Quartermaster role during travel, you gain +1 boon on the Quartermaster roll. On any success, one party member of your choice does not need to make a Supply Check for rations that day.

**Rank 2.** When you fill the Quartermaster role during travel and succeed, each party member can ignore 1 Fatigue they would suffer that day from failed camp conditions (insufficient shelter, short rations, or interrupted sleep).

**Rank 3.** When you fill the Quartermaster role during travel and achieve a Strong or Critical success, the party gains +1 additional progress on the travel challenge die that day.

**Archetype enablement:** Warlord (field logistics as part of battlefield preparation), Engineer (supply chain and equipment maintenance expertise), Priest (organized sacred expedition management), Apothecary (medical supply and ration quality control), Magus (scholarly expedition planning).

**Design notes:** Expedition Leader builds directly on the Quartermaster role's existing Mind + Crafting/Education roll — no new roll type is introduced, only clear benefits for performing that role well. The R1 passive boon (+1 boon on the role roll) is within the R1 minor-always-on budget since it is conditional on filling the Quartermaster role. R1's "one party member skips Supply Check" addresses the core logistics fantasy — careful management prevents resource depletion. R2's Fatigue mitigation is the biggest quality-of-life improvement for extended travel and represents camp discipline and waste prevention. R3's +1 progress requires Strong or Critical success, rewarding dedicated investment without trivializing travel.

---

#### Field Instructor (Basic)

*Transfers explicit skill knowledge to allies through structured briefing — the teaching aspect of Education.*

**Rank 1.** During a delving turn, you instruct one willing ally in a named skill, sharing techniques and critical information relevant to what lies ahead. Until the end of the following scene, that ally gains +1 boon on their first roll with the named skill. You can't target yourself with this ability.

**Rank 2.** When you use your Field Instructor ability, you may instruct up to two allies with the same briefing. Additionally, each instructed ally may re-roll one failed roll with the named skill during the following scene.

**Rank 3.** When you use your Field Instructor ability, you may instruct up to three allies. The +1 boon now applies to all rolls the instructed allies make with the named skill during the following scene (not just the first roll). You may also use this ability as a Quick Action during a combat scene; the benefit applies to the current scene instead of the following one.

**Archetype enablement:** Warlord (pre-combat briefing on Fighting or Athletics technique), Bard (teaching Influence or Lore skills before a performance), Oracle (sharing Insight or Lore interpretation before a ritual), Priest (preparing allies for temple etiquette), Engineer (rapid-briefing on Crafting or Perception for a technical challenge).

**Design notes:** Field Instructor is distinct from Commander (Quick Action on an ally's turn granting tactical action bonuses mid-combat) and from social morale talents (emotional inspiration with no skill specificity). Field Instructor is intellectual: it requires a calm preparation window, names a specific skill, and commits to an anticipated use case — fitting for a skill whose design lens is anticipatory power. The named-skill constraint is load-bearing: the instructor must predict the upcoming challenge, which is the Education identity. The R3 Quick Action option opens a small combat-utility window (adaptive coaching mid-fight) without turning the talent into a combat talent. The boon does not scale with skill rank, only with number of targets and duration.

---

#### Grand Strategist (High-Level)

*Battlefield mastery at the highest tier — tactical intelligence that shapes encounter openings and pre-plans decisive assaults.*

**Rank 4.** Once per scene, as a Quick Action at the start of a combat scene (before the first turn resolves), you perform a rapid tactical assessment. Roll Mind + Education vs. the highest Resist among visible enemies.

- **Weak.** Choose one visible enemy: learn their current HP total and all active conditions on them.
- **Strong.** As Weak, and all allies gain +1 boon on all rolls they make against that enemy until the end of the current round.
- **Critical.** As Strong, and one ally of your choice (including yourself) may immediately act next in the initiative order, before the round continues.

**Rank 5.** Once per day, during a delving turn before a combat scene you know is approaching, you deliver a comprehensive tactical briefing to your allies. At the start of that scene, all allies who heard the briefing gain the following benefits:

- +1 boon on their Initiative rolls.
- During the first round, each such ally may re-roll one roll and take the better result.
- If you have the Tactician talent at Rank 3, each ally may also use one Tactician benefit (add your Education to the damage of one attack, or regain 2 × your Education as HP) once during the first round without requiring an additional action.

**Archetype enablement:** Warlord (primary capstone — Education 4–5 and Tactician R3 together unlock the full R5 effect), Priest (religious warfare planning and sacred ground analysis), Oracle (foresight-enhanced combat setup), Sorcerer (theoretical arcane warfare strategy), Magus (combat theory into practice mastery).

**Design notes:** Grand Strategist is a single-skill capstone (Education 4+) rather than a cross-skill prestige talent, making it accessible to any Education-invested character without a second prerequisite at R3. The R4 roll uses success levels deliberately: Weak is pure intelligence (actionable but not decisive on its own), Strong adds a round-scoped party boon (encounter-shaping within the R4 budget), Critical adds initiative manipulation (potentially decisive, but limited to one round and one ally). R5's pre-battle plan requires narrative investment (a declared delving turn) and scope constraint (only allies who heard the briefing). The Tactician synergy at R5 rewards the dedicated Warlord who has invested in both talents — Grand Strategist's R5 effectively delivers Tactician's round-1 benefits to all allies without making Tactician a hard prerequisite. Non-Tactician builds still receive the Initiative boon and round-1 re-roll, which are independently worthwhile at the session-defining tier.

## High-Level Capstone Expansion (R4–R5)

*Grand Strategist (R4–R5) was designed in Phase 3 above. Two additional High-Level capstones are needed to bring Education to exactly 3 High-Level talents.*

---

### The Theoretician (Education, High-Level, Prerequisite: Education Rank 3)

*The scholar whose accumulated knowledge lets them apply academic theory to any challenge — the living proof that understanding the principle is half of mastering the skill.*

**Rank 4.** Once per scene, when you are about to make a roll using any skill, you may declare a Theoretical Approach: briefly describe how your academic knowledge applies to this specific problem. You may substitute Mind + Education for the normal attribute + skill roll, using your Education rank in place of the other skill's rank. This substitution can be declared for any skill, including combat and physical skills. If you fail this substituted roll, you cannot use this ability again for the same type of roll this scene.

**Rank 5.** Once per day, when you use your Rank 4 ability, you gain +1 boon on the substituted roll. On a Strong or Critical success, you may immediately extend the insight to one ally within medium range: they gain +1 boon on their next roll made before the end of the scene, as your analysis illuminates the correct approach for them as well.

**Archetype enablement:** Oracle (divine theory informing any practical action), Sorcerer (theoretical magic applied to combat or social challenges), Magus (fighting informed by encyclopedic martial history), Summoner (binding theory applied to any ritual moment), Bard (academic scholarship applied to performance or social challenges), Apothecary (pharmaceutical theory applied to survival or crafting under pressure).

**Capstone feeling:** You are the scholar who has read every treatise on swordsmanship, every manuscript on divine law, every text on architecture — and when it matters most, that understanding delivers where direct experience would falter.

**Design notes:** General Education R2 already substitutes Education for any expert skill once per scene. The Theoretician breaks out of the expert-skill constraint, allowing substitution for any skill including combat (Fighting, Athletics), social (Influence, Insight), and physical (Fortitude, Perception). The narrative requirement — the player must briefly describe the theoretical framework — keeps it from being a free pass and doubles as role-playing friction the GM can rule on. R5 adds +1 boon on the daily use and a ripple effect to one ally on Strong or Critical results, giving the character a way to share their insight without making them a general-purpose re-roll factory. The once-per-day cap on the R5 version is the key limiter: R4 (once per scene) is the workhorse, R5 (once per day with a boon) is the dramatic declaration.

---

### Mentor's Legacy (Education, High-Level, Prerequisite: Education Rank 3)

*The grandmaster educator who changes people — whose teachings outlast a single scene and whose scholarly standing opens every institution in the world.*

**Rank 4.** Once per long rest, you conduct a structured teaching session lasting 1 hour. Choose one skill. Up to your Education rank in willing allies who participate gain +1 boon on all rolls with the named skill until the next long rest. This benefit is lost if the ally is incapacitated.

**Rank 5.** Your teaching leaves a mark that lasts beyond a single rest. When you use your Rank 4 ability, each participating ally gains the following additional benefit until the next long rest: once per scene, they may re-roll one failed roll with the named skill and take the better result. In addition, you are recognized as a preeminent authority in your fields of study. In any scene involving formal institutions — courts, temples, academies, guilds, or military command structures — you automatically have standing to speak and be heard. NPCs with institutional authority cannot dismiss you without first formally acknowledging your credentials, which costs them one social action before they may proceed.

**Archetype enablement:** Warlord (training allies in Fighting or Education before a campaign), Priest (religious instruction in Mysticism or Education for a ritual), Oracle (mentoring junior seers in Insight or Lore), Bard (teaching performance skills to a traveling troupe), Engineer (briefing a construction crew in Crafting before a siege project), Apothecary (instructing field medics in Nature or Crafting before a campaign).

**Capstone feeling:** You are the master of a tradition — a teacher whose students become exceptional and whose name guarantees access to every hall of power.

**Design notes:** Extends Field Instructor (Basic R1–R3) upward in both duration and depth. Field Instructor provides scene-scoped boons from a delving-turn briefing; Mentor's Legacy provides rest-scoped boons from a dedicated teaching hour. The R5 re-roll upgrade meaningfully extends benefit duration (the skill matters all day, not just for one scene) and the once-per-scene re-roll gives allies a safety net on their most important roll with that skill. The institutional standing passive does not grant Social Intrigue Interest bonuses — it only guarantees the conversation starts, not that it ends well. This keeps Mentor's Legacy distinctly separate from Diplomatic Renown (Influence) which handles the social starting advantage. Requiring Education Rank 3 as a prerequisite gates the talent appropriately: a character without real Education investment cannot teach at grandmaster level.
