# Anutep Elves — Phonetic Analysis

## Cultural Inspiration

Egyptian — the eternal kingdom of the Nile. Sun worship, monumental architecture, and a sophisticated priestly caste. Names evoke pharaonic grandeur and solar reverence.

## Tonal Identity

Regal, measured, and sun-warmed. Names flow smoothly with open vowels and gentle consonants, suggesting an ancient and refined civilisation. There is a stately cadence — unhurried and confident.

## Favoured Phonetic Arrangements

- CV and CVC syllables predominate
- Prefixes frequently begin with voiceless stops or fricatives (Kha-, Thot-, Hes-)
- Suffixes tend toward open vowels or soft consonants (-is, -em, -et, -a)
- Aspirated consonants give an airy, sunlit quality

## Typical Syllable Structure

- **Prefixes**: 1–2 syllables, CV or CVC (Im-, Seb-, Khep-)
- **Medial syllables**: CVC or CV (-men, -hot, -ket)
- **Suffixes**: V or VC (-a, -is, -et, -on)
- Overall name length: 2–4 syllables

## Key Phonemes and Sounds

- **Consonants**: /k/, /kʰ/ (kh), /t/, /θ/ (th), /s/, /n/, /m/, /p/, /r/, /h/
- **Vowels**: /e/, /a/, /i/ dominate; /o/ and /u/ are secondary
- **Characteristic clusters**: -kh-, -th-, -tep, -men, -het
- **Avoids**: harsh gutturals, heavy voiced stops, sibilant clusters

## Design Notes

Names should feel like inscriptions in warm sandstone — elegant, dignified, and timelessly regal. Favour open vowels and aspirated consonants. Avoid harsh or gritty sounds.
