# Orgon Orcs — Phonetic Analysis

## Cultural Inspiration

Greek / Hellenic — but through a warlike, volcanic lens. Think Spartan discipline mixed with Mycenaean warriors and volcanic island fortresses.

## Tonal Identity

Bold, sonorous, and martial. Names carry a Mediterranean weight with strong vowel endings and heroic rhythm. The combination of Greek phonetics with orcish aggression produces powerful, declarative names.

## Favoured Phonetic Arrangements

- CV or CVC prefixes, often with voiced stops (Tho-, Pylo-, Kres-)
- Medial syllables tend to be CV (-ra, -se, -ko)
- Suffixes strongly favour Greek-style endings (-os, -on, -eus, -ros, -ax)
- Vowel-consonant alternation creates a flowing, rhythmic pattern

## Typical Syllable Structure

- **Prefixes**: 1–2 syllables, CV or CVC (Am-, Dana-, Glau-)
- **Medial syllables**: CV or CVC (-le, -kon, -pos)
- **Suffixes**: VC or V (-os, -on, -a, -ax)
- Overall name length: 2–4 syllables

## Key Phonemes and Sounds

- **Consonants**: /k/, /t/, /r/, /s/, /l/, /n/, /m/, /p/, /d/, /θ/ (th)
- **Vowels**: /o/, /a/, /e/ dominate; open and sonorous
- **Characteristic clusters**: -th-, -kr-, -ph-, -os, -eus, -ros
- **Avoids**: sibilant clusters, nasal endings, guttural sounds

## Design Notes

Names should feel like heroes carved in marble — bold syllables, strong vowel endings, and a rhythm that demands to be shouted across a battlefield. The Greek foundation gives orcs an unexpected nobility.
