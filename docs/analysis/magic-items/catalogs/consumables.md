# Consumables — Body Parts & Natural Items Design Catalog

> **Status:** Design proposals pending owner approval. Nothing here is published.

## Category Identity

Single-use magical consumables harvested from creatures and natural sources — the direct-use complement to the crafting economy. Making these consumable (instead of permanent trinkets) avoids collision with wearable enchantments and ties straight into the published **Harvesting Creature Parts** rules (`docs/06-scenes/06-harvesting-creature-parts.md`): a harvested "Tool" result of the right creature level *is* one of these items or its raw form.

**Boundary against the alchemy systems (established 2026-07-19):**

- **Field Alchemy** (`docs/06-scenes/05-crafting-professions/01-field-alchemy.md`, published): talent-gated crafting of catalog items that expire at day's end.
- **Alchemy analysis** (`docs/analysis/alchemy-crafting-system.md`): essences as crafting *inputs*.
- **This catalog**: found/harvested items used *directly*, stable until used, no talent required. A magical consumable at a given quality must beat the same-quality field-alchemy product or offer an effect alchemy cannot make — otherwise it is dead treasure.

**Pricing** (consumable columns, verified against cost tables 2026-07-19): Q4 ≈ 350 / Q5 ≈ 1,050 / Q6 ≈ 3,050 / Q7 ≈ 10,050 coins per use. Multi-dose items multiply. Q8 consumables are relic artifacts (principle 21) — see the [Q8 artifact framework](q8-artifacts.md).

**Power budget (owner-approved two-track ruling, main analysis §3.7):** single-use consumables are **scarce access** — their effect may reach **quality-equivalent +1 spell rank** (Q4 ≈ up to R2, Q5 ≈ up to R3, Q6 ≈ up to R4). This is a ceiling, not a mandate: under-budget entries are legitimate, over-budget never.

**Quality by source:** harvested part quality follows the creature-level table (level 4 ≈ Q4, level 5–6 ≈ Q5, level 7–8 ≈ Q6). Each entry names its source part so harvest results translate directly.

### Audit Findings (2026-07-19 re-evaluation)

1. **Serpent Venom Vial was strictly worse than mundane alchemy** — a Q4 magical consumable inflicting "poisoned briefly" loses to the Q2 field-alchemy Paralyzing Poison (weak), which does the same for a short duration. Rebuilt to be clearly supernatural.
2. **Static-TN language scrubbed** — no entry may carry a "resist TN X" save (principle 9); effects are self-targeted, attack-delivered, or rolled by the user.
3. **Phoenix Ash confirmed at the right tier** — restore-at-0-HP within the round ≈ *Revivify*-class (R3), and Q6 ≈ R3. Good parity anchor for the whole table.
4. **Luck Bone reroll wording aligned with house currency** (re-roll one failed test).
5. **"Blindsight" is not a defined game term** — Bat Echolocation rewritten using sense language the conditions page actually supports.
6. **Two-track budget pass (2026-07-19, after the owner ruling):** entries re-checked against the scarce-access ceiling (quality +1 rank). Ghost Moth Dust moved Q5 → Q4 (its R2-grade invisibility now fits the Q4 scarce budget). Troll Blood Draught raised to 20 HP total (R3-grade healing fits the Q5 scarce budget and now clearly beats the Q3 field-alchemy remedy). Phoenix Ash Vial stays Q6 **deliberately under budget** — revival effects follow the resurrection rarity framework and principle 19, not the raw ceiling. All other entries sit at or under their ceiling by design.

### Entry Design Notes

- **Serpent's Kiss / Scorpion Sting** now justify their price over field alchemy: the Q4 venom resists mundane antidotes; the Q5 extract bundles staggered without a coating roll. Both stay below the Q5 field-alchemy Paralyzing Poison (strong) stun, keeping crafting investment relevant.
- **Troll Blood Draught** total healing (20 HP over two turns) sits at rank-3 healing power — the Q5 scarce-access ceiling — with the delay and the no-other-healing clamp as its costs. It now clearly out-heals the Q3 field-alchemy Herbal Remedy (12 HP instant), which a magical Q5 consumable must.
- **Wyvern Acid Sac** picks up the acid identity from the §15B rework: Durability pressure, not flat AV shaving.
- **Basilisk Eye Powder** is a narrow immunity with its circumvention built in — it answers only gaze attacks, briefly, from a consumed dose (principle 13).
- **Diviner's Knuckles** carries the bounded GM menu plus safety valve required for information effects (principle 4).
- **Ghost Moth Dust** ≈ rank 2 invisibility effect, now Q4 under the scarce-access ceiling (Q4 ≈ up to R2 for single-use items). At 350 coins it is a real tactical purchase for a level 3–4 party without trivializing stealth play (one turn in combat, one minute outside).
- **Phoenix Ash Vial** stays Q6 although the scarce ceiling would allow R3-grade effects at Q5: revival availability follows the resurrection rarity framework and the worldbuilding axis (principle 19), which override the raw budget ceiling.
- **Harvest hook:** each Source Part doubles as a harvesting "Tool" result suggestion at the matching creature level. A GM can hand the raw part and let the party pay an alchemist a preparation fee (suggested: 25% of list price) instead of finding the finished item.

### Q7 Entry Notes

- **Ichor of the Gods** is the R5-grade full restoration (Q7 scarce ceiling: single-use caps at rank 5 from Q7 up, §3.7). The source part is deliberately non-harvestable by force — divine blood *freely given* is a quest, not a loot table entry (principle 14: the limit lives in the fiction).
- **Q8**: no Q8 rows in this table. A relic consumable (the Phoenix Draught capstone — the resurrection-ceiling draught, dead within one moon) is a whole artifact in itself, built via the [Q8 artifact framework](q8-artifacts.md).
