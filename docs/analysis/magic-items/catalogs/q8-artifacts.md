# Q8 Artifacts — GM Construction Framework

> **Status:** Design proposals pending owner approval. Nothing here is published.
>
> **Tier rules (owner rulings 2026-07-19):** Q7 is the mortal crafting ceiling and lives inside the normal category tables. **Q8 is the artifact tier** — never crafted, commissioned, or bought (principle 21). Q8 items enter play only as found treasure, quest rewards, and story beats. Their coin values (~75,000–450,000) are treasure-economy valuations, not prices.

## What a Q8 Artifact Is

The model is the D&D legendary artifact (*Axe of the Dwarvish Lords*, *Eye of Vecna*): a singular named object with a history, an agenda, and mechanical weight that shapes a whole campaign arc. In Nexus terms:

- **Unique and named.** There is one. It has a story, prior bearers, and a reputation. Finding it is a milestone, not a loot roll.
- **Peak mortal power, never beyond.** Every active effect respects the R5 ceiling (quality ≈ rank; scarce single-use parts may not exceed R5 either). No reality-warping, no auto-wins.
- **Allowed to bend item rules.** An artifact may hold multiple enchantments, carry sentience, and grow with its bearer — the "exceptionally powerful items found during adventures" clause in the published enchanting rules reserves exactly this space.
- **Never unanswerable.** Every artifact has a destruction or release condition, and every immunity or ward it grants carries a circumvention (principle 13).

## Construction Procedure

Build an artifact in seven steps. Steps 1, 2, 3, and 7 are mandatory; 4–6 are optional layers for bigger artifacts.

1. **Story and identity.** Who made it, for what war or wonder, and why it is lost. The name is a promise in a pre-scientific register (principle 18) — every power chosen below must fit it.
2. **Base item and material.** Any base item with Q8 automatic bonuses (+5 weapon damage or AV, +3d Durability). Pick a legendary-tier material (Q7–Q8 range) from the materials tables for its passive identity. Artifacts ignore mundane wear: they take Durability damage only from deliberate supernatural harm or their destruction condition.
3. **Signature capstone (pick exactly one).** The power that defines the artifact — from the capstone menu below or built fresh at R5-equivalent power, always triggered or limited.
4. **Secondary powers (pick up to two).** Lesser abilities that round out the identity — from the secondary menu below. This is where the multiple-enchantment bend happens.
5. **Sentience (optional).** Communication mode, drive, and a conflict rule — from the sentience menu.
6. **Growth (optional).** Lock the capstone or a secondary power behind deeds. Limits live in the fiction (principle 14): "after the bearer avenges the axe's maker", not "at character level 9".
7. **Price and undoing (mandatory).** Choose a drawback that enacts the artifact's fiction (principle 10 — never a flat stat tax, never Resolve) or explicitly declare the artifact price-free. Then define the destruction or release condition: a specific, quest-shaped method (counterplay is non-negotiable).

## Component Menus

Mix and match. Every listed effect is R5-capped and pre-audited against the designer principles; GMs can substitute their own using the same shapes.

### Capstone Menu (pick one)

| Capstone | Fits | Effect |
|----------|------|--------|
| Earthshaker | Weapon | Once per day, Action — strike the ground: roll your attack vs. the Dodge of each creature in close range. On a hit, they take 16 blast damage (ignore 1/2 AV) and are knocked prone. The ground in the area becomes difficult terrain for a short duration. Structures and unattended objects in the area crack. |
| Heartseeker | Weapon (or ammo relic) | On a hit, increase your attack's SL by one step (max. critical). If this makes the hit a critical success and your attack roll also equals or beats the target's Resist, they suffer a Wound in addition to all other effects. Once per day. |
| Living Vestment | Armor | The armor is half-awake. Once per scene, it lashes out: roll Strength + Fortitude vs. the Resist of one creature in melee range — on a success they are grappled by the armor's tendrils (escape rolls vs. your Strength + Fortitude). Once per day, when a critical hit would inflict a Wound on you, the armor absorbs it: ignore the Wound and reduce the armor's Durability die by one step until repaired at its place of making. |
| Spellmirror | Shield, armor, or wearable | Once per day, when an enemy's spell targeting your Resist fails against you, the artifact turns it back: the caster suffers the spell's damage and effects as if they were its target. |
| Deathless Ward | Wearable or armor | Once per day, when you would drop to 0 HP, you instead remain at 1 HP. The artifact visibly cracks when this triggers and mends itself over the following night. While cracked, this ability cannot trigger again. |
| Hidden Court | Standing object (pavilion, tent, door) | Once per day, unfolded over an hour: its entrance opens into a hidden courtyard outside the world with room for up to 8 people. A night's rest inside is always a full night's rest, and nothing outside reaches in. The entrance seals until the next dawn once all guests are inside. |
| Twin Gates | Paired standing objects | Once attuned to two sites by a week-long rite at each, creatures stepping through one gate during the same round emerge from the other, once per day per direction. The gates reach across the same or a neighboring region. Moving a gate breaks the attunement. |
| Death-Knell | Instrument or bell | Once per day, rung during a short rite naming one dead person or spirit known by name: their shade manifests in close range for up to three questions. The GM answers each truthfully as the spirit would know it (one sentence, or yes/no/unknown), or the spirit refuses, bargains, or is beyond reach. The same shade cannot be called again for a year. |
| Phoenix Draught | Consumable relic | Poured over a creature dead for less than one moon whose body is mostly whole: they return to life with 1 HP. All their conditions end and their Wounds are treated (not healed). They suffer 2 Fatigue from the crossing. Consumed on use — a consumable relic is the whole artifact. |

### Secondary Power Menu (pick up to two)

1. **One Q6-tier enchantment** from the artifact's category table (this is the sanctioned multiple-enchantment bend).
2. **Foe-bane:** +1 boon on rolls against one creature type or faction tied to the artifact's story, and +4 damage against them on a hit.
3. **Warding presence:** allies in close range gain +1 Resist (enhancement bonus).
4. **Sense:** the artifact perceives one narrow thing (its foes within long range, lies spoken in its presence, the direction of its lost home) and signals by glow, heat, or hum. Information effects state what, how often, at what scope (principle 4).
5. **Voice of command:** once per scene, the bearer re-rolls a failed Influence roll against those who know the artifact's legend.
6. **Tireless bearer:** once per travel day, ignore one Fatigue from Forced March, weather, or a bad night.
7. **Sanctified light:** sheds bright light in close range on command; while lit, undead and spirits treat the light as difficult terrain to enter.

### Sentience Menu (optional — pick one from each row)

| Axis | Options |
|------|---------|
| Communication | Empathic pulses / full speech / speaks only in dreams / speaks only to the attuned bearer |
| Drive | Reclaim a lost place / destroy a named enemy line / protect a bloodline or folk / be wielded gloriously / return to its maker's god |
| Conflict rule | When the bearer acts against the drive, the artifact withholds its capstone until the bearer makes amends in line with the drive (state the concrete amends). The conflict enacts the artifact's fiction — it never taxes Resolve or unrelated stats (principle 10). |

### Price Menu (pick one, or declare price-free)

- **Oathbound:** the artifact's powers work only while the bearer upholds a stated oath (the axe defends any dwarf who asks; the crown never retreats from battle).
- **Hungering:** the capstone recharges only through a stated act (a night atop unworked stone, a kill of the foe-bane type, a day of silence).
- **Marked bearer:** the bearer is visibly known — the artifact cannot be hidden or disguised while borne, and its enemies recognize the bearer on sight.
- **Jealous:** while attuned, the bearer cannot attune any other item in the artifact's slot family.

### Undoing Menu (mandatory — pick or invent one)

- Unmade only in the forge, grove, or rite of its making.
- Consumed if used for the exact opposite of its drive (the protector-axe spilled innocent blood of its own folk).
- Surrendered: a release condition that lets the artifact *choose* to leave an unworthy bearer.
- Sundered by a named counter-artifact or by the being who made it.

## Worked Example — The Axe of the Deep Kings

*Model: Axe of the Dwarvish Lords.*

1. **Story:** forged by the last king of a fallen dwarven deep-hold to reclaim it; lost when he fell at its gates. Every dwarf smith knows its name.
2. **Base:** Q8 greataxe (+5 weapon damage, +3d Durability), legendary deep-iron (materials table). Valuation ~310,000 coins. Immune to mundane wear.
3. **Capstone:** Earthshaker.
4. **Secondary powers:** Sundering Q6 (ignore 2 AV, thrice per day) and Sense (knows the direction and rough distance of its lost hold, and hums within long range of Giants, its makers' foe).
5. **Sentience:** speaks only in dreams; drive: reclaim the deep-hold; conflict: if the bearer turns away from the reclamation, the axe withholds Earthshaker until the bearer takes a concrete step back toward the hold.
6. **Growth:** Sundering is available at once; Earthshaker wakes only after the bearer first sheds Giant blood with the axe.
7. **Price and undoing:** Oathbound — the axe serves only a bearer sworn to the reclamation before dwarven witnesses. Undoing: it can be unmade only on the anvil of the deep-hold's forge, the very place it seeks to reach.

The result reads like a campaign spine: the artifact's own drive generates the adventure that its powers exist to serve.
