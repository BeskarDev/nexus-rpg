# Sword & Sorcery Genre and Game Vibe Review — V2

> **Scope:** Does the game deliver its vibe promise? The owner's pitch: *"Bronze age sword and sorcery. My main inspiration for the game's vibe are comic books for Conan the Cimmerian. So a mix of quick and bloody action mixed with exploring foreign and weird places of ancient magic and the brutality of nature. In terms of movies and shows, a mix of Clash of the Titans, the Spartacus TV show, and the Scorpion King (2002). Although the game system itself is very different, the vibe and setting of Runequest is the closest in the TTRPG space."*
>
> **Relation to V1** ([sword-and-sorcery-genre-review.md](../_archive_/sword-and-sorcery-genre-review.md), April 2026, archived): V1 was a mechanics-focused genre audit. V2 supersedes it as the working document. It incorporates owner rulings issued in the July 2026 review session (recorded below — several V1 recommendations are now settled as rejected or reframed), re-verifies which V1 items shipped, and shifts focus to where the vibe actually reaches players: presentation surfaces, defaults, and naming.
>
> **Method note:** This review initially misjudged the equipment chapter as "medieval European, no Bronze Age content." That was wrong — the content exists (cultural weapon/armor variants, bronze-default materials, iron taboo) but lives below the fold. The corrected finding is a *presentation* gap, not a content gap, and that pattern (right content, wrong prominence) recurs across several findings.

## 1. The Promise, Decomposed

The pitch breaks into five testable claims:

| # | Claim | Verdict |
|---|-------|---------|
| P1 | **Quick and bloody action** — fights resolve in few exchanges, violence has teeth | ✅ Delivered (with a pitch-language caveat, §4.F) |
| P2 | **Foreign and weird places of ancient magic** — exploration of the strange and old | ✅ Mostly delivered; urban half missing (§4.D) |
| P3 | **The brutality of nature** — the wilderness itself is an antagonist | ✅ Delivered — the system's strongest suit |
| P4 | **Bronze Age register** — the world reads ancient, not medieval-European | ⚠️ Content present, prominence wrong (§4.A) |
| P5 | **Runequest-adjacent** — cults, culture matrix, deadly grounded combat | ✅ Holds up, chiefly via Mystic Traditions and the folk/culture matrix |

**P1 verified numerically:** a d8-Strength greataxe fighter deals 8/12/16 damage on weak/strong/critical hits against creature HP pools of roughly 15–25 at low tiers. Two to three solid hits decide most exchanges. Armor as damage reduction, durability checks, and the Wound table make the blood visible. The combat *system* is medium-crunch (initiative, Quick Actions, Combat Arts, boons/banes), but *resolution* is fast — see ruling R2.

**P3 verified:** travel roles, supply dice, extreme-climate checkpoints inflicting Fatigue, doubled ration checks in heat, Fatigue for fleeing combat, the Deprived spiral. No system reviewed here needs genre work; this is the benchmark the urban game should be held to (§4.D).

**P5 substance:** Mystic Traditions (tenets, oaths, offerings, penance) are structural cousins of Runequest cults — the offerings table (electrum sun disks, lapis lazuli, a black goat, the ransom of a captive) is the single best piece of Bronze Age texture in the rules. The folk/culture matrix (each folk split into region-anchored cultures) mirrors Glorantha's culture-first worldbuilding. Iron as a taboo, spirit-repelling metal deepens the kinship.

## 2. Settled Design Rulings (Owner, July 2026)

Recorded so future analyses do not re-litigate them.

**R1 — Short-break full-HP reset is deliberate.** HP is a per-fight resource by design; Fatigue and Wounds are the persistence layer, and that is enough. HP amounts are balanced to deplete quickly within a fight — forcing slower recovery would break that balance. V1's Recoveries proposal (V1 §3.1) is **rejected as a default rule**. Held in reserve: a resource limiting *how often per day* HP can be regained, to be introduced only if actual play shows excessive rest-chaining. Watch trigger: parties routinely stacking 4+ fights per day with zero carried-over attrition and no Fatigue/Wound accumulation.
*Corollary:* V1 §3.2 (tiered Focus recovery) is downgraded from "genre defect" to "optional hard-mode module candidate." Daily-reset resources are consistent with the ruling's design philosophy.

**R2 — "Quick" means few interactions until resolution, not a simple system.** The design target is low HP relative to damage so that any single exchange matters and fights resolve decisively. Rules weight comparable to Runequest is acceptable and expected. Pitch language should reflect this (§4.F).

**R3 — Swashbuckler and Engineer belong in the setting.** The concepts are genre-valid (Conan himself fights as a swashbuckler in several stories; engineers are ancient — siegecraft, monuments, waterworks). Any work is naming and introduction text only, not concept removal (§4.C).

**R4 — Crossbows stay.** High-tech, expensive (Q3+) options with genuine ancient precedent: the gastraphetes (~400 BC Greece, already the listed cultural variant) and mass-produced Warring States Chinese crossbows, which the Eastern Mist Realms analog supports. Their price and quality tier already position them as exotic military technology, not commonplace kit.

**R5 — The creature roster is an early demo roster.** Identity guardrails for all future bestiary work are now encoded as creature-design principle 13: mythological-first sourcing; D&D concepts importable only when naturalized — renamed (mandatory for D&D-identified names, legal minimum) and given setting-fit ecology, anatomy, or behavior. A stirge as an oversized delta mosquito fits; the *name* "stirge" and the copied dungeon register do not.

## 4. Findings and Recommendations

### 4.A Equipment Presentation — the defaults carry the vibe

**Priority: High. Effort: Low. Type: presentation pass, near-zero mechanical change.**

**Finding.** The Bronze Age content exists and is good: the cultural variant lists carry khopesh, gastraphetes, linothorax, macuahuitl, and o-yoroi; the materials system establishes bronze as the default working metal and iron as the taboo "Cursestone." But the surfaces players read first say the opposite. The main weapon and armor tables lead with Longsword, Chain Mail, and Plate Harness; the cultural lists sit below the fold as an appendix of reskins; the bronze-default/iron-taboo worldbuilding lives on a magic-item subpage (`04-equipment/07-magic-items/materials.md`) most players will never open before session five. First tables form first impressions, and the first impression is "generic fantasy."

**Owner constraint honored:** players must not face "what is that?" for every table entry. Recognizability wins ties. The recommendations below therefore change defaults only where the ancient name is *equally or more* recognizable, and use pairing everywhere else.

**Recommendations:**

1. **"Metals of the World" callout** at the top of the Weapons and Armor pages (3–4 sentences, linking to the materials page): bronze is the metal of tools, weapons, and armor; steel does not exist; iron is rare, feared, and repels spirits — some call it Cursestone. Zero lookup cost, and it single-handedly reframes every table beneath it. This is the highest vibe-per-word change available in the entire ruleset.
2. **Selective default renames** — only where recognition survives:
   - *Scimitar → Khopesh* (arguably the most recognizable Bronze Age weapon in existence; "Scimitar" moves to the cultural list).
   - *Longbow → Composite Bow* (reads chariot-age, universally understood; "Longbow" to the cultural list).
   - *Plate Harness → Bronze Panoply* (the Dendra panoply is literal Bronze Age plate armor — the rename is *more* period-correct and still self-explanatory).
   - Leave alone: Spear, Dagger, Greataxe, Club, Sling, Shortbow, Breastplate — generic terms are timeless, not medieval. Leave Chain Mail (mildly anachronistic but instantly parsed; Linothorax stays the offered variant).
3. **Dual-naming in quickstart equipment lists** — quickstarts are the #1 vibe touchpoint for new players, and their starting items already do flavor right ("Ancestor's weapon hilt," "brass knuckle duster"). Extend the pattern to gear: "Khopesh (scimitar stats)," "Elipson Axe (greataxe stats)." The mechanical anchor removes the lookup cost the owner is worried about.
4. **Optional, later:** regional kit boxes — the cultures are already region-keyed, so a sidebar per region ("A Ghahar warrior typically carries…") would let the cultural lists do worldbuilding work instead of reskin work. Fits naturally into any future folk/culture expansion.

### 4.B Bestiary Identity Iterations

**Priority: Medium. Effort: Ongoing (roster is demo, per R5). Guardrails already encoded in creature-design principle 13.**

**Finding.** The roster is split-register. On-brand: Anubite Warrior, Sphinx, Tomb Guardian, Empusa, jackals, desert vipers, giant scorpions, harpies, manticores, and the tier 8–10 Clash-of-the-Titans apex (Kraken, Primordial Titan, Demigod Champion) — keep all of that direction. Off-brand: names copied from D&D with register and legal baggage (Otyugh, Grell, Stirge, Bugbear, Kobold, Tarrasque, Hell Hound).

**Recommendations for the next bestiary pass** (apply principle 13's naturalize-don't-copy rule):

| Current | Naturalization direction |
|---|---|
| Stirge | Oversized delta mosquito or blood-wasp; name from the swamp cultures (e.g. *marsh lancet*, *vaashk bloodfly*) |
| Otyugh | Tomb/refuse scavenger of the underworld; ghul-adjacent carrion beast lairing in charnel pits |
| Grell | Keep the floating-horror niche as an aberration of the Void register; new name, new anatomy detail |
| Bugbear / Kobold | Replace with culture-specific humanoid statblocks the setting already owns (Snikrez cave-goblin raider, gnoll packs, Zakhar canyon raiders) |
| Hell Hound | Nearly fine — anchor it in Greek/underworld myth (Cerberus-spawn, temple guardian hound) and rename accordingly |
| Tarrasque | Rebuild the apex-destroyer niche from Mesopotamian myth (Tiamat's brood, an Ur-Beast of the old world) |
| Generic "Lich" / "Archfiend" | Mechanically fine; ground each in the setting (the Enlightened Lich already gestures at this) — named cults, regions, tomb-cities |

Native expansion targets that fill gaps while raising identity: lamassu, girtablilu (scorpion folk), ammit, sirrush, ushabti, roc, gorgon, mummy lords.

### 4.C Archetype Naming and Introductions

**Priority: Low. Effort: Trivial (text only). Concepts stay, per R3.**

**Finding.** Quickstart archetype names skew D&D-generic in places while the concepts underneath are setting-correct. The Swashbuckler is a street-born dirty fighter out of the Maul in Conan's Zamora — the concept is right; the *word* carries an age-of-sail register. The Engineer is an ancient siegewright and builder — concept right, intro text doesn't yet say so.

**Recommendations:**

1. *Swashbuckler* → **Bravo** (the historical term for a hired street blade; short, period-neutral, self-explanatory in context). Note the Streetwise rank 1 talent named *Swashbuckler* would rename with it.
2. *Engineer* → keep the name (it is ancient — Imhotep, Archimedes, Assyrian siege corps) but rewrite the one-line introduction to anchor it: siege engines, monument construction, canal works, temple machinery. Alternative if a rename is wanted anyway: **Siegewright**.
3. Cheap blanket fix for all 24 quickstarts: one sentence per introduction tying the archetype to a region or culture of the world ("trained in the fighting pits of…", "apprenticed to the canal-builders of…"). The Hoplite, Gladiator, Oracle, and Shaman already show how much work a name alone can do.

### 4.D Urban Play and Streetwise — the missing half of "foreign and weird places"

**Priority: High. Effort: Large, but the spec work is mostly done elsewhere.**

**Finding (confirmed by owner).** The wilderness has a full threat engine; cities have none, and Streetwise is the most underutilized skill in the game. Conan's stories alternate between wilderness and city trouble at roughly equal rates — currently only one of those registers has rules gravity.

**Recommendation: make the downtime rework the vehicle, not a new system.** The pieces are already specced:

- Settlement events, faction agendas, and complications: [downtime-system-analysis.md](../downtime-system-analysis.md) §3.2, §4.1, §4.2 and [factions/FACTION_SYSTEM_GDD.md](../factions/FACTION_SYSTEM_GDD.md).
- Social intrigue entry points: downtime analysis §3.5 (Petition/Negotiate).

Two additions to that work would close the genre gap specifically:

1. **A Heat mechanic, Streetwise-managed.** V1 §4.1's city-as-threat concept, implemented in the downtime framework's own vocabulary: a per-settlement exposure die that degrades when the party steals, kills, or draws attention (the Complication table's "Unwanted Attention" result already points here), and that triggers faction action when depleted. Streetwise lowers heat, Stealth avoids checks, Influence repairs standing. This gives Streetwise the same daily-procedure job Survival has in travel.
2. **Settlement roles, mirroring travel roles.** Travel works because everyone has a job every day (Navigator, Scout, Quartermaster…). Give settlement scenes the same shape: Fixer (Streetwise), Lookout (Perception), Broker (Influence), Ear-to-the-Ground (Insight). This is the proven pattern in this ruleset for making a skill matter — reuse it.

### 4.E Cheap Open Items From V1 Worth Closing

**Priority: Low. Effort: One-line to one-paragraph edits.**

1. "Path of the gods" flavor quote and Heroic/Epic level titles (`01-basic-rules/04-character-progression.md`) — replace with reputation vocabulary (Storied, Feared, Legendary). Text only.
2. Wound-negation → wound-treatment (Hard to Kill R2, Orc *Pride above Death*) — same short-term relief, keeps the scar. Consistent with R1: Wounds are the persistence layer, so abilities deleting them cut against the owner's own stated model.
3. Pact of Devotion instant wound-heal → wound-treat; Divine Sense R3 aura → once-per-scene single-ally extension. Same rationale.

### 4.F Pitch Language

**Priority: Low. Effort: None (external communication only).**

Per R2, "quick and bloody" invites a rules-light misread from TTRPG-literate audiences. In pitches, prefer phrasing like: *"deadly and decisive — fights resolve in a few meaningful exchanges, and what they cost you doesn't rub off"* — accurate to the design (fast resolution, Fatigue/Wound persistence) without promising a light system. The Runequest comparison already in the pitch does correct expectation-setting work; keep it.

## 5. Summary

| # | Recommendation | Priority | Effort |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4.A.1 | "Metals of the World" callout on Weapons + Armor pages | **High** | Trivial |
| 4.A.2 | Selective default renames (Khopesh, Composite Bow, Bronze Panoply) | **High** | Low |
| 4.A.3 | Dual-naming in quickstart equipment lists | **High** | Low |
| 4.D | Urban threat engine via downtime rework: Heat die + settlement roles | **High** | Large (spec exists) |
| 4.B | Bestiary naturalization iterations (principle 13 now governs) | Medium | Ongoing |
| 4.C | Archetype rename/reframe pass (Bravo; Engineer intro; region hooks) | Low | Trivial |
| 4.E | Close cheap V1 leftovers (progression flavor, wound-negation talents, Divine Sense) | Low | Trivial |
| 4.F | Calibrate external pitch language ("deadly and decisive") | Low | None |

**Bottom line.** The systems that create the vibe — travel brutality, wound persistence, dangerous magic, tradition oaths, bounded mortality, the culture matrix — are built and genre-correct. The remaining gap is almost entirely at the presentation layer (equipment defaults, creature names, archetype framing) plus one structural build-out (the urban threat engine, whose spec already exists in the downtime and faction documents). Do the three High items and the game reads like its pitch on the first page a new player opens, not just in its deep systems.
