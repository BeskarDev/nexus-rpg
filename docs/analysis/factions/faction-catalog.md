# Faction Catalog

> **Companion to:** [Faction Turns — GDD](FACTION_SYSTEM_GDD.md). Every table below is pick-or-roll, in the style of the GM tools random tables. Build a faction by walking the setup procedure (GDD §4) with this file open. On publication these tables become `<RollableTable>` components.

## 1. Type (d12)

The type frames everything after it. Each entry lists defaults: typical Might, tags that fit, and the goals such factions chase. Defaults are suggestions, not rules.

| d12 | Type | Typical Might | Leans Toward |
|-----|------|---------------|--------------|
| 1 | **Ruling Court** (palace, governor, council) | d8–d12 | Tags: Entrenched, Feared. Goals: install an ally, suppress dissent, raise a monument |
| 2 | **Temple or Priesthood** (public faith) | d6–d10 | Tags: Beloved, Zealous. Goals: spread the faith, claim sacred ground, dictate law |
| 3 | **Merchant House or Guild** | d6–d10 | Tags: Wealthy, Connected. Goals: corner a trade, break a rival, buy protection |
| 4 | **Criminal Brotherhood** | d4–d8 | Tags: Hidden, Ruthless. Goals: own a district, corrupt an official, pull one great theft |
| 5 | **Military Order or Free Company** | d6–d10 | Tags: Militant, Honorable or Ruthless. Goals: win a contract, take a stronghold, arm for war |
| 6 | **Hidden Cult** | d4–d8 | Tags: Hidden, Zealous. Goals: complete a rite, acquire a relic, place believers near power |
| 7 | **Noble House** | d6–d10 | Tags: Ancient, Connected. Goals: restore lost standing, secure a marriage or heir, ruin a rival house |
| 8 | **Tribe or Clan** (marsh, desert, hill) | d4–d8 | Tags: Beloved, Desperate or Militant. Goals: defend the land, settle a feud, win grazing or water rights |
| 9 | **Scholarly Circle** (archive, academy, seers) | d4–d8 | Tags: Prophetic, Connected. Goals: recover lost knowledge, decode an omen, gain a patron |
| 10 | **Rebels or Dissidents** | d4–d8 | Tags: Desperate, Beloved or Hidden. Goals: survive the purge, win the streets, strike the symbol of power |
| 11 | **Foreign Power** (embassy, agents, vanguard) | d8–d12 | Tags: Hidden, Wealthy. Goals: map the defenses, buy allies, prepare the ground |
| 12 | **The Other** (beast cult, undead court, spirit pact, awakened ruin) | d4–d12 | Tags: Feared, Prophetic. Goals: reclaim what was theirs, feed, be remembered |

## 1b. Drive (d12)

The want beneath every goal. Drive is the faction's compass, not a mechanic: it tells the GM which goal to pick next, how the faction fights, and what it will never trade away. When two goals would fit, pick the one the drive demands. When roleplaying the faction's people, the drive is what shows in their eyes.

| d12 | Drive | They Will... | They Will Never... |
|-----|-------|--------------|--------------------|
| 1 | **Supremacy** | Sacrifice allies to stand alone at the top | Accept being second, even profitably |
| 2 | **Survival** | Do anything, ally with anyone | Trust that the danger has passed |
| 3 | **Revenge** | Wait years and smile while waiting | Forgive, even when forgiving would win |
| 4 | **Faith fulfilled** | Spend lives on the promise of their creed | Question the promise itself |
| 5 | **Wealth as scoreboard** | Sell to both sides of any war | Leave coin on the table for principle |
| 6 | **Restoration** | Chase what was lost past all reason | Admit the old days are gone |
| 7 | **Recognition** | Overreach for a seat at the high table | Endure an insult quietly |
| 8 | **Protect their own** | Burn the world to shield their people | Spend their people as coin |
| 9 | **Forbidden knowledge** | Dig where digging is death | Destroy what they could study |
| 10 | **Dread of prophecy** | Commit atrocities to avert what they fear | Speak plainly about what they saw |
| 11 | **Loyalty to one person** | Follow their star into ruin | Outlive that person's fall unchanged |
| 12 | **Inhuman hunger** | Pursue a logic no mortal court shares | Want what mortals assume they want |

> Drive and Weakness together are the faction's inner life: the drive is what they run toward, the weakness is what runs after them. The best factions have a drive their weakness directly threatens (a *Restoration* house with a *rival claimant abroad*, a *Faith fulfilled* temple with a *hidden heresy*).

## 2. Might (guidance)

One die, d4 to d12. Match the tier of opposition the faction's rank and file would present as an encounter, or the widest reach the faction can credibly exert.

| Might | Reach | Examples |
|-------|-------|----------|
| d4 | A street, a village, a single den | Alley gang, village shrine circle, a smuggler family |
| d6 | A district, a town, a road | Town guild, bandit company, minor noble house, river cult |
| d8 | A city, a valley, a trade route | City temple, thieves' court, veteran free company, proud noble house |
| d10 | A city-state and its hinterland | High priesthood, merchant league, the palace guard, a governing dynasty |
| d12 | Several city-states, a civilization's institutions | The great temple of a god, a conquering crown, a league of cities |

## 3. Tags (pick two from different groups, or roll d6 twice)

Each tag is one clause. Boons apply to faction turn and opposed rolls where the element plainly fits, maximum one tag boon per roll.

### Group A — Means (how they work)

| d6 | Tag | Clause |
|----|-----|--------|
| 1 | **Militant** | +1 boon on goals and collisions pursued by force. |
| 2 | **Wealthy** | +1 boon on goals and collisions pursued with coin. |
| 3 | **Hidden** | May suppress their sign each turn. Anyone investigating them after a suppressed sign gains +1 boon (silence is evidence). |
| 4 | **Connected** | +1 boon on goals and collisions pursued through favors and third parties. Once per turn, may shift one backstage faction's disposition by 1 step. |
| 5 | **Prophetic** | Once per turn, the GM reveals to them (in fiction) one other faction's current goal. +1 boon when acting on it. |
| 6 | **Beloved** | +1 boon on goals pursued in the open with popular support. Complications that would turn commonfolk against them soften by one step of severity (GM adjusts). |

### Group B — Temper (how they behave)

| d6 | Tag | Clause |
|----|-----|--------|
| 1 | **Zealous** | +1 boon when the goal serves their creed. On a blunder, the complication is always Weakness flares. |
| 2 | **Ruthless** | Their Complication results of Blood price strike others, not them: the named casualty belongs to a rival or bystander. Dispositions with third parties worsen one step faster. |
| 3 | **Honorable** | Other factions and NPCs deal with them at +1 disposition step for agreements. They never take the Exposure complication (nothing shameful to expose), but cannot suppress signs. |
| 4 | **Desperate** | +1 boon on survival and recovery goals. All their clocks are one size smaller (minimum d4): they move fast because they must. |
| 5 | **Patient** | Their clocks are one size larger, and they ignore the first complication rolled against them each act (they absorb the loss and wait). |
| 6 | **Scheming** | +1 boon in collisions they initiated. If they lose a collision they initiated, the complication is always Exposure. |

### Group C — Standing (how the world holds them)

| d6 | Tag | Clause |
|----|-----|--------|
| 1 | **Ancient** | Their Weakness is common knowledge (no discovery needed), but once per act they may declare an old debt: one NPC or backstage faction owes them a favor now called in. |
| 2 | **Entrenched** | +1 boon in collisions where they defend what they already hold. |
| 3 | **Feared** | Factions of lower Might suffer +1 bane initiating collisions against them. Their signs are always noticed. |
| 4 | **Sprawling** | May hold one extra asset above their Might bracket. Losing assets never steps their Might down until the last one. |
| 5 | **Upstart** | Steps Might up on any completed goal (not only clock ≥ Might), but their Weakness fires on any failure result, not only blunders. |
| 6 | **Overextended** | Rolls +1 bane on turn rolls, but holds two goal clocks at once (the only exception to one clock per faction). GM's tool for empires in decline. |

## 4. Assets (by Might: d4 holds 1, d6–d8 hold 2, d10–d12 hold 3)

Pick or roll a group (d3), then d6 within. An asset grants +1 boon where it plainly applies (maximum one asset boon per roll) and is the default thing lost to complications. Every asset is concrete: name it, place it.

### Group 1 — Muscle

| d6 | Asset |
|----|-------|
| 1 | Mercenary band or war-sworn kin |
| 2 | Stronghold, fortified compound, or walled temple |
| 3 | War beasts, chariots, or river war-canoes |
| 4 | A champion (named, statted when needed) |
| 5 | Armory of bronze and good iron |
| 6 | Watchful patrols and paid street muscle |

### Group 2 — Coin

| d6 | Asset |
|----|-------|
| 1 | Trade fleet or caravan line |
| 2 | Granaries and storehouses |
| 3 | Mine, quarry, or salt pans |
| 4 | Counting house and moneylenders |
| 5 | Monopoly charter or toll rights |
| 6 | A hoard (portable, hidden, and stealable) |

### Group 3 — Whispers

| d6 | Asset |
|----|-------|
| 1 | Spy ring or web of informants |
| 2 | Blackmail archive (letters, ledgers, confessions) |
| 3 | Oracle, seer, or dream-reader |
| 4 | Smuggling routes and safe houses |
| 5 | Friendly officials in useful offices |
| 6 | Sacred relic or famous banner (draws believers and legitimacy) |

## 5. Weakness (d12, keep secret)

Fires on blunders and lost collisions (Complication 2). Players who uncover it gain +1 boon on rolls exploiting it.

| d12 | Weakness |
|-----|----------|
| 1 | **Succession dispute.** Two claimants, one seat, knives out. |
| 2 | **Crippling debt.** Someone owns their promises, and may call them. |
| 3 | **Hidden heresy.** Their faith or charter is not what they profess. |
| 4 | **A mole inside.** Someone reports to a rival (GM decides to whom when it fires). |
| 5 | **Blood feud.** An old enmity overrides their judgment when invoked. |
| 6 | **Dependent on a patron.** Their strength is borrowed and can be recalled. |
| 7 | **Bound by omen.** A prophecy or taboo dictates what they cannot do, and it is exploitable. |
| 8 | **Overextended lines.** Their reach exceeds their grip: one asset is always undefended. |
| 9 | **Leader's vice.** Gambling, cruelty, love, or the lotus: the head is compromised. |
| 10 | **Restless ranks.** The rank and file want something the leadership refuses. |
| 11 | **Dark secret.** One buried deed that would turn allies to enemies overnight. |
| 12 | **Rival claimant abroad.** Somewhere lives someone with a better claim to what they hold. |

## 6. Goals (d12) & Clock Size

Roll or pick the goal, make it concrete (name the target), then write the **stakes sentence**: "when this clock fills, X." A goal without a named target and stakes is not done.

| d12 | Goal | Example Stakes |
|-----|------|----------------|
| 1 | **Seize a place** (ford, district, shrine, mine) | "The Copper Gate district answers to the Brotherhood." |
| 2 | **Corner a trade** (grain, salt, bronze, passage) | "No salt moves without their seal and their price." |
| 3 | **Install an ally** (office, temple seat, marriage) | "The new harbormaster is theirs." |
| 4 | **Convert or suppress** (faith, custom, movement) | "The river rites are outlawed in the city." |
| 5 | **Arm for war** (recruit, fortify, stockpile) | "They field twice the spears they did, openly." |
| 6 | **Pull a secret operation** (theft, assassination, forgery) | "The treaty tablet in the archive is a forgery, and theirs." |
| 7 | **Acquire a relic or knowledge** | "They hold the burial maps of the old necropolis." |
| 8 | **Destroy a rival** (faction, house, leader) | "The River League's charter is revoked and its factors scattered." |
| 9 | **Survive a threat** (purge, siege, debt, curse) | "The Inquiry leaves, having found nothing." |
| 10 | **Restore lost glory** (title, land, name) | "The house's old seat and seat at council are returned." |
| 11 | **Forge an alliance** (factions, cities, powers) | "Temple and crown act as one hand in the delta." |
| 12 | **Prepare the ground** (map, infiltrate, seed agents) | "When their masters move, every gate has a friend at it." |

**Clock size by scope:**

| Clock | Scope | Feels Like |
|-------|-------|------------|
| d4 | Minor scheme | A move within a district, done within an act |
| d6 | Significant venture | A campaign-act arc, the default |
| d8 | Major undertaking | Shapes a whole campaign phase |
| d10 | Transformative work | Redraws the region's board |
| d12 | Generational design | The impossible dream, background of an entire campaign |

## 7. Signs (d12)

One per faction that moved. Concrete, perceivable, unexplained.

| d12 | Sign | Players Might Notice |
|-----|------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Market move** | A price doubles or halves, a good vanishes from stalls, new coin circulates |
| 2 | **Steel in the streets** | New guard postings, mercenaries drinking on someone's coin, weapon prices rise |
| 3 | **Procession or rite** | Public ceremony, new banners, an altar raised or torn down |
| 4 | **Disappearance** | A known NPC stops appearing, a shop shutters, a boat never docks |
| 5 | **New faces** | Strangers asking questions, laborers from elsewhere, an envoy's retinue |
| 6 | **Letters and seals** | A messenger at odd hours, a posted decree, a summons delivered |
| 7 | **Construction** | Fortification, a warehouse rising fast, a road crew where no road was planned |
| 8 | **Charity or festival** | Bread given freely, debts forgiven, a feast with an agenda |
| 9 | **Aftermath** | A burned barge, a beaten factor, mourning in a quarter, blood by the gate |
| 10 | **Court and law** | An arrest, a trial, a tax collector with new instructions |
| 11 | **Word on the wind** | The same rumor from three mouths, a song with new verses, a name suddenly unspoken |
| 12 | **Silence** | Something withdrawn: patrols gone, a voice missing from council, an empty shrine |

## 8. Entanglements (d12, roll once per pair of active factions)

Dispositions say how factions feel about each other. Entanglements say **why**, and why it can get worse. Roll or pick one per pair when building the roster. Every entanglement is a pre-loaded collision, leverage point, or hook the players can pull.

| d12 | Entanglement | The Texture |
|-----|--------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Old blood.** A past war, raid, or execution stands between them. | Any collision between them escalates: ties do not hold, someone always pushes. |
| 2 | **Secret debt.** One quietly owes the other: coin, a life, a buried favor. | The creditor can call it once, as one free tick or one canceled complication. Then it is spent, and resented. |
| 3 | **Bound by marriage or oath.** A formal tie neither fully wants. | Open collision between them costs both a disposition step with third parties (oath-breaking is seen). |
| 4 | **Shared enemy.** They cooperate against a third power, teeth gritted. | While that enemy stands, no collisions between them. The day it falls, roll a new entanglement. |
| 5 | **Broken promise.** An alliance, betrothal, or contract one side abandoned. | The wronged side gains +1 boon in collisions against the other, fueled by righteousness. |
| 6 | **Trade dependence.** One cannot function without something the other supplies. | Cutting the supply is a d4 goal for the supplier and a survival crisis for the dependent. |
| 7 | **Schism.** They were one faction once, split over creed, blood, or spoils. | Each knows the other's Weakness. Both deny the kinship in public. |
| 8 | **Infiltrated.** One has an agent placed inside the other (GM decides who and how deep). | When the infiltrated faction blunders, the infiltrator may claim the complication result as their doing. |
| 9 | **Rivals for one patron.** Both court the same power above them. | Every completed goal by one worsens the patron's disposition toward the other by 1 step. |
| 10 | **Public allies, private rivals.** Smiles at festivals, knives in ledgers. | Their collisions must produce deniable signs (sabotage, proxies, silence), never open ones. |
| 11 | **One does not see the other.** A power gap or a blind spot: the greater faction has not noticed the lesser. | The unseen faction acts at +1 boon against them until their first sign lands, then this becomes Old blood or Broken promise. |
| 12 | **Something between them.** A place, relic, person, or claim both believe is rightfully theirs. | Neither can complete a goal touching it without a collision. The thing itself is a hook the party can hold. |

## 9. The Face (d6, one named NPC per faction)

Factions are felt through people. Give every active faction one **face**: the NPC through whom the players experience it. Roll or pick the archetype, then use the existing [NPC generator](../../10-gm-tools/01-random-tables/11-social-intrigue.mdx) tables for name, manner, and detail. The face is a person, not the faction: they can be reasoned with, corrupted, protected, or mourned, and dispositions with the face (via Tend to Relationships and social intrigue) are the party's handle on the whole faction.

| d6 | The Face | The Crack in Them |
|----|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | **The fanatic visionary.** Speaks for the drive in its purest form. | Cannot compromise, and knows the faction sometimes must. |
| 2 | **The weary pragmatist.** Keeps the machine running despite the leadership. | Would trade the drive itself for one quiet year. |
| 3 | **The ambitious second.** Loyal today. Watching the seat above. | The party can serve or spoil the ambition, and they know it. |
| 4 | **The figurehead.** The public face. The true will sits behind a curtain. | Discovering who actually decides is worth a whole goal clock. |
| 5 | **The beloved elder.** Their standing IS the faction's standing. | Aging, tired, and the succession is the Weakness waiting. |
| 6 | **The newcomer with everything to prove.** Fresh authority, no roots. | Overreaches on the faction's behalf. Blunders wear their face. |

> **Future work (GM random tables):** faction names by folk and region, regional dressing for signs, and expanded face detail belong in the `docs/10-gm-tools/01-random-tables/` section on publication, alongside the settlement and NPC generators. The catalog stays the mechanical core.
