## Request Alignment

## Patience (Challenge Die)

Roll to set how long the NPC is willing to engage. This is the challenge die, and it advances on every roll (success or failure), so a small die means a short, pressured conversation.

## NPC Pitfalls

Roll once or twice to generate Pitfalls. Triggering one lowers Interest by ‚àí1. If Interest reaches ‚àí2 or lower, the intrigue ends in a Breakdown.

<RollableTable>
| d20 | Pitfall | Trigger |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | *Threat: Past crimes* | Implying you know something they want hidden, even subtly. It reads as blackmail |
| 2 | *Threat: Rival leverage* | Mentioning their rivals in a way that sounds like a deal or a warning |
| 3 | *Insult: Underestimating them* | Suggesting the task is easy or beneath their usual standard |
| 4 | *Insult: Doubting their word* | Questioning their honesty or asking for excessive proof |
| 5 | *Insult: Unfavorable comparison* | Naming someone else who could do the job better |
| 6 | *Insult: Dismissing their judgment* | Overriding or second-guessing a decision they've clearly already made |
| 7 | *Taboo: Forbidden topic* | Raising a subject they consider deeply private, sacred, or shameful |
| 8 | *Taboo: Cultural violation* | A gesture, phrase, or act that breaches a local custom they hold seriously |
| 9 | *Rival: Hated faction* | Invoking the name of a group they despise, even in passing |
| 10 | *Rival: Personal enemy* | Associating the request with someone they have bad history with |
| 11 | *Fear trigger: Exposure* | Describing the plan in a way that highlights their legal or social risk |
| 12 | *Pride wound: Old failure* | Referencing a past mistake or defeat, however unintentionally |
| 13 | *Loyalty conflict: Prior oath* | The request conflicts with a promise or obligation they've already made |
| 14 | *Overconfidence: Presuming agreement* | Acting as if the deal is done before they've committed to anything |
| 15 | *Rushed decision: Pressing for an answer* | Demanding a response before they feel ready to give one |
| 16 | *Status error: Misreading their rank* | Treating them as a middleman when they're the decision-maker, or the reverse |
| 17 | *Unwanted witness: Unexpected company* | Bringing someone along the NPC didn't expect and doesn't trust |
| 18 | *Revealed alternatives: You tried someone else* | Letting slip that you approached another party before coming to them |
| 19 | *Uncomfortable truth: Pointing out their weakness* | Highlighting a vulnerability in a way that feels like gloating rather than honesty |
| 20 | *Social contract: Breaking an implicit rule* | Violating an unspoken expectation of the setting, such as speaking out of turn in a formal gathering |
</RollableTable>
