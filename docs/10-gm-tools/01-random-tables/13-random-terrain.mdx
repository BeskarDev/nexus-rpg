# 🏔️ Terrain & Locations

<TerrainAutoRollerWrapper />

## Overview

Use this tool to generate flavorful **terrain features** and **locations** for travel, exploration, or adventure scenes. Choose a category and roll to produce a ready-to-use description you can drop into your game.

- **Natural Terrain:** Mundane landforms and environmental features — wadis, ridges, marshlands, and the details that make them memorable.
- **Supernatural Terrain:** Terrain touched by magic, spirits, or unexplained forces — places where the natural world behaves strangely.
- **Mundane Location:** Built or established sites in their physical state — ruins, outposts, tombs, forges, and other discoverable places.
- **Supernatural Location:** Sites of ritual, divine, or arcane significance — places where something beyond mortal hands has left its mark.

### How to Use

1. **Choose a category** — Natural Terrain, Supernatural Terrain, Mundane Location, or Supernatural Location.
2. **Roll** — the generator fills all blanks from the tables below.
3. **Add campaign detail** — replace abstract fragments with specific names, creatures, and places from your setting.

### Generating Multiple Results

When preparing a hex, region, or travel route, generate several results at once. Mix categories for variety:

- A **Natural Terrain** result for the dominant landscape.
- A **Supernatural Terrain** result for an unusual area the party may stumble upon.
- A **Mundane Location** result for a discoverable site with potential for interaction.
- A **Supernatural Location** result for a place tied to the mythic or divine.

---

## Sentence Seeds

Each category uses a template sentence. The generator fills the blanks from the d66 tables below.

- **Natural Terrain:** *"A/An [theme] [feature] with [detail]."*
- **Supernatural Terrain:** *"A/An [theme] [feature] with [detail]."*
- **Mundane Location:** *"A/An [theme] [feature] with [detail]."*
- **Supernatural Location:** *"A/An [theme] [feature] with [detail]."*

## Extending the Tables

Each table is a d66 grid (36 entries). To add new content:

1. Create a **themed variant** of any table — scoped to an environment, faction, or tone — and swap it in at the GM's discretion.
2. New variant tables use the same d66 grid format and follow the entry guidelines below.

### Entry Guidelines

| Table | Style |
|---|---|
| Theme | Adjective or descriptor — evocative but short (1–2 words) |
| Feature | Noun — a landform, structure type, or natural landmark |
| Detail | Concrete, observable detail — something characters can see, hear, smell, or touch |
