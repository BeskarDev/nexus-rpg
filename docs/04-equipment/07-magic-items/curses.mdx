# Cursed Items

Not every magic item is a gift. Some carry a curse, a malignant will bound into them alongside their power. A curse does not simply harm its bearer. It **demands** something of them, and it makes them pay when the demand goes unmet. Curses are a tool for the GM to turn found treasure into a gamble and to press a character into choices they would not otherwise make.

## When an Item Is Cursed

Only a **permanent item you equip or wield** can be cursed, such as a weapon, a piece of armor or a shield, or a worn item. Consumables and single-use items cannot.

Items you craft or commission are never cursed. You know exactly what you made. Only treasure **found as loot** might carry a curse.

There is no fixed chance. When preparing loot, or in the moment at the table, the GM decides whether a found item is cursed and which curse it bears. The table below and the **Magic Item Curses** tables in the treasure section are a guide, roll on them for inspiration or choose what fits the story.

## Revealing and Binding

A curse stays hidden until the item is first attuned or meaningfully used, usually after a short break spent with it or the first scene it is worn or wielded. The **Identify** spell and the **Identify Artifact** talent can reveal it sooner, telling you that a curse exists on a strong reading and exactly how it works on a critical one.

Once a curse takes hold, the item **cannot be unequipped, discarded, sold, or given away** until the curse is lifted. Forced apart from its bearer, it finds its way back to them.

## Living With and Breaking a Curse

Each curse states one flavorful demand in italics, then its rules plainly. Obeying the demand grants a small benefit. Defying it exacts a price. Apply each benefit and price once per the interval it names.

The **Break Curse** spell does not destroy an item's curse. Cast on the bearer, it suppresses the curse for a short duration, long enough to use the item without its price, or to remove the item and set it aside or hand it to another without the curse fighting back. A cursed item is only truly freed by **destroying it** or by **fulfilling its release condition**, if it has one (the GM decides).
