The ritual consumes the inscribed materials, worth 5000 coins, whether or not it succeeds.<br/>On a success, a shell of solidified force closes over the creature and they fall into a deathless slumber. While the seal holds, they do not age, breathe, eat, or dream, and no effect from outside reaches them through the shell. The shell is immovable and anchored to the place of its sealing. It can be attacked and destroyed from outside like any other object. It has 14 Parry, 14 Dodge, 12 AV, and 80 HP. A casting that ends spells destroys it.<br/>At the end of each moon, the sealed creature's spirit strains against the seal. They roll Spirit + Fortitude vs. your Resist. On a success, the shell shatters. The seal lasts for one year, then dissolves on its own. When the shell is destroyed or the seal ends, the creature wakes immediately. You can renew a seal before it fades by casting this spell again on the sealed creature. 

### Planar Gateway

**Rank** | **Focus** | **Target** | **Range** | **Properties**
---|---|---|---|---
 5 | 10 | Legendary (16) | Short | ritual (minutes),  concentrate,  material (attuned object),  material cost (5000 coins)

**Effect** <br/> You trace a ring of burning sigils in the air and tear space open along its line. You must name your destination as you begin: either a place you have visited before within the same sphere of existence, or another sphere of existence. To open the way into another sphere, you must hold a physical object attuned to that sphere of Quality 6 or higher, such as a relic carried out of it, an idol consecrated to its powers, or a shard of its very substance. The object is not consumed. The ritual consumes engraved sigil materials of precious metals worth 5000 coins, whether or not it succeeds.<br/>On a success, a gateway tall and wide enough for a large creature tears open at the target location. It shows the destination through its frame, and it is open from both sides. Anything can pass through in either direction while it holds. The gateway remains open while you concentrate, for up to a short duration, then seals without a trace.<br/>A destination within the same sphere of existence is exact. If the destination lies in another sphere, you do not choose the spot. The gate opens at one of the following, chosen by the GM to fit the attuned object: a known threshold between the spheres, a landmark tied to the object's origin, or the place in that sphere most attuned to it. 

### Subjugation

**Rank** | **Focus** | **Target** | **Range** | **Properties**
---|---|---|---|---
 5 | 10 | Legendary (16) | Touch | ritual (hours),  material cost (5000 coins),  singular

**Effect** <br/> You inscribe a binding circle on solid ground and call an entity out of the spheres beyond, chaining them to service. Name the service you require as you cast (such as guarding a place, fighting your enemies, carrying you and your companions over land or sea, raising or razing a great work, or retrieving something from where only they can go). The ritual consumes offerings and inscribed materials worth 5000 coins, whether or not it succeeds.<br/>On a success, an entity appears within the circle. They have the statistics of a tier 7 creature of the Aberration or Spirit type, chosen and played by the GM to fit the service you named. The binding lasts for one day. While it holds, the entity cannot refuse your spoken commands and cannot harm you or your allies, but they follow the exact wording of each command and take every freedom your wording leaves them, for they serve with resentment. They must stay within a day's march of the circle. In combat, roll initiative for them. The GM controls their actions within your commands.<br/>The circle is a physical inscription. Scratching it open, breaking the ground, or a casting that ends spells destroys it. If the circle is destroyed, or you fall unconscious or die, the binding breaks early. A freed entity keeps their own will. Most turn on their binder or abandon the task and return to their home sphere. When the binding ends, the entity returns to their home sphere in an instant.
