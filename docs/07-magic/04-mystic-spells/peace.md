# Peace

### Aura of Sanctuary

**Rank** | **Focus** | **Target** | **Range** | **Properties**
---|---|---|---|---
 0 | 0 | Medium (8) | Medium | quick,  singular

**Effect** <br/> You shield a creature in range with a magical aura discouraging other creatures from inflicting harm on them. Cast this spell as a Quick Action on your turn.<br/>On a success, the target is shielded by the aura for a short duration. While shielded by the aura, whenever another creature attempts to attack them, the attacker has to roll Spirit + Fortitude vs. your Resist. On a failure, they are unable to attack the shielded creature and must choose a new target.<br/>This spell ends early, if the shielded creature attacks any other creature.

---|---|---|---|---

**Effect** <br/> Over hours of consecration you anoint a doorway, arch, or gate with sacred oils, binding it in covenant to its one distant twin.<br/>Each threshold is consecrated separately with its own casting of this spell, and each casting consumes 1500 coins worth of sacred oils and offerings.<br/>On a success, the threshold is consecrated and bound to its twin. ### Sacred Covenant

**Rank** | **Focus** | **Target** | **Range** | **Properties**
---|---|---|---|---
 5 | 10 | Legendary (16) | Touch | ritual (hours),  singular,  material cost (5000 coins)

**Effect** <br/> Over hours of rite and offering you bind a place to the law of the gods, anointing its threshold and its stones with oil, ash, and the blood of a sacrificed bull. The rite consumes 5000 coins worth of oils, offerings, and sacrifices whether or not it succeeds.<br/>On a success, the covenant settles over a single temple, palace, village, or other bounded site up to a thousand paces across, and holds for one season. You may maintain only one Sacred Covenant at a time. During the rite, you may name any number of keepers of the ground. While the covenant holds, apply the following effects within the covenant ground:<br/>- When a creature freely swears an oath aloud within the ground, the covenant seals it before the gods. Any creature who breaks a sealed oath is branded with a sigil of broken faith, plain to see upon their brow, and suffers +1 bane on all rolls while the brand lasts. The brand only fades when they atone for the breaking at the covenant ground.<br/>- When a creature sheds blood within the ground or defiles its stones and altars, the wrath of the covenant strikes them for 8 psychic damage (ignore AV) and they are branded with the sigil of broken faith as if they had broken a sealed oath.<br/>- When a creature makes an attack within the ground, they must roll Spirit + Fortitude vs. your Resist or be paralyzed for a short duration as the weight of the covenant presses down on them. They can repeat the roll at the end of their turns to end the effect early.<br/>- You and the named keepers know the moment the covenant ground is defiled by bloodshed, desecration, or the breaking of a sealed oath, wherever you are within the same sphere of existence, and you know which of the three it was.<br/>If you recast this spell upon the same ground each time the season turns, four seasons in a row, the covenant becomes permanent. A permanent covenant needs no further renewal and no longer counts as the one covenant you may maintain.<br/>The covenant can be undone by a rite of equal weight, by the ground's destruction, or by an effect that ends spells, but only if that effect is cast at rank 5.

> **GM framing note.** Sacred Covenant is a temple rite, not a courtroom instrument. It binds oaths sworn *within* the covenant ground and brands those who break faith, but it compels no confession, forces no answer, and reaches no farther than the bound site. It is meant to make a holy place somewhere vows carry weight and violence is unwelcome, never to industrialize law. Atonement takes one of these forms, chosen to fit the breaking: making the broken oath good, restitution or blood-price paid to the wronged, a sacrifice of matching worth offered at the ground, or, when none of these fit, a season of service to the covenant ground. Keep the rite rare and costly, and let the brand of broken faith be a story hook, not an interrogation tool.

### The Pilgrim's Road

**Rank** | **Focus** | **Target** | **Range** | **Properties**
---|---|---|---|---
 5 | 10 | Legendary (16) | Touch | ritual (hours),  singular,  material cost (5000 coins)

**Effect** <br/> Through a night-long rite you consecrate the road ahead, anointing staff and sandal and naming the journey's end aloud before the gods, and the way opens itself to the faithful. The rite consumes 5000 coins worth of blessed oils and offerings whether or not it succeeds.<br/>Name a destination you know of and whose location you know, even if you have never walked the way there. On a success, you bless the journey for yourself and up to twelve willing travelers with you. The blessing holds until the company reaches the destination, forsakes the journey, or one moon passes, whichever comes first. While the blessing holds, apply the following effects:<br/>- The road reveals itself. The company always knows the truest way toward the destination, even through lands unknown to them, and cannot become lost.<br/>- The company travels with supernatural swiftness, tireless as the road itself. The group gains +2 progress on each day of travel, rolls to travel the blessed route gain +1 boon, and no traveler gains Fatigue from the journey itself.<br/>- The road wards its pilgrims from foul weather along the way, and harsh weather inflicts no Fatigue on them.<br/>- When a creature makes an attack against a blessed traveler on the road, the attacker must roll Spirit + Fortitude vs. your Resist or be dazed for a short duration.<br/>The road follows the living land and does not pierce it. It is no portal, and the journey is still made step by step.

> **GM framing note.** The Pilgrim's Road speeds and shields a journey. It does not remove it. Bandits, storms too great to ward, closed passes, and the ordinary dangers of the road all remain. The blessing makes a pilgrimage safer and swifter, never certain. Encounters can and should still happen on the blessed road.
